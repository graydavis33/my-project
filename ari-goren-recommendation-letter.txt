To Whom It May Concern,

I'm writing in support of Ari Goren and his short film project, "The Space Between." I first met Ari at Camp Mah-Kee-Nac, where I was a counselor and also the lead videographer in charge of producing weekly recap episodes for the kids to watch. This also meant I worked with a few campers who made short films and entered them in the film contest at the end of camp. Being able to see his art firsthand and his work ethic made me know he can create incredible work. For someone at his age, it is incredible to see the level he is at.

One thing that stood out to me the first time I read "The Space Between" is how well Ari was able to put us in the minds of the characters. You could read and understand the feelings of the characters, which made it so easy for me to relate. Yet, you still had to fill in the gaps of the story to figure out what's going on. As someone in the audience reading stories or watching films, I've always appreciated that because it kept me engaged with the plot.

Ari keeps the story very descriptive, especially in the small details. For example, "a smooth, oval stone. Gray with a white stripe running through it." This is an incredible detail and it emphasizes the separation between the two parents. The same is true of the gummy bears and the unsealed stack of business cards in Dan's pocket. Small physical objects holding the full emotional weight of the story. Those are the details a director shoots with real care.

He makes it easy for the reader to imagine themselves in the story by using the environment itself to do emotional work. Early in the train ride, he writes: "The landscape rolls by outside. Curving hills. Fields. Small towns that blur together." Pages later, as the train nears Bloomington: "The landscape has changed. Less rolling hills. Flatter. More industrial. Warehouses. Power lines." Same train, same boy pressed against the same window but the world outside has gone hard. I can't wait to see how he produces some of these shots on screen. Especially like the shots when James and his mother are perfectly aligned in the window.

I have full confidence in Ari Goren as a writer and as a filmmaker capable of bringing this script to the screen. With financial backing, this script will be very manageable in producing with few actors, locations, and props. "The Space Between" deserves the support it needs to be made. I am proud to see his passion for filmmaking has only grown since I first met him. At his age and the level of seriousness he puts into his craft, he is only going to improve at an exponential rate. I can't wait to watch him in his career and see how he becomes as a filmmaker and as a man.


Sincerely,
Gray Davis


Gray Davis graduated from the University of Georgia with an Entertainment and Media Studies degree at Grady College of Journalism and Mass Communications. He's a freelance videographer, editor, and the founder of Graydient Media. He spent nine months as the in-house videographer at a marketing agency, where he filmed and edited personal and business social media content across the agency's roster of clients, and currently works as a Creative Director for Sai Karra. Sai Karra is the co-founder of Trendify, which is an ad agency in New York City.
