# Street Talk Founder Interview — Question Bank

_Created: 2026-04-29. Cross-referenced with the School of Hard Knocks crash-course + playbook in this folder._

## Subject Context

- Founder of **Street Talk** — ad agency that produces unscripted street interviews as paid ads for brands
- **22 years old**
- **Syracuse dropout** — left to go all-in on this
- Started solo doing NYC street interviews, scaled by hiring a team
- Reportedly **~$500k/month** in revenue

## The Angle (Through-Line)

Meta-interview: a street-interview founder being street-interviewed. Two unique lenses:

1. **Founder origin** — 22, Syracuse dropout, $500k/mo. Classic SoHK wealth-reveal + age-anchor territory.
2. **The craft** — He runs the *productized* version of School of Hard Knocks. What he learned, stole, invented, or rejected from the format that built that genre.

The craft questions are the differentiator — every other interviewer will only ask about the money.

# FULL QUESTION BANK

## SECTION 1 — Cold Open / Hook (Pick ONE)

| # | Question | Why it works |
|---|----------|--------------|
| 1 | "How much did Street Talk make last month?" | SoHK Archetype A (direct wealth ask). Number on screen in second 1. |
| 2 | "You're 22 and you run the agency that's eating the street-interview market. How?" | Age anchor + provocative claim (Template 2 + 7). |
| 3 | "You dropped out of Syracuse to ask strangers questions on the street. Worth it?" | Contrast setup (Template 5). Sets up comeback arc. |
| 4 | "How many brands are paying you right now to film on the street?" | Number flashcard hook (Template 4). |

**Recommended opener: #2.**

## SECTION 2 — Origin Story (The Comeback Arc)

5. Walk me through the exact moment you decided to drop out of Syracuse. Where were you?
6. Was there one street interview — the first or second one you ever filmed — where you knew this was the thing?
7. What did your parents say when you told them? What do they say now?
8. Before you ever made a dollar from this — how broke did it get? Be specific.
9. What was the first brand that paid you, and what did they pay you?
10. What did you charge for that first deal that you'd laugh at today?
11. What's the moment you realized you weren't a kid with a camera anymore — you were running a business?

## SECTION 3 — The Craft (Cross-Reference with SoHK)

12. School of Hard Knocks is the OG of this format. What did you steal from them — and what did you decide to do completely differently?
13. SoHK's hook is almost always "How did you get rich?" What's your equivalent first sentence — the one that earns the next 15 seconds?
14. How do you decide in the first 3 seconds whether someone on the street is a yes or a no?
15. What's the highest hit-rate question you've ever asked a stranger? The one that always gets a usable answer?
16. What's a question that sounds great in theory but always tanks the interview?
17. SoHK shoots in a tight MCU and never opens with context. What's your shot/edit signature that someone watching could ID as a Street Talk reel before the logo lands?
18. How do you turn an unscripted answer into something a brand will actually pay to put their name on? Where's the line?
19. When a stranger says something gold but doesn't sign the release — what do you do?
20. What's the highest view count a Street Talk ad has ever pulled, and what was different about it?
21. Brands always want to over-script. How do you push back when a client asks for talking points?
22. You film New Yorkers — the most media-trained, jaded interview pool in the country. How do you get them off-script?

## SECTION 4 — The Business (Agency Mechanics)

23. Walk me through how a brand actually books Street Talk. What's the call, the price, the deliverable?
24. What's the average deal size now vs. a year ago?
25. What does a $500k month actually look like — how many clients, how many shoots, how many cuts?
26. What was the first hire you made, and what did that unlock?
27. How big is the team today, and what's the org chart look like?
28. What's the hardest role to hire for — interviewers, editors, or producers?
29. What's the one thing in the business that still only you can do?
30. What's your margin look like? Is this an actually profitable business or a topline business?
31. What's the biggest deal you've turned down — and why?
32. Where does Street Talk go next? Sequels, sub-brands, owned IP, an in-house creator roster?

## SECTION 5 — Failure / Friction

33. What's a campaign that flopped — that you got paid for but you knew was bad?
34. Have you ever filmed something that got the brand in trouble? What happened?
35. What's the closest you've come to losing the company?
36. Who's the competitor you actually respect — and what do they do better than you?
37. What's a hire you made that didn't work out, and what did it teach you about hiring?

## SECTION 6 — Wealth Reveal (Mid-Reel, Not Opener)

38. How much money have you personally taken home in the last 12 months — give me a range?
39. At what age did you realize you were actually wealthy?
40. More than $1M in the bank? More than $5M? More than $10M? *(ascending ladder)*
41. Most you've ever made in a single deal?
42. What was the first thing you bought that felt insane?
43. What's something you can afford now that 19-year-old you would lose his mind over?

## SECTION 7 — Mentorship / Identity (Quotables)

44. What's the one thing you'd tell a 19-year-old at Syracuse right now who's thinking about doing what you did?
45. What does most of your peer group not understand about what running this actually takes?
46. What's a belief about success you used to have that you no longer believe?
47. What separates the founders who scale past $1M/mo from the ones who plateau at $50k?
48. What do you tell yourself on the days when nothing's working?

## SECTION 8 — Closer (Hold Freeze Frame)

49. "If Street Talk didn't exist, what would you be doing right now?"
50. "Finish the sentence: most 22-year-olds will never be wealthy because ___"
51. "In one sentence — what is Street Talk actually selling?"

# TEAM QUESTIONS (Side Interviews)

Get one-on-one, not as a group. Pick 4–6 per team member.

1. How did you meet [him], and what made you decide to bet on this?
2. What's [his] unfair advantage — the thing he does that other founders you've worked with don't?
3. What's a Street Talk shoot day actually like? Walk me through it.
4. Most disagreements you've ever had with him — what's it usually about?
5. What's the most surprising thing about working for a 22-year-old?
6. What's something he's gotten really right that nobody outside the company sees?
7. What's something he's still figuring out?
8. If you had to bet on one founder in your network, would it be him? Why?
9. Best street interview the team has ever shot — and what made it work?
10. What's the wildest thing that's happened on a shoot?
11. Who picks the questions — him, the producer, or the brand? How does that fight go?
12. What does a "no, scrap that, reshoot" decision sound like inside the office?
