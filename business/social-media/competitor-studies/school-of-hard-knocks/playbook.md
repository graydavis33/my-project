## SECTION 1: Pre-Shoot Checklist

### 📍 Location Scouting

- [ ] **Is there a visible status signal in frame?** (luxury car, skyline, office lobby, boardroom, construction site, private club exterior) — SOHK's Archetype C hooks only work if the asset is visible in the first 3 seconds
- [ ] **Can you film here without interruption for 8–12 minutes?** If someone says *"you can't film here"* — that's actually usable (Rank #16 formula). Note it but have a backup
- [ ] **Is natural light hitting Sai's face?** Window light preferred. No overhead fluorescents. If outdoors, position Sai with sun at his back or at 45° — not directly in eyes
- [ ] **Is the background layered/textured?** Brick, glass, plants, city — anything with depth. Flat white walls test poorly
- [ ] **Is there ambient noise that competes with voice?** Test audio before positioning Sai. AC hum, HVAC, traffic = bad. Light ambience = fine
- [ ] **Can you get a clean wide, medium, and close-up from this location?** Walk the angles before Sai arrives

**Best location tiers for Sai's content:**
1. Exterior of a building Sai owns, invested in, or is closing on — instant status signal
2. Lobby or entrance of a high-end office — implies scale
3. Walking shot on a city block — dynamic, energetic, CEO-on-the-move energy
4. Seated across a desk or conference table — authority frame for longer content
5. Car interior (passenger seat of luxury vehicle) — Archetype C adjacent

---

### 🎥 Equipment Checklist

- [ ] **Primary camera** charged to 100%, memory card cleared
- [ ] **Lens:** 35mm or 50mm equivalent — wide enough for environment context, tight enough for face
- [ ] **Lav mic** clipped and tested on Sai — run a 10-second audio check before every setup
- [ ] **Backup audio:** phone recording in shirt pocket as failsafe
- [ ] **Stabilizer/gimbal** charged if doing walking shots
- [ ] **ND filter** if shooting outdoors (prevents blown highlights)
- [ ] **Extra battery** in pocket
- [ ] **Phone mounted** for B-roll capture of environment details (hands, objects, signage)
- [ ] **Shot list open** (Section 2 of this doc) — review before rolling

---

### 🎬 Framing & Camera Settings

- [ ] **Aspect ratio: 9:16** — shoot vertical or crop plan confirmed
- [ ] **Frame rate: 60fps** minimum for all footage — allows slow-down in edit without stutter
- [ ] **Resolution: 4K** — gives reframing flexibility in post
- [ ] **Auto-focus: ON** for walking/movement shots — manual only for locked setups
- [ ] **Exposure:** slightly underexpose rather than blow highlights — easier to recover in post

---

### 👔 Sai's Wardrobe Brief (tell him before he arrives)

- [ ] **Solid colors preferred** — patterns create visual noise in compressed vertical video
- [ ] **Dark or jewel tones** (navy, charcoal, forest green, burgundy) read as authority on screen
- [ ] **No logos on clothing** unless intentional (his own brand, relevant signaling)
- [ ] **Collar or structured top** if possible — open collar reads casual-CEO, full suit reads formal, hoodie reads founder — match to content topic
- [ ] **Watch visible** if he wears one — it's a passive status signal
- [ ] **No bright white shirts** if shooting outdoors — will blow in sunlight

---

### 🧠 Subject Prep (2 minutes with Sai before rolling)

Do not skip this. It changes his energy completely.

- [ ] Tell Sai the **single question you're opening with** — not all of them, just the first
- [ ] Tell him **not to look at the camera** unless it's a confessional/talking-head setup — eyes on you (Gray), not lens
- [ ] Tell him to **answer in complete sentences** — not "yeah" but "Yeah, I closed my first deal at 23"
- [ ] Give him **one emotional target**: "Tell this story like you're telling it to a friend at a bar, not a journalist"
- [ ] Remind him: **if he fumbles an answer, just pause and restart** — you'll cut it. No need to announce mistakes
- [ ] If doing a **walk-and-talk**: rehearse the route once before rolling so he's not thinking about where to walk

## SECTION 2: Shot List

Use this sequence for every short-form reel. Adapt for long-form.

### Shot 1: The Hook Frame
**Framing:** Medium close-up (MCU) — shoulders to top of head, subject slightly off-center
**When to use:** Opening shot of every reel
**Why:** 38 of 43 SOHK reels open on MCU or tighter. The face must be visible and recognizable within the first frame. Viewer makes a stay/scroll decision in under 1 second — face = human connection = stay
**Execution:** Frame Sai with one-third of frame showing environment context. Ask the opening hook question while rolling. Capture his initial reaction — that micro-expression before he answers is often the best frame

---

### Shot 2: The Response Frame
**Framing:** Same MCU or slight push to close-up (CU — chin to forehead)
**When to use:** Sai's first 15–30 seconds of answer
**Why:** SOHK cuts tighter during emotional peaks. When the answer is big ("we closed a $40M deal"), push to CU so his face fills the frame. The number lands harder
**Execution:** If static setup, push in digitally in post. If handheld, slowly drift 2–3 inches closer during a strong answer

---

### Shot 3: The Walk-and-Talk
**Framing:** Shooting from slightly below eye level, walking backward or alongside
**When to use:** Transition between topics, or as an alternative opening
**Why:** Movement creates momentum. SOHK uses walking shots in ~20/43 reels to sustain energy through mid-reel. Static reels lose viewers faster after 20 seconds
**Execution:** Gray walks backward, Sai walks toward or alongside. Keep frame loose enough to show body language — hands gesturing = energy = watch time

---

### Shot 4: The Object/Asset Insert
**Framing:** Tight detail shot — license plate, watch face, building signage, handshake, contract, car door
**When to use:** Whenever Sai references a physical thing (a car he bought, a building he owns, a check he wrote)
**Why:** Archetype C hooks (Rolls-Royce, McLaren, yacht) are SOHK's highest-performing reel type. The object does narrative work without words. For Sai, this means filming the thing he's talking about, not just him talking about it
**Execution:** Capture these on your phone as B-roll mid-conversation. Even 3–4 seconds of a detail shot is enough

---

### Shot 5: The Reaction Frame
**Framing:** CU or ECU (extreme close-up — eyes and nose only)
**When to use:** Guest reactions, surprise moments, Sai's emotional response to a bold question
**Why:** SOHK's most-shared moments are faces reacting, not people explaining. The pause after a big number. The laugh after an uncomfortable question. Film the face, not just the answer
**Execution:** If interviewing a guest alongside Sai, cut to guest's face during Sai's answer. If solo Sai, push to ECU when he pauses before answering

---

### Shot 6: The Wide Establishing
**Framing:** Full environment — Sai small in frame, location dominant
**When to use:** Opening 2–3 seconds if location IS the hook (building exterior, luxury setting)
**Why:** Archetype C requires the asset to be visible before the question is asked. If Sai is standing next to a status signal, open wide so viewer sees both
**Execution:** This shot often works best as a 2-second cut before the MCU hook — location → face → question

## SECTION 5: Edit Recipe

### Cut Cadence
- **Target:** 1 cut every 2–3 seconds during hook (first 15 seconds)
- **Target:** 1 cut every 4–5 seconds during content body (seconds 15–45)
- **Target:** 1 cut every 3–4 seconds during outro (last 10 seconds)
- **Rule:** Never let a static shot run longer than 6 seconds without a cut, B-roll insert, or zoom push
- **Jump cuts:** Yes — preferred over padding. Cut every filler word ("um," "like," "you know"). SOHK reels are dense and tight
- **Pace escalation:** Each act should feel slightly faster than the last — hook is quick, body settles slightly, outro re-accelerates

---

### Caption Style
- **Font:** Bold, sans-serif — Impact, Arial Black, or platform native bold
- **Size:** Large enough to read without zooming — test on your phone at arm's length
- **Position:** Lower third (bottom 25% of frame) — never cover eyes or mouth
- **Color:** White text, black drop shadow or outline — readable on any background
- **Word count per frame:** 3–6 words maximum
- **Timing:** Captions sync to speech — not floating independently
- **Emphasis:** Key words (numbers, names, action words) in a second color — yellow or red
- **ALL CAPS** for emphasis words: "I made $40 MILLION in one year"
- **No full sentences in captions** — fragment the speech into punchy visual beats

---

### B-Roll Integration Rules
- **Insert B-roll within the first 8 seconds** if location isn't visually interesting
- **B-roll triggers:** Any time Sai says a noun that can be shown (a car, a building, a number, a city, a product)
- **B-roll duration:** 2–4 seconds per clip — never longer
- **B-roll types to capture on every shoot:**
  - [ ] Sai's hands (signing, gesturing, pointing at something)
  - [ ] Whatever is visible in the environment (skyline, office details, cars)
  - [ ] Sai walking away from camera — 5 seconds minimum
  - [ ] Any objects referenced in conversation — film them before Sai leaves
  - [ ] Screen of a laptop or phone if numbers/data are discussed (blur sensitive info as needed)
- **Never use B-roll to hide bad audio** — fix audio first, then add B-roll on top

---

### Sound Design
- **Music:** Subtle instrumental bed under dialogue — low enough that voice is primary
- **Genre:** Lo-fi hip-hop, light trap, or cinematic ambient — nothing with lyrics
- **Music enters:** At cut 1 or at the first B-roll insert
- **SFX:** Whoosh on fast cuts, subtle cash register or notification sound on number reveals (optional — use sparingly)
- **Audio level:** Dialogue at -6db, music at -18db to -20db

## SECTION 6: Outro Templates

### Outro Type 1: The Callback Close
Bring back the opening hook and close the loop.

> *"So that's how [he went from X to Y]. And it all started with [callback to opening statement]."*

**Example:** Open with "How did you make your first million?" → Close with "And that one deal — the one he almost walked away from — that's the million that started everything."

---

### Outro Type 2: The Quotable Drop
End on Sai's most quotable line from the conversation. Let it sit for 1–2 seconds with no music, no cut.

> *Subject delivers line. Hard cut to black or freeze frame. On-screen text echoes the line.*

**Example from SOHK data (Rank #5 — Jesse Itzler):** *"You've gotta do something every day that makes you uncomfortable."* — delivered, then held.

**Example for Sai:** "The deal doesn't close in the meeting. It closes in every conversation you had before the meeting." — hold on his face, freeze, caption reinforces it.

---

### Outro Type 3: The Tease Forward
End by setting up the next piece of content or creating an unanswered question.

> *"But that's only half the story. The deal he almost lost — that's next."*

**Use for:** Multi-part content, series, or when a story runs long and needs a second reel

---

### Outro Type 4: The Direct CTA (LinkedIn / Long-Form Specific)
Sai speaks directly to the lens for the last 5 seconds.

> *"If you're building something right now — [specific advice statement]. Follow for more."*

**Example for Sai:** "If you're trying to raise your first round and no one's calling you back — your deck isn't the problem. Your story is. Follow, I'll show you how to fix it."

---

### Outro Type 5: The Numbers Recap
Flash the key numbers from the reel as text on screen over a freeze of Sai.

> *Age at first deal: 19. First portfolio: $2M. Today: $__M. How: [one-line summary]*

**Use for:** Wealth-reveal reels where the numbers were the content
