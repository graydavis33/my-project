## Theme of the week

**Surface:** systems beat stress — "every stress in my life can be solved with a system" (C2656).
**Underneath:** it's all about people — the company is the belonging/community Sai never had (C2669).

The episode uses *systems* as the spine and lands the *why* on *people*.

### Title (theme-only — NOT LOCKED, Gray to choose)
Working candidates:
- **"Systems Over Stress"** — the literal lesson
- **"It's All People"** — the emotional core
- **"Structure Sets You Free"** — the counterintuitive idea

## Logline

The week Sai's big launch slips again — so instead of forcing it, he has an epiphany (every
stress is just a missing system) and spends the week building the machine: splitting his team,
delegating his own job, rebuilding the SOPs. Underneath the systems is the real reason he's
doing any of it — the company is the community he never had.

an internal one: the quiet stress of a founder who measures himself by daily progress, and the
relief of realizing the answer is structure, not grinding harder.

## ⭐ THE INTERVIEW SCRIPT (the driver — film this sit-down)

Controlled cinematic setup: same spot, nice light, clean-but-real background, A-cam tight +
B-cam wide. Looser "talking to a friend" delivery. ★ = must-have (the spine won't work without
it). Each block maps to a cut-in point above.

### Block A — The intention / the delay (feeds Week-Ahead + season thread)
- ★ Last week the plan was to finally turn the ads back on and launch the site. What actually
  happened — where does the launch stand right now?
- Why did it slip, and how do you feel about it slipping again?
- Walk me through what you decided to focus on instead this week.

### Block B — The low point / the stress (feeds Act 1 — the mind-dump)
- ★ There's a moment this week where you say you tie your self-worth to productivity. Unpack that.
- What does a "bad day" actually do to your head as a founder?
- You traced it back to a Knicks loss and skipping your routine. Why does the routine matter that
  much to you?

### Block C — The turn / the epiphany (feeds The Theme + Act 2)
- ★ You had an epiphany: "every stress in my life can be solved with a system." Say that again and
  explain what clicked.
- ★ You said structure is what frees your creativity. What do you mean — aren't those opposites?
- You'd lost sight of systems for a couple weeks. What pulled you back?

### Block D — The machine (feeds Act 2 — the unglamorous work)
- Walk me through what you actually built this week — the editor workflow, the dashboards, the SOPs.
- ★ You handed off the onboarding call you used to run yourself. Why was that scary, and why does
  delegating matter so much right now?
- Why is an agency uniquely hard to build? (the "it's all people" / role-change example)

### Block E — The why (feeds Act 3 — emotional core)
- ★ You said building an agency is "just people," and that you love it. Where does that come from?
- ★ Tell me about UGA — the semester of college, not having friends because all you did was work.
- ★ The office you dream of — how is it the social life you never had?
- You surprised Gray with a laptop this week. Why was that the instinct? (culture beat — optional)
- ★ You keep making these raw videos with nothing to sell. Why?

### Block F — The cliffhanger (feeds the closer / season thread)
- ★ The launch still hasn't happened. What has to be true for it to finally go live?
- What's the one thing you're most afraid of as you try to grow?

### Block G — Connective lines (film these explicitly, short and clean)
- Cold-open reset: "Last week the plan was to launch. That's not what happened."
- Mid-doc rehook: "All of this — it's not about org charts. It's about people."
- Closer line: a single quotable wisdom-drop for the outro card.
