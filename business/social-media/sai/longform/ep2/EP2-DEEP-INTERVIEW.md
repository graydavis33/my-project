# EP2 — Deep Interview Questions (grounded in the real week)

_Created 2026-06-17. Same elite open-ended format as
`../../interviews/2026-06-17-elite-interview-questions.md`, but every question is anchored to
something Sai actually lived or said in EP2's footage (June 6–12), then elevated into a question
he can run with for days._

These are a **different lens** than the doc-narrative Block A–G questions in
`EP2-INTERVIEW-QUESTIONS.md`. Those serve the episode's story spine; these are built to be
rewatchable, philosophical, and to spin off multiple short-form cuts each. Source clip noted on
each.

---

**1. The Missing System** _(C2656 — the epiphany)_
"You had an epiphany this week — that every stress in your life right now is really just a system
you haven't built yet. So let me push on that: is stress actually information? A signal pointing
straight at the thing that's broken? And if that's true — what is the stress you feel right now
trying to tell you?"
→ Threads: Is *all* stress solvable, or is some just the price of caring? The stress that has no
system answer. How he tells the two apart.

**2. The Productivity Trap** _(C2655 — the confession)_
"You said something most founders won't admit out loud — that you tie your sense of worth to
whether you moved the business forward that day. Where did that wiring come from? And have you
ever genuinely felt like *enough* on a day you did nothing?"
→ Threads: When it started. Whether he'd give it up if he could. The line between drive and
self-punishment. What he says to himself on a zero day.

**3. Structure and the Muse** _(C2656 — "structure frees creativity")_
"You said something that sounds like a flat contradiction — that building rigid structure is what
sets your creativity free. Most people think a system is where ideas go to die. So make the case:
why does the cage make the bird sing louder?"
→ Threads: An idea that only existed *because* of a constraint. A time structure killed the magic.
Where the line sits.

**4. Firing Yourself** _(C2643 — handing off the onboarding call)_
"This week you watched someone else run an onboarding call — a job you used to do, and probably
still do best. There's something almost painful about handing off the thing you're great at. What
does it actually feel like to make yourself unnecessary on purpose?"
→ Threads: The fear of being replaceable vs the *goal* of being replaceable. What he still refuses
to let go of. How you trust someone with your baby.

**5. A Company Is Just People** _(C2669 — "it's just people")_
"You said building an agency is uniquely brutal because in the end it's just people — you can't
write a process that fully captures a human being. So if the whole thing comes down to people,
what are you really in the business of: marketing, or human behavior?"
→ Threads: The role-change SOP nightmare. Why he loves it anyway. The hardest people-problem he's
ever solved. Whether every great founder is secretly just great at people.

**6. The Belonging You're Building** _(C2669 — the UGA story)_
"You told a story that stuck with me — that in college you had no friends because all you did was
work, and what you really wanted was people who loved building as much as you. So be honest: are
you building a company, or are you building the belonging you never had?"
→ Threads: The loneliness of the obsessed. Is the dream office about success or connection? What
he'd say to that kid at UGA. The risk of needing your company to love you back.

**7. Nothing to Sell** _(C2685 — the raw-content ethos + the trillionaire bit)_
"You keep making these raw videos — no hook tricks, no pitch, nothing to sell. In an attention
economy built on the exact opposite, that's almost irrational. Why document the climb before
you've reached the top — and what will you have that the people who waited to post don't?"
→ Threads: The "one day I'll be a trillionaire and say *I showed you how*" bet. Building in public
vs hiding until you win. What he wants this footage to mean in 10 years. Is it confidence, or a
hedge?

**8. The Thing You Keep Not Doing** _(C2614 — the launch slips again)_
"For two weeks straight the plan has been to go live — turn the ads on, launch the site — and for
two weeks it's slipped. At some point a delay stops being logistics and becomes something else.
What's really underneath the thing you keep putting off?"
→ Threads: Perfectionism, fear, or honest priority? What going live actually risks. The story he
tells himself about why it's "not time yet." What finally flips it.

**9. The Highest-Leverage Point** _(C2668 — formats)_
"You said the biggest lever on creative — above almost everything — is the *format* you test, not
the individual video. Most people pour everything into the wrong layer. Where else in business do
people obsess over the visible thing while the real leverage sits one level up, invisible?"
→ Threads: Formats as the actual product. How he finds leverage points. The thing he stopped
caring about that everyone else still optimizes. Leverage in hiring, money, attention.

**10. One Game, One Whole Day** _(C2655 — Knicks loss → broken routine)_
"You traced an entire wasted, stressful day back to one thing — staying up for a Knicks game,
getting too fired up, skipping your night routine. That's a lot of power to hand one small input.
Do you think high performers are actually *more* fragile than people realize — that the machine
only runs if every little thing is protected?"
→ Threads: The non-negotiables. What happens when one domino falls. Discipline as self-respect,
not self-control. Is the routine actually the real product?
