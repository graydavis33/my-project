## 1. 🚩 THE "~2 HOURS A WEEK" VERDICT — do NOT put this number in the video

**The "~2 hours a week talking to customers" framing is NOT sourceable.** Across all 18 sources and 76 extracted claims, the word "hour" appears **only in our own research question** — no source attributes any "2 hours a week" (or any quantified time) to Bezos for customer contact.

**What's actually real:** Bezos's customer connection is **ritual + habit, not a clocked time block** — reading customer emails, the "?" forwards, the empty chair, working backwards from the customer. None of it is "2 hours a week."

**Recommendation for the wheel/file card:** reframe the tactic away from a fake number. Options:
- *"Stay obsessively close to your customers — read their complaints yourself and act on them."*
- *"Make the customer the most important person in every room."*
- *"When the data and the customer disagree, believe the customer."*

Any of these is true and citable. "2 hours a week" is not — and on a video whose whole hook is *copying a billionaire's real tactic*, a made-up number is the one thing that can get you called out.

## 2. HOW he actually does it — the real, sourced mechanics

### a) The one-character "?" email ⭐ (the most video-ready mechanic)
Bezos keeps a public address, **jeff@amazon.com**, reads most of what comes in, and forwards customer complaints to the responsible executive with a single character: **"?"**.
> **Bezos (verbatim):** *"I see most of those emails. I see them and I forward them to the executives in charge of the area with a question mark… It's shorthand. 'Can you look into this? Why is this happening?'"*
— 2018 George W. Bush Center interview, via CNBC (2018-05-07), Inc, Yahoo Finance. **Solid.**
The "?" became famous internally as a mini-emergency — execs scramble to root-cause the issue before reporting back.

### b) The empty chair
Bezos left an **empty chair** at the conference table and told the room it was occupied by *"the most important person in the room"* — the customer.
— Inc (Koetsier), Entrepreneur, Benzinga. **Solid** (widely reported; secondary sources).

### c) "Start with the customer and work backwards"
Every Amazon project begins with a mock **press release + FAQ** written from the customer's POV *before* anything is built.
— Amazon Leadership Principles (amazon.jobs, primary). **Solid.**

### d) Anecdotes over metrics
> **Bezos (verbatim):** *"When the data and the anecdotes disagree, the anecdotes are usually right… It doesn't mean you just slavishly follow the anecdotes. It means you go examine the data,"* because the data is usually being measured wrong.
— 2016 shareholder letter (aboutamazon.com, primary). **Solid.**

## 3. WHY he does it — customer obsession, in his own words

**Amazon's #1 Leadership Principle is "Customer Obsession":**
> *"Leaders start with the customer and work backwards. They work vigorously to earn and keep customer trust. Although leaders pay attention to competitors, they obsess over customers."*
— amazon.jobs (primary). **Solid.**

**Why customers over competitors:**
> *"If you're competitor-focused, you have to wait until there is a competitor doing something. Being customer-focused allows you to be more pioneering."*
— widely documented Bezos quote (aligns with the 2016 letter). **Solid in substance** (one aggregator source was low-quality; the idea is straight from his letters).

**The 2016 letter — the philosophy in one line:**
> *"There are many ways to center a business. You can be competitor focused, you can be product focused… But in my view, obsessive customer focus is by far the most protective of Day 1 vitality."*
— 2016 shareholder letter (primary). **Solid.**

**The engine — "divinely discontent" customers (2017 letter):**
Bezos calls customers *"divinely discontent"* — their expectations are never static, always rising; *"yesterday's wow quickly becomes today's ordinary."* That permanent dissatisfaction is what forces Amazon to keep improving.
— 2017 shareholder letter, via Founders Tribune. **Solid in substance.**

**1997/1998 letters (the origin):**
Customer obsession was there from day one — the 1997 letter has an *"Obsess Over Customers"* section; the 1998 letter states the intent to *"build the world's most customer-centric company… customers are the ones to whom we owe a great obligation."* **Solid** (primary).
