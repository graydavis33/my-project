## The 5 questions (in ask order)

1. When's the last time you bit your tongue in a meeting here?
   - Follow-up: What was it?
   - Why it glues: everyone has one, and the viewer wonders if they'll actually say it. Proves the filter exists through a real story instead of asking about honesty in the abstract. (Replaced the "easier if I wasn't in the room" opener — Gray cut it 2026-07-06.)

2. If you ran Trendify tomorrow, what's the first thing you'd change?
   - Follow-up: Could we fix that by Friday if I just said yes?
   - Why it glues: role reversal forces a specific; the follow-up turns it into a shippable change (feeds success bar 3).

3. What do I do that makes your job harder?
   - Follow-up: When's the last time a decision got made without asking the person doing the work?
   - Why it glues: employee critiquing the boss to his face; the pause before the answer is content. Follow-up escalates from Sai's habits to the company's habits.

4. When's the last time you thought about leaving?
   - Follow-up: What kept you?
   - Why it glues: highest personal stakes; time-anchored so it forces a real story. "What kept you" usually resolves warm — fits the no-teardown brand rule.

5. The belief echo — PLAYBACK MOMENT. Play them the Beat 5 clip of Sai stating his belief ("everyone here wants this as much as I do"), film their face while they watch. Then:
   How true is that?
   - Follow-up: silence. Let them fill it.
   - Why it glues: the master loop said out loud — the reaction shot while the clip plays is the most watchable moment in the episode. Must go last.

## The standing closer (every conversation)

Last one. Name one thing slowing you down that we could fix by Friday — if I said yes right now.

- Five conversations = five yes-moments. The "ship 3 changes before the video ends" checklist fills itself on camera.
