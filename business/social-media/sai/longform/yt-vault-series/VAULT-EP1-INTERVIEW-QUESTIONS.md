# The Vault — Episode 1 Interview Questions
### "I Let a Wheel Decide Which Billionaire I Copy"

Built from Gray + Sai's 2026-06-26 refined concept. The A-roll sit-down is the **spine** — Sai's answers are the narration; the live scenes + B-roll + VFX cut in under them. ★ = must-have (the story breaks without it). Film it cinematic, raw, "talking to a friend" — Netflix-doc, not a tutorial. Heavy graphics/VFX live mostly in the first 30–60s hook + the draw + context beats; the body stays raw and human.

**Voice rule (important):** Sai is *not* the expert here. Every answer is "I'm as guilty of this as anyone / still figuring it out," never "here's how you should run your team." The whole hook is a confession, not a lecture.

## Block A — The Confession (the relatable thesis)
Feeds slots 1. This is the emotional hook — make it honest, not cute.
- ★ Be real with me — how much business content do you actually consume in a week? Books, clips, podcasts.
- ★ Of everything you've learned from founders you admire, how much have you *actually implemented*? Say the real number.
- Why do you think that is — why is it so easy to learn this stuff and so hard to do it?
- Does it ever bug you? Watching all these people and not changing anything?

## Block B — What This Is (the vault concept)
Feeds the rules beat. The viewer needs to get the format fast and trust the rule.
- ★ In your own words — what is the Vault? Walk me through what's actually in it.
- Why two wheels? Why hand the decision to chance instead of just picking a good tactic?
- ★ What's the rule you're making for yourself with this — and why does it matter that you can't back out?

## Block C — The Draw (reaction to the pick)
Feeds slot 3. Film this raw, in the moment of the spin.
- ★ It just landed on Bezos and *(the tactic)*. First honest reaction?
- Is this one you'd have picked yourself — or one you'd have avoided?
- What's the part of you that wants to re-spin right now? (then: why aren't you?)

## Block D — The Twist: Why Team, Not Customers ★
Feeds slot 5 — the heart of the why. Pull this all the way.
- ★ Bezos did this with *customers*. You're adapting it to your *team*. Walk me to that decision — why them, why now?
- ★ Right now, what's the real lever in Trendify — new clients, or the people you already have? Say it straight.
- What do you actually not know about your own team that this might surface?
- What are you a little afraid you'll hear in these conversations?

## Block E — The Setup (fairness, the 5, the plan)
Feeds slots 7–8. Show the unglamorous prep honestly.
- How did you decide who the 5 are? Why keep it fair / random instead of just picking favorites?
- ★ What did you actually want to get out of each conversation — what were you listening for?
- How'd you build the agenda? Did how Bezos listened change how you set these up?

## Block F — The Conversations ★
Feeds slot 9. Film Sai's real reactions close to the moment — right after each 1-on-1.
- ★ Which conversation surprised you the most, and why?
- What's something one of them said that you didn't expect?
- Was there a moment you realized you'd been missing something obvious? What was it?
- How did it feel to actually sit and *listen* for two hours instead of doing?

## Block G — Takeaways + Implementation
Feeds slots 11–12. The turn from listening to doing — the whole point of the format.
- ★ By the end, what were the tangible things — the 3 to 5 you could actually act on?
- ★ Which ones are you implementing, who's involved, and what does success look like for each?
- What was hard about actually shipping these instead of just nodding and moving on?
- Be honest — was there a takeaway you heard and *still* almost didn't act on?

## Block H — The Results + Meaning ★
Feeds slots 13–15. The payoff and the wisdom-drop that makes it a story, not a case study.
- ★ What actually moved after you implemented? (film any real result raw — a change, a number, a moment)
- ★ Did forcing yourself to implement work? What did it teach you that just *learning* it never did?
- You keep building this in public. Why let people watch you possibly fumble a billionaire's tactic?
- ★ Quotable closer line for the outro card.
- ★ Cliffhanger for the next spin: one line that makes them want the next episode.
