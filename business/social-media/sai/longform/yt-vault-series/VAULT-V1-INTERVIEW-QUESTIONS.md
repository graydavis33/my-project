## Block A — The Old Wound (the past ad failure)
Feeds slots 2–3 (the doubt + failure montage). This is the emotional low point that makes the whole bet matter.
- ★ The first time you ran ads — what happened? Take me back to it.
- ★ You said when the ads stopped working, you had people to pay and you "basically went under." How bad did it actually get?
- What did that failure do to *you* — not the business, you personally?
- Why did you walk away from ads back then? What did you tell yourself?

## Block B — What This Even Is (context)
Feeds slot 5. The viewer needs to feel the agency is real and earned before they care about the bet.
- ★ You started this at 15. What is the agency, in your own words — who do you make ads for?
- What's changed in the last couple years — you said you "focused on the product." What does that mean?
- ★ Why is *now* the moment you finally turn ads back on?

## Block C — The Paper on the Wall (the real why) ★
Feeds slot 6 — the heart of the video. This is the cryptic thread from the memo. Pull it all the way.
- ★ You said you "pulled a paper off the wall" before deciding to run ads again. What was on that paper? Walk me to that exact moment.
- ★ Right now, how much of your cash flow runs through just a few clients? What happens to you if one of them leaves?
- ★ Is this bet really about growth — or about finally controlling your own business? Say it straight.
- What are you actually afraid of if this doesn't work this time?

## Block D — The Grind (the obstacles)
Feeds slots 7–8 (recording ads, the website realization). Show the unglamorous work.
- Walk me through deciding what these new ads had to be — why did they need to look different from everything out there?
- ★ What was the problem you hit while recording the ads, and how'd you get past it?
- ★ You realized your website was "trash" and had to rebuild it. What made you stop and fix that first?
- What was going through your head doing all this with no guarantee it pays off?

## Block E — The Bet Lands (the wait + payoff)
Feeds slots 9–10. Film Sai's real reactions close to the moment — nerves before, the call after.
- ★ The day the ads went live — how were you feeling? (film this raw, day-of)
- What was it like watching the dashboard those first days, waiting for anything to move?
- ★ When the first real company booked a call — what did that feel like in the moment?
- (After the sales call) Did it work? What does this first win actually mean?

## Block F — The Meaning (the why under the why)
Feeds slot 11. The wisdom-drop that makes it a story, not a case study.
- ★ You keep building this in public with nothing to sell. Why let people watch you possibly fail?
- What does proving you can sign clients on your own terms give you that money alone doesn't?
- If a 15-year-old version of you saw this, what would he think?

## Block G — Connective Lines (film explicitly, short + clean)
Feeds the cold open, mid-doc rehooks, and the Part 2 tease.
- ★ Cold-open line (under the vault VFX): "Everything in here is worth over a hundred billion dollars. Today I'm betting my own company on one of them."
- ★ Mid-doc rehook (~halfway): one line that re-stakes the bet — "If this doesn't work, I'm right back where I started."
- ★ Cliffhanger: "Thirty days. A billionaire's tactic. The only question left — did it actually work?"
- One quotable closer line for the outro card.

## Live decisions only you + Sai can make (flagging, not filling)

1. **The entrepreneur** — tactic is locked ("run ads"); the *who* is open. Needs a research pass to pick the most thumbnail-worthy founder whose ad story maps to Sai's (Bezos/Amazon is the placeholder).

3. **The outcome is a real future event** — the payoff (slot 10) depends on Sai *actually* signing a client in the 30 days. Decide: do we film first and let the story resolve honestly, or only publish if it lands?
4. **Length** — you said 5–20 min, no target. The slot map works at any length; more interview depth = longer.
