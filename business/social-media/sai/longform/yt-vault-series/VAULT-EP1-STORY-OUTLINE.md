# PART A — THE VAULT FORMAT (repeatable across episodes)

## A1. The format in one sentence
> **Every episode, a wheel randomly assigns Sai one exact tactic from a legendary founder — and he has to actually implement it in his real company, on camera, no cherry-picking.**

The randomness is the engine. It's what separates this from every "5 lessons from Bezos" video on the internet: he doesn't get to pick the easy one. The vault picks, he obeys.

## A2. The mechanic (what's physically on set)
- **The Vault** — a real cabinet/box of files, one per entrepreneur. Each file: a photo, a short bio, and their **top 10 business tactics** (numbered 1–10).
- **Wheel 1 — Faces:** the entrepreneurs.
- **Wheel 2 — Numbers:** 1–10, maps to the tactic on that person's list.
- **The Rule (say it out loud every episode):** whatever it lands on gets implemented. No re-spins, no swaps. This *contract* is what makes the audience trust it.

## A3. The repeatable episode skeleton
Every episode runs the same spine — only the founder, the tactic, and the implementation change:
1. **Cold open + the confession** — the relatable thesis ("I learn constantly and never implement").
2. **The draw** — spin both wheels, reveal the founder + tactic.
3. **Context** — a tight, graphics-driven explainer of how that founder actually used the tactic.
4. **The adaptation** — how it maps onto Trendify *right now* (the real "why this matters to me").
5. **The success bar** — 2–3 concrete conditions that decide if the episode "worked" (the checklist).
6. **The implementation journey** — the real work, filmed across the week.
7. **The takeaways** — the tangible things pulled out of it.
8. **The results + meaning** — did it move anything, and what forced-implementation taught him.
9. **Closer + cliffhanger** — quotable line + tease the next spin.

## A4. The retention engine (how the Addiction Loop runs it)
- **Master loop (opens in the hook, closes at results):** *"Will copying a billionaire's exact move actually change Sai's business — or is this just another thing he never really uses?"*
- **Two reliable head fakes every episode:** (1) the **draw** itself (you don't know what it'll land on — prediction error), and (2) the **adaptation twist** (the tactic doesn't fit cleanly, so he has to bend it — e.g. customers → team).
- **The success bar = a nested checklist** the viewer tracks like a progress bar.
- **Re-hooks at each step transition** using Want-But-Until connective tissue, never a flat "and then."

## A5. Reusable production checklist (per episode)
- [ ] Pre-lock the founder + exact tactic (so the week can be prepped).
- [ ] Build/refresh the physical vault file for that founder (photo, bio, top 10).
- [ ] Decide how the wheel "lands" on the locked pick (gaffed wheel vs. edit — see open decisions).
- [ ] Script + film the hook + draw sequence first.
- [ ] Film the implementation journey live across the week (horizontal B-roll).
- [ ] Capture the real result, even if it lands after the week.
- [ ] Script + film the A-roll sit-down interview LAST (it's the narration spine).
- [ ] Deliver **3 titles + 3 thumbnails** for A/B/C testing (standing rule).

# PART B — EPISODE 1

## B1. The compass — one sentence (Want–But–Until)
> **Sai wants to actually become the founder the greats are — BUT like everyone, he consumes endless business advice and almost never implements it — UNTIL a wheel forces him to copy Jeff Bezos's exact habit and live it inside his own company for real.**

If a clip doesn't serve *want / but / until*, it's filler — cut it or move it.

## B2. The Five Lines (the whole story before any footage)

| Line | The Vault Ep 1 |
|------|----------------|
| **1. Situation** | Sai is a young founder who studies the greats obsessively — books, clips, tactics. He knows more business advice than he could ever use. |
| **2. Desire** | He doesn't just want to *know* it — he wants to *be* it. To actually run his company the way the founders he admires ran theirs. |
| **3. Conflict** | He never implements. Knowledge piles up, nothing changes. It's the quiet disease of every founder and creator — learning as a substitute for doing. |
| **4. Change** | He builds the Vault and hands the decision to a wheel. It lands on **Bezos → talk to your customers ~2 hrs/week.** No backing out — he adapts it to his team and runs real 1-on-1s with 5 people. |
| **5. Result** | He walks out with 3–5 tangible changes actually shipped into Trendify — and early proof that forcing the implementation is what finally made the advice real. |

The 5-line core never changes. Every beat must trace back to one of these five.

## B3. The Story Lens (the prism — say it, don't show it)
This is **not** "how to run better 1-on-1s." If it reads as a management tutorial, it's dead.

**The lens: "What happens when you stop collecting advice and are finally forced to live one piece of it?"**
It's a *knowing-vs-doing* story wearing a billionaire-tactic costume. The vault/wheel is the hook candy; the **real** story is Sai vs. his own habit of learning and never applying. Declare that stakes-line early (cold open + mid-doc rehook both lean on it).

**Series callback:** adapting Bezos's *customer* time into *team* time ties straight into the BTS series' underlying thread — *it's all people.* Trendify's leverage right now is its team and retention, not new logos. That's why he bends the tactic this way, and it's worth saying out loud.

## B4. Title + Thumbnail = the contract (deliver 3 each for A/B/C testing)

**Titles:**
1. *I Let a Wheel Decide Which Billionaire I Copy*
2. *I Was Forced to Steal Jeff Bezos's Exact Routine*
3. *Every Founder Learns This — I Actually Did It*

**Thumbnails:**
1. Sai mid-spin at the physical wheel, Bezos's face locked in the window, bold **"2 HRS / WEEK."**
2. Split frame — Sai's face | Bezos's face — the open vault glowing between them.
3. Sai at the org chart, **"5 PEOPLE / 1 WEEK"** + a stopwatch, the wheel in frame.

**The promise this sets (must be paid off):** (a) a real, random draw, (b) a real founder's real tactic, (c) Sai *actually* doing it with real stakes and a real outcome. Reality must beat this or they leave.

## B6. The hook — draft script (first ~40 seconds)

> *(Sai, to camera, plain and honest — no music yet)*
> **"I've probably watched a thousand hours of people way smarter than me talk about business. And if I'm being honest — I've actually used almost none of it."**
> *(beat — he stands, walks to the vault, score creeps in)*
> **[NARR]:** *"Inside this vault is a file on basically every founder I've ever looked up to. Their story. And their ten best moves."*
> *(opens it — files, photos, the two wheels)*
> **[NARR]:** *"Two wheels. One picks the person. One picks the tactic. And whatever it lands on — I don't get a say. I have to actually do it. In my real company."*
> *(spin → Bezos. spin → a number → "TALK TO YOUR CUSTOMERS — 2 HRS / WEEK")*
> **Sai:** *(small laugh)* *"Okay. Of course it's the one that's actually hard."*
> *(title card: The Vault — Episode 1)*

That's Stakes + the thesis + the first head fake inside 40 seconds, master loop open, with the honest confession doing the work instead of a flashy claim.

## B7. Production sequencing (matches your method + the physical-prop build)
1. **Monday Lockdown** (this doc is most of it): founder = Bezos, tactic locked, the ONE payoff = *real changes shipped from the 1-on-1s*, cold-open promise, shot wishlist.
2. **Build the physical props** — vault, Bezos file (photo/bio/top 10), both wheels. Decide how the wheel lands on the locked pick (see open decisions).
3. **Script + film the hook + draw sequence first** (it frames everything).
4. **Film the implementation journey live across the week** — org chart, picking the 5, the 5 conversations (timelapse), the takeaways session, the implementation BTS. **B-roll horizontal.**
5. **Capture the result for real** — even if it lands a few days after the week.
6. **THEN script + film the A-roll sit-down interview** (the spine — Blocks A–H), once the outcome is known. Assemble most of the video before the A-roll, exactly as you work.
7. Lay interview audio as backbone → cut raw under it → cold open / title / transitions → music + color last → pull 2–4 verticals.

**Arc reference:** study Jesse James West + "Janice" for the open/close-loop rhythm — map their beats onto this skeleton, don't copy their topic.
