# The Vault — Vid 1: Full Story Outline
### "I Tried [Billionaire]'s $1B Marketing Tactic On My Own Company"

Built from Gray + Sai's 2026-06-24 coffee-shop storyboard memo, run through every storytelling framework in the playbook:
- **Five-Line Story Method** (`09-five-line-story-method`, Tim Runia) — the core skeleton
- **Want–But–Until** (`11`, Tim Runia) — the one-sentence compass

> Read top to bottom: it goes **compass → skeleton → engine → beat-by-beat → hook script → production**. Each layer zooms in. Nothing here is locked — it's the strongest version of the idea to react to.

## 1. The compass — one sentence (Want–But–Until)

> **Sai wanted to grow Trendify by turning ads back on — BUT the last time he ran ads it nearly sank the company — UNTIL he pulled a billionaire's exact advertising tactic from the vault and bet everything on signing one new client in 30 days.**

This is the line you come back to every time the edit gets lost. If a clip doesn't serve *want / but / until*, it's b-roll filler — cut it or move it.

---

## 2. The Five Lines (the whole story before any footage)

| Line | The Vault Vid 1 |
|------|-----------------|
| **1. Situation** | Sai built an ad agency starting at 15. The last two years he went heads-down perfecting the product — surviving on a small handful of clients. |
| **2. Desire** | He wants to grow on his own terms — turn ads back on and prove he can sign clients without depending on the few he has. |
| **3. Conflict** | The last time he ran ads, they failed — no retention, couldn't make payroll, nearly went under. Turning them back on means walking straight back into the thing that almost killed the company. |
| **4. Change** | He pulls a billionaire's advertising tactic from "the vault," commits to a 30-day bet (great ads + great team + sign a client), and rebuilds the broken pieces to ship it. |
| **5. Result** | A real company books a call — first proof the tactic is working, and the first step out from under his concentration risk. *(Full resolution = Part 2.)* |

**Expansion note:** this 5-line core never changes. Acts 1–3 are just the 10- and 20-line expansions of lines 3→4. When you script the A-roll, every beat must trace back to one of these five.

## 3. The Story Lens (the prism — say it, don't show it)

This is **not** "how to run ads." If it reads as a tutorial, it's dead (your 6/14 review + the Tim Runia trap).

**The lens: "What happens when a founder bets on the exact thing that once almost destroyed him?"**
It's a *redemption / face-your-failure* story wearing a business-tactic costume. The billionaire/vault is the hook candy; the **real** story is Sai vs. his own worst memory. Declare the stakes of that early (the cold open + the mid-doc rehook both lean on it).

## 5. The retention engine — how the Addiction Loop runs the whole video

The video is one **master loop** with smaller loops nested inside. The brain stays because there's always an unresolved prediction (ref 10).

- **`( MASTER LOOP` — opens in the hook, fully closes only in Part 2:**
  *"Will a billionaire's tactic actually work for Sai — will he sign a client in 30 days?"*
- **The two big Head Fakes (the dopamine spikes):**
  1. **Hook head fake:** viewer expects a confident "here's a winning tactic" video → Sai *laughs* → "we ran ads before and it nearly killed us." The guy applying the tactic is the guy it already failed on. (Prediction error — and it *clicks*, because it raises the stakes instead of confusing.)
  2. **Mid head fake:** "Ads are recorded, we're ready to ship" → *"…except our website was trash. Start over."* The viewer thought the work was done; it wasn't.
- **The 3 Rules = 3 nested open loops the viewer tracks as a checklist** (great ads ✓? great team ✓? signed client ✓?). This is the spine's progress bar.
- **Re-hooks at every step transition** — never a flat "and then." Each uses Want-But-Until connective tissue: *"The ads were done — but they couldn't go anywhere, because…"*

**Stakes (ref 10, step 1) are already strong:** a real character (Sai), something at risk (the company / his payroll / his pride), and urgency (a self-imposed **30-day clock**). Name all three in the first minute.

## 7. The hook — draft script (first ~40 seconds)

> *(over the vault VFX, cinematic score)*
> **"Everything inside this vault is worth more than a hundred billion dollars. Tactics from the most successful founders alive. Today, I'm pulling one out — and betting my own company on it."**
> *(spin → pull the card → [Billionaire] / "ADS")*
> **[NARR]:** *"In 2011, [Billionaire] was sitting on a business everyone thought was dead. So he did one thing — he bet on advertising. A quarter later, [company] had grown [X]%."*
> *(cut to Sai — he's laughing)*
> **Gray (off-cam):** "What's funny?"
> **Sai:** *"Because I've run ads before. And last time… it almost ended my company."*
> *(beat — score drops out)*
> **[NARR]:** *"Two years ago, the ads stopped working. We had a team to pay and nothing coming in. We nearly went under. I swore I was done with ads."*
> *(title card hits: Trendified Diaries, Episode N)*
> **[NARR]:** *"So for this to actually work this time, three things have to be true…"*

That's Stakes + Big Question + Head Fake all inside 40 seconds, master loop open, score-and-silence punctuation on the gut-punch line. Everything after is paying it off.

## 8. Production sequencing (matches your memo + Series v2 edit order)

1. **Lock the Monday Lockdown** (this doc IS most of it): story = **The Bet**, title + thumbnail, the ONE payoff = *the first client call*, cold-open promise, shot wishlist.
2. **Pick the billionaire** (research pass — see open decisions). Tactic is locked = "run ads"; the *who* is the thumbnail.
3. **Film the hook + script the hook first** (it frames everything).
4. **Film the raw/B-roll** against this plotline with a rough idea (record-ads scenes, website rebuild, dashboard, day-of nerves).
5. **Capture the payoff for real** — the first client call inside the 30 days. The story resolves honestly; if it won't be on camera, the interview narrates it (Series v2 fix).
6. **THEN script + film the A-roll interview** (the spine — Blocks A–G), once the outcome is known. Assemble most of the video before the A-roll, exactly as you said.
7. Lay interview audio as backbone → cut raw under it → cold open / title / transitions → music + color last → pull 2–4 verticals.

## 9. Live decisions only you + Sai can make

1. **The billionaire** — the tactic is "run ads"; the founder is the thumbnail. Needs a research pass for the most click-worthy founder whose ad comeback maps to Sai's (Bezos/Amazon = placeholder).
2. **How dramatic was "we went under"** — if genuinely dramatic, beat 3 (the old wound) is the engine. If milder, lean harder on beat 7 (concentration risk) as the stake.
3. **Film-first vs publish-only-if-it-lands** — the payoff is a real future event. Decide whether you film regardless and let it resolve honestly (stronger, more reality-TV) or only ship if the client signs.
4. **Length** — 5–20 min, no target. This skeleton holds at any length; more interview depth (Blocks A–G) = longer runtime.
5. **Part 2 scope** — "we ran ads for 30 days using this billionaire's tactic" — confirm it's a planned 2-parter so the cliffhanger is honest.
