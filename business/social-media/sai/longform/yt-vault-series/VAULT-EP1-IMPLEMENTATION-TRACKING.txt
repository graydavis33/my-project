THE VAULT — EP 1: IMPLEMENTATION TRACKING SYSTEM

How we film, track, and prove the changes Sai implements from the team 1-on-1s — even though we can't know what those changes are until the conversations happen. Once the tactics are chosen, duplicate the PER-TACTIC TRACKER block at the bottom, one copy per tactic.

Last updated: 2026-07-07


THE IDEA IN ONE PARAGRAPH

We can't script the tactics, but we can script the shape every tactic must fit. The sit-down A-roll interview films LAST, after outcomes are known — that interview is the narration spine. So the footage from the implementation weeks doesn't need to tell a story by itself; it needs to PROVE whatever Sai ends up saying. We're not shooting scenes in order — we're stocking an evidence locker. If the 10 ingredients below exist for every tactic, any structure can be built in post.


THE PROPS

- A small 3-column board on Sai's office wall for the two weeks: OPEN, SHIPPED, PROVEN
- One printed card per chosen change, styled like the vault files (same fonts + orange, smaller format) — a change graduates out of a conversation and into the vault's world
- Sai moves cards on camera. The camera holds on every card move. Board moves are act punctuation in the edit.
- Exactly 3 cards — the count is locked (Gray, 2026-07-07)


THE COMMIT CARD (filled by hand, on camera, at the takeaways session)

Every chosen change gets one card with exactly these 5 fields:

1. THE CHANGE — one line
2. WHO RAISED IT — which of the 5, and the anecdote that triggered it
3. SHIP ACTION — the single observable act that counts as "shipped" (first new meeting happens / doc goes live / the send)
4. PROOF — always a person, never a number: go back to whoever raised it and ask "real talk — better?" (the episode's own tactic, anecdotes over the dashboard, becomes the measurement method)
5. SHIP-BY — a date inside the two weeks

Filling the cards IS the takeaways beat (Beat 11). Pinning them to OPEN closes the success-bar loop S2. How the 3 get chosen + Sai's on-camera script for that session: VAULT-EP1-TAKEAWAYS-SCRIPT.


THE 10 MUST-CAPTURES PER TACTIC

GROUP 1 — one-take-only live moments. These can never be re-shot. Schedule them onto Gray's filming days, period:

1. THE ORIGIN — the moment in the 1-on-1 where the employee says the thing. Log the timestamp the second something sounds like a future change, so it's findable in post.
2. THE ANNOUNCE — the team hearing the change for the first time. Real faces. The most valuable shot of the implementation section.
3. THE SHIP MOMENT — the ship action from the card actually happening, plus the card sliding to SHIPPED.
4. THE CHECK-BACK — Sai returns to the person who raised it: "real talk — better?" Two-shot, uncut. Keep it even if the answer is lukewarm — ESPECIALLY if it's lukewarm. Card slides to PROVEN. This is the Bezos-phone-call mirror and the results payoff.

GROUP 2 — dies if not grabbed immediately:

5. THE BEFORE-STATE — footage of the world before the change: the old meeting, the old process, the Slack channel as it looks today. The moment a tactic is chosen this is the FIRST thing to shoot — once the change ships, the before-state no longer exists and can never be filmed again.

GROUP 3 — recapturable, flexible, self-capture friendly:

6. THE PREDICTION — right after committing, Sai to camera: what he expects, what he's afraid of, what failure would look like. Stakes generator — triumph material if he's right, honest gold if he's wrong.
7. THE FRICTION — at least one moment per tactic where it's harder than expected: pushback, a revision, "this isn't working." A smooth-sailing montage is dead footage. If friction happens off-camera, recover it via a confessional.
8. DIARY CONFESSIONALS — 60 seconds of Sai to camera (or phone, horizontal) every 2-3 days: where things stand, what surprised him. The highest-leverage habit in the system — confessionals timestamp emotion, create the re-hooks, and patch any hole where an event wasn't filmed.
9. ARTIFACTS — screenshots, Slack threads, docs, calendar before/after, dashboard screens ("the dashboard says fine — let's go ask"). These become the VFX/insert layer.
10. THE BOARD MOVES — every card state change on camera.


THE THREE DISCIPLINES

- SHOOT THE THING THAT'S ABOUT TO DISAPPEAR — before-state first, always, same day the tactic is chosen.
- IF YOU MISSED IT, CONFESS IT — any moment that happens uncaptured, Sai retells it to camera the same day while it's hot. The fresh retell often cuts better than the event itself.
- END-OF-DAY COVERAGE TICK — after each filming day, tick the coverage boxes below. The empty boxes ARE the next shot list. That's how "we don't know what we're shooting" becomes "we always know what's missing."


SELF-CAPTURE RULE (for anything that happens off Gray's filming days)

- Sai grabs 30-60 seconds on his phone, HORIZONTAL, and drops it into one shared Drive folder per card
- Screen recordings, Slack screenshots, and docs count as artifacts
- When in doubt, capture it — a bad angle beats a missing moment


THE TWO-WEEK CALENDAR

- DAY 0 — Takeaways session: pick the changes, fill the cards on camera, pin to OPEN. Same day: shoot every before-state and every prediction.
- DAYS 1-3 — The announces (scheduled on a filming day). Confessional #1.
- DAYS 3-9 — The doing. BTS + self-capture + artifacts. Confessionals every 2-3 days. Watch for friction.
- DAYS 7-10 — Ship-by window. Each ship moment filmed, card to SHIPPED.
- DAYS 10-14 — Check-backs with whoever raised each change, card to PROVEN.
- AFTER — Script and film the sit-down A-roll interview once outcomes are known. It narrates; the locker proves.

Notes:
- Beat 6's success-bar narration stays UNRECORDED until after the takeaways session, so the on-record promise matches the final cards word for word. The count is locked: exactly 3. Narration films last anyway.
- If a proof lands after edit lock, that card's payoff becomes the Ep 2 cold-open tease — the board carries over.
