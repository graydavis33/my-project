## The one-line version (logline)

The week Sai bets on Trendify again — turning ads back on for the first time since the
company that failed — and gets hit with a 24-hour fire drill from a Fortune 500 client
that tests whether everything he rebuilt actually holds.

## Story lens (the angle no one else takes)

Not "a founder vlog." The lens is: the unglamorous machinery of building an agency —
the hiring, the systems, the chaos — from a founder who has nothing to sell you.
Everyone else documenting their startup is funneling you to a course. Sai isn't.
That "I have nothing to sell you" honesty is the whole differentiator. Lean into it.

Series-long question this episode plants: can a self-taught 20-something who did high
school online actually build "the most creative ad agency in the world"? Episode 1 is
day one of the receipts.

## Target emotion

Primary: inspiration through honesty (the opposite of hype). Secondary: tension/curiosity
during the Fortune 500 crisis. We want the viewer to finish feeling "this is real, and I'm
rooting for him."

## Runtime

8–12 min. Target ~9–10. The Fortune 500 arc earns the longer end if the interview delivers.

## Episode arc (beat sheet)

Only the first ~30 seconds is heavily edited. Everything after breathes — raw footage,
music bed, color, and the interview cut in at the marked points.

### COLD OPEN (0:00–0:25) — the only fully-edited part

- Visual hook, frame 1, no talking yet: the most chaotic Thursday shot — Sai mid-scramble,
  phone up, team messages flying, or the laptop with the recruiting dashboard. Something that
  reads "something is going wrong."
- On-screen text (3–5 words): 24 HOURS or NOTHING IS WORKING.
- Verbal hook (pull from C2588): "This is stuff no one shows you. Today a Fortune 500 client
  rejected every single creator we spent weeks recruiting — and we have until the end of the
  day to replace them." Optionally hard-cut his line "everyone glamorizes being a founder…
  man, it is a lot."
- Then the reset line (interview, to be filmed): "But to understand why this week mattered so
  much, I have to back up." Smash to title.

### ACT 1 — SETUP: "the week we bet on ourselves again" (0:25–3:00)

- Monday B-roll: back in NYC, walking, coffee shop, opening the laptop.
- Interview cut-in (THE SPINE): the ads-bet origin — the company that failed, two years
  rebuilding, why turning ads back on this week is terrifying and exciting. This is the loop
  that carries the whole episode. Plant it here, pay it off late.
- Monday "week ahead" B-roll soundbite (C2508): website + ads going live, five days of
  onboarding he's leading.
- Character layer: the hiring philosophy (C2515) — the beer test, A+ players, the hire that
  burned him. Cut under B-roll of the interviews / NYC.

### ACT 2 — THE MACHINE: hiring, onboarding, systems (3:00–5:30)

- This is the "what building an agency actually looks like" section. The unglamorous core.
- Onboarding Shelby across days (C2559) + the improved creator cohort (C2532).
- The overwhelmed creator lead (C2538) — he under-trained her, owns the mistake. Best
  vulnerability beat in the week. Pair with interview: "training is an extension of your
  product." This makes him human and credible at once.
- Breather/texture: the eggs meal (C2545), roommate Abhi (C2533), Knicks (C2559). Don't
  overuse — 2 quick hits max, they're seasoning.
- The meta-beat (C2587/C2559): why he films this at all — "I have nothing to sell you."
  Place this as a mid-doc rehook surface.

### MID-DOC REHOOK (≈5:30) — right before the crisis

- Interview line (to film): "Everything I just showed you — the hiring, the systems, the
  onboarding — Thursday was the day it all got tested at once." Then cut to Thursday.

### ACT 3 — THE CRISIS (5:30–8:00)

- Thursday raw footage (C2588): the deep-work day blown up, the chaos, the team scramble,
  the appreciation voice note to Kate & Amelia (leave the "take two" honesty if it fits).
- Interview cut-in: the DoorDash-Miami callback (blur the name) — "the reason we even have a
  recruiting system is because of a fire drill exactly like this one." Therefore-connector:
  past chaos built the system that's being tested now.
- Interview cut-in (REQUIRED — the payoff): did they get the creators? What happened by end
  of day? Why this brand-side project matters (possible long-term retainer). Without this the
  climax doesn't land.

### OUTRO — THE WHY + SERIES HOOK (8:00–9:30)

- Friday reflective footage (C2603) + interview, intercut.
- Pay off the Act 1 loop: the ads went live / the bet is placed (state the outcome as far as
  it's known).
- The deep why: family financial struggles, took care of families, online high school,
  sacrificed social life, the office he dreams of building. This is the emotional landing.
- The Last Dab (quotable closing line, pick one and design toward it):
  - "I have nothing to sell you. I just wish someone had shown me this when I started."
  - "Everyone glamorizes being a founder. This is what it actually looks like."
  - "We've figured out how to keep clients for years. Now I just have to figure out how to
    get more of them — and I have no idea if I can."
- Series cliffhanger (from C2603): they've cracked retention/LTV but NOT how to get more
  clients. That's the open question that pulls people to Episode 2. "Next week, I try to
  solve the one problem that's been beating me."
- Soft CTA only. No hard pitch (he literally has nothing to sell). "If you want to follow
  the build, subscribe — that's it."

## A-ROLL INTERVIEW QUESTION BANK

Film this in ONE controlled sit-down AFTER the week, same outfit/lighting/background, looser
"talking to a friend" delivery. These answers ARE the narration spine — each maps to a cut-in
point above. Star-marked questions are the must-haves; the rest are bonus depth.

### Block A — Origin / the ads bet (feeds Act 1, the spine loop)
- ★ Last time you ran ads, what happened? Walk me through the company that failed and what it
  felt like to shut it down.
- ★ Why turn ads back on now, after two years? What's different this time?
- What did those two years of "only building something that retains clients" actually look
  like day to day?
- The morning you decided this was the week — what was going through your head?

### Block B — Hiring & the machine (feeds Act 2)
- ★ You said three interviews in one day and none were good. What changed about how you hire?
- Explain the "beer test." Where'd it come from and why does it matter more than skill?
- Tell me about the first account manager — the hire that burned you. What did it teach you?
- ★ You told your creator lead she was overwhelmed and it was your fault. What happened, and
  why do you call training "an extension of the product"?
- What does a great onboarding actually look like now vs. a year ago?

### Block C — The Fortune 500 crisis (feeds Act 3 — most important block)
- ★ Thursday was supposed to be a deep-work day. What actually happened?
- ★ Walk me through the moment you found out the client rejected every creator. What went
  through your head?
- ★ How did it actually end — did you get the creators by end of day? (REQUIRED payoff.)
- Why does this specific brand-side project matter so much? What's at stake if you nail it
  vs. blow it?
- You mentioned the DoorDash Miami campaign built your recruiting system. (Blur the name.)
  Tell that story — how did past chaos prepare you for this?
- You recorded a thank-you voice note to your team mid-chaos. Why was that the instinct?

### Block D — The why / vision (feeds the outro — emotional core)
- ★ What's the actual vision for Trendify — say it the way you'd say it to a 15-year-old you.
- ★ You grew up with financial struggles. How does that drive what you're building?
- You did high school online and one semester of college. What did you give up, and how does
  the office you dream of connect to that?
- ★ You keep saying you have nothing to sell. Then why film all of this?
- What's the one problem you still haven't solved? (Sets up the series.)

### Block E — Connective narration / rehooks (short lines, film these explicitly)
- A reset line for after the cold open: "But to understand why this week mattered, I have to
  back up."
- A rehook line before the crisis: "Everything I'd built was about to get tested at once."
- A wisdom-drop closer line for the outro card.

Shoot against this every episode so coverage is never missing. Aim for both WIDE and DETAIL
of each.

Establishing / day:
- [ ] NYC exterior — apartment door-out, street, skyline (morning + golden hour)
- [ ] Walking-and-talking tracking shot (gimbal) — arrival / between meetings
- [ ] Coffee shop: wide of him working + detail (hands on laptop, coffee, notebook)

- [ ] Over-the-shoulder of the laptop during a real call (screen blurred for client privacy)
- [ ] Interview/hiring call from across the room — wide, him animated
- [ ] Phone-in-hand during the Thursday scramble — the crisis texture
- [ ] Detail inserts: messages flying in, the recruiting dashboard, calendar packed
- [ ] Whiteboard / notes / SOP docs being built

Character / quiet:
- [ ] The eggs meal — top-down cooking detail + him eating (recurring motif)
- [ ] Gym — 2–3 dynamic angles

- [ ] Sit-down, controlled light, clean but "behind the scenes" background (not a studio)
- [ ] A-cam tight + B-cam wide/side for cutting rhythm
- [ ] 5+ min of room-tone b-roll of the interview space for transitions

## Edit workflow (recommended order)

1. Lock the cold-open clip first (the Thursday chaos line). Everything builds around it.
2. Film + transcribe the sit-down interview. That's the spine — you can't assemble Acts
   without it.
3. Lay the 3-act interview audio as the backbone.
4. Drop raw week footage under/around each interview answer (But/Therefore audit — every clip
   earns its place or it's cut).
5. Build cold open + Act transitions + outro card.
6. Music (dips under every interview answer, returns under montages), sound-design act bridges,
   color last.
7. Pull a vertical short from the cold-open crisis + the "I have nothing to sell you" line for
   cross-post.
