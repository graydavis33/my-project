# Ep 1 — Sit-Down Interview Script (grounded in the filmed week)

Companion to `EP1-STORYLINE.md`. Every question is a **callback to something Sai actually said on camera June 1–5** (`_transcripts/`). This sit-down is the **narration spine** — his answers get cut in over the week's raw footage. Film it in ONE controlled session, same outfit/light/background, loose "talking to a friend" delivery.

> ⚠️ NON-NEGOTIABLE CAPTURE: the footage **never shows how the Fortune 500 crisis ends.** If you get nothing else, get Block C's payoff. Without it, the climax has no landing.

---

## Run-of-show
- **Restate the question in the answer** ("The last time we ran ads…") so each line stands alone for voiceover.
- **Push past the headline to the scene.** When he gives the summary, ask "put me in that moment — where were you, what did it feel like."
- **Honor the flags:** never name the current Fortune 500 client; **blur/cut every "DoorDash"** (he already flagged it himself on June 4).
- ★ = must-have (it's the spine). Unmarked = bonus depth.

## BLOCK A — The ads bet *(feeds the Cold Open reset + Act 1 spine)*

- ★ **The last time you ran ads was January 2024.** Walk me through that company — the "really crappy product," the ads fatiguing, bleeding payroll for months until you shut it down. What did it feel like to pull the plug?
- ★ **This week you turned ads back on for the first time since then.** Why now, after two years? Why is flipping that switch terrifying *and* exciting?
- For two years you said the only thing you did was "build something that actually retained clients." What did those two quiet years actually look like day to day?
- Monday you laid out the week — website going live, ads going live, five straight days of onboarding you're leading. Standing there Monday morning, what were you betting on?

## BLOCK B — Hiring & the machine *(feeds Act 2 — "what building an agency actually looks like")*

- ★ **"Three interviews, none of them were good."** What changed about how you hire — when did "charismatic and I want to be around them" stop being enough?
- Explain the **beer test**. Where'd it come from, and why does "would I get a beer with them" decide culture more than skill?
- ★ Tell me about **the first account manager at Trendify** — first person you interviewed, offered the job, burned within a week. What did getting carried away with charisma teach you?
- ★ Your **creator lead put a 5 out of 10** on her end-of-day report. You realized you'd dropped a system on her without training her. Walk me through that — and why do you call **"training an extension of your product"**?
- The creator cohort that "felt 10 times better" — what was so broken about the old onboarding, and what did simplifying 90% of the info actually fix?

## BLOCK C — The Fortune 500 crisis *(feeds Act 3 — the climax. MOST IMPORTANT BLOCK)*

- ★ **Thursday was supposed to be deep work** — the deck, the creator roster, the editor dashboard. Instead? Walk me into how the day fell apart.
- ★ Walk me through the moment you found out **the brand rejected every creator you'd spent weeks recruiting.** What went through your head — three specific creators needed, none onboarded, end of day?
- ★ **How did it actually end — did you get the creators by end of day?** *(REQUIRED — this is the payoff the footage is missing. Get the outcome on the record.)*
- Why does this **brand-side project** matter so much — the shot at a long-term retainer? What's at stake if you nail it vs. blow it?
- The recruiting system that saved you Thursday — you built it during the **Miami campaign fire drill** *(blur the brand name)*. Tell that story: how did past chaos build the thing being tested now?
- Mid-chaos, your instinct was to record a **thank-you voice note to Kate and Amelia.** Why was that the move when everything was on fire?
- You said **"everyone glamorizes being a founder… man, it is a lot."** Say more — what's the part nobody puts on camera?

## BLOCK D — The why / the vision *(feeds the Outro — emotional core)*

- ★ Say the vision the way you mean it: **"the most creative ad agency in the world"** + the in-person Manhattan office, the morning meditations, the culture. Why does the culture piece matter as much as the agency?
- ★ You grew up with **financial struggles in your own family.** How does that drive who you're building this for — "take care of a lot more families than just myself"?
- You **did high school online and one semester of college, and sacrificed your social life.** How does the office you dream of connect back to that — building the friend group you missed, for yourself and people with your story?
- ★ You keep saying **"I have nothing to sell you."** Then why film all of this — why does reaching "even five young entrepreneurs" matter to you?
- ★ **The one problem you haven't cracked:** you've nailed retention and LTV — clients stay for years — but not *how to get more clients.* Say that out loud as the thing you're chasing next. *(This is the series cliffhanger.)*

## BLOCK E — Connective lines *(short — film these explicitly, clean takes)*

- **Cold-open reset** (after the Thursday hook, smash to title): *"But to understand why this week mattered so much, I have to back up."*
- **Mid-doc rehook** (right before the crisis): *"Everything I'd built — the hiring, the systems, the onboarding — Thursday was the day it all got tested at once."*
- **The closing Last-Dab line** (pick one, do a few takes):
  - *"I have nothing to sell you. I just wish someone had shown me this when I started."*
  - *"Everyone glamorizes being a founder. This is what it actually looks like."*
  - *"We've figured out how to keep clients for years. Now I just have to figure out how to get more — and I have no idea if I can."*
