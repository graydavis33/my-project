# Sai BTS Doc — SERIES FORMAT (canonical, story-first)

The repeatable skeleton for EVERY long-form episode. This is the **v2 format** Gray locked on
2026-06-23 when he took full creative agency of Sai's content.

**It supersedes `ep2/_SERIES-TEMPLATE.md`.** Same cinematic Netflix-doc style, same fixtures, same
scripted-interview spine — but the backbone flips from *theme-of-the-week* (reactive) to
*story-of-the-week* (intentional). Copy this into each new episode folder and fill it in.

---

## Why v2 exists (read once)

Ep 1 and Ep 2 both stalled for the same reason: we filmed the week, then went looking for the
story afterward — and the payoff (Ep 1's Fortune 500 resolution, Ep 2's launch result) was never
captured on camera. That's the "going in blind" trap.

v2 fixes it with two additions:
1. **The Story Engine** — a short menu of episode types, so on Monday you already know what story
   you're chasing. You're never staring at a blank week.
2. **The Monday Lockdown** — before you shoot, you lock the title + thumbnail + the ONE payoff you
   must capture. If the payoff won't happen on camera, you schedule the interview to capture it.

Same look. Same enjoyment. Just decided up front instead of discovered after.

---

## The format in one line

A weekly, cinematic "Netflix-doc" of Sai building Trendify. Each episode is built around ONE
**pre-chosen story** (a bet, a crisis, a launch...), narrated by a **scripted sit-down interview**,
with the real week's footage cut in as the evidence that proves the story.

## The locked style decisions (don't re-litigate)

| Decision | Locked choice |
|---|---|
| **Backbone** | One **story** per episode, chosen Monday — the days are the evidence for it |
| **Narration spine** | Post-week scripted sit-down interview — **the interview is the DRIVER** |
| **Cold open** | ~30s dramatic, trailer-style hook built from the story's stakes, then title |
| **Body feel** | Cinematic / Netflix-doc — great story + raw beautiful shots, NO flashy cuts |
| **Length** | 8–12 minutes (no hard cap; let the story set it) |
| **Closer** | A quotable Sai line + a next-week cliffhanger |
| **Continuity** | Season-long thread — callback to last week, tease next week, one big season question |
| **Music** | Licensed cinematic score (swells under story, dips under talking) |
| **Title card** | On-screen = series name + number only ("Trendified Diaries, Episode N") |
| **Voice** | First-person "here's what worked for me / still figuring it out" — Sai is NOT the all-knowing expert. Never disparage other people/brands. |

## ⭐ The Story Engine (the core of v2)

Every episode is ONE of these archetypes. Each one comes with built-in stakes, a clear thumbnail
moment, and a payoff you must capture. On Monday, look at the real week ahead and pick the one
archetype that fits the biggest thing actually happening.

| # | Archetype | The story is... | Built-in stakes | The payoff to capture |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **The Bet** | Sai makes a risky call this week (e.g. turning ads back on) | What happens if it fails | Early result + what he learned |
| 2 | **The Crisis / Fire Drill** | Something breaks or a hard deadline lands (Fortune 500 24h recruit) | The clock | Resolved or not — and the cost |
| 3 | **The Launch / Ship** | Putting something into the world (website, product, ads) | Will it land | The launch moment + first reaction |
| 4 | **The Rebuild / The Fix** | Fixing a broken system (Ep 2's "systems") | The pain it's causing now | The new thing working — or a deeper problem revealed |
| 5 | **The Milestone / The Number** | Hitting or missing a goal (first $X month, Nth client) | The streak / the goal | The moment it lands + the reflection |
| 6 | **The Decision / The Fork** | A hard choice between two paths (hire vs. don't, this client vs. that) | The tradeoff | The call he makes + why |
| 7 | **The Person** | An episode anchored on a key relationship (mentor, new hire, teammate) | What's at stake between them | What the relationship reveals about Sai |

**How to pick (Monday, 5 minutes):**
1. What's the single biggest *real* thing happening this week? Map it to an archetype above.
2. If nothing huge is happening, run a "quiet" archetype — **Decision**, **Person**, or pull a
   beat off the **season thread** (the unanswered question from last episode).
3. Never force a story that isn't real. The archetype is the *lens*, not a fiction — Sai's voice
   rule (not-the-expert, honest) still governs everything.

## 🔒 The Monday Lockdown (do this BEFORE you shoot)

This is the ritual that kills the blind-week problem. One page, locked before any footage:

- [ ] **Story + archetype** — one sentence. ("THE BET: Sai turns ads back on for the first time
      since the company that failed.")
- [ ] **Working title** — locked first; it sets the promise. (See the story-arc-playbook: the
      title is a contract with the viewer.)
- [ ] **Thumbnail moment** — the single frame/idea that sells the click. What has to be shot to get it?
- [ ] **The ONE payoff to capture** — the resolution the story needs. Write where/when it gets
      captured. **If it won't happen on camera, the interview captures it.** (This is the Ep 1/Ep 2 fix.)
- [ ] **Cold-open promise** — the ~30s hook concept and the stakes line it leads on.
- [ ] **Shot wishlist** — 3–5 must-get scenes/moments that prove the story.
- [ ] **Season-thread hook** — how this episode pays off last week + teases next week.

Lockdown lives at the top of each episode's `EPN-STORYLINE.md`.

## The repeatable episode skeleton

```
0:00  COLD OPEN      ~30s trailer-style hook built on the story's STAKES (captioned, scored)
0:30  TITLE CARD     "Trendified Diaries, Episode N"
              ↓
      THE STAKES     interview + footage establish what's on the line this week
              ↓
      ACT 1–3        the week's scenes that PROVE the story play out,
                     interview audio as spine + raw footage cut under it
              ↓
      MIND-DUMP      one honest, raw vulnerability beat (fixture)
              ↓
      THE PAYOFF     the result of the bet/crisis/launch — the resolution (the must-capture beat)
              ↓
      THE WHY        the deeper personal meaning behind it
              ↓
      CLOSER         quotable line + next-week cliffhanger + season thread
```

## Fixtures — must appear in every episode

- [ ] **Week-ahead intro** — Sai states the plan / what's at stake at the start
- [ ] **One honest mind-dump** — a raw reflection beat (the emotional anchor)
- [ ] **The payoff beat** — the story actually resolves on screen (NEW, non-negotiable in v2)
- [ ] **Lesson / takeaway** — stated clearly, kept light on graphics
- [ ] **Season-long thread** — one callback to last week + one tease toward next week

## Interview question blocks (built from the CHOSEN story, reuse every week)

The interview is scripted off the Monday Lockdown — the questions exist to pull the chosen story's
spine out of Sai, not to discover it.

- **Block A — The stakes:** What were you betting on / up against this week, and why did it matter?
- **Block B — The low point:** Where did it get hard / uncertain? (the honest beat)
- **Block C — The turn:** What shifted — the moment or decision the story hinges on?
- **Block D — The work:** What did you actually do? (the unglamorous machine)
- **Block E — The payoff:** How did it land? *(This is where you capture a resolution that wasn't
  on camera — ask Sai to narrate the outcome directly.)*
- **Block F — The why:** Why does this connect to something deeper/personal?
- **Block G — The cliffhanger:** What's still unsolved heading into next week? (season thread)
- **Block H — Connective lines:** Short reset/rehook lines filmed explicitly for the edit.

## Look & sound rules (the style — unchanged from v1)

- **On-screen text = minimal.** Let the footage breathe. Cinematic, not a vlog.
- **Captions only where needed:** (a) Sai genuinely hard to hear, (b) the cold-open hype recap,
  (c) the title-card sequence. NOT burned in throughout.
- **Date / day lower-thirds** as the week progresses.
- **No flashing / no fast-cut energy.** Restrained, premium pacing. Raw cinematic shots.
- **Licensed cinematic score** under the story; dips under interview answers, returns under montage.

## Standing privacy rules (carry every episode)

- **Never name clients on camera** — blur/mute or cut; refer to them generically.
- Watch for brand names Sai asks to cut mid-clip (Ep 1: a delivery brand; Ep 2: "Cloak"/"Atlas").
- Confirm anything personal/sensitive before it goes in.

## Edit order (every episode)

1. Lock the cold-open hook concept first (it frames everything).
2. Write + film + transcribe the scripted interview (the spine — can't assemble without it).
3. Lay interview audio as the backbone, in story order.
4. Cut raw footage under/around each answer (every clip earns its place or it's cut).
5. Build cold open + title + act transitions + closer.
6. Music, sound design, color last.
7. Pull 2–4 vertical shorts from the strongest moments for cross-posting.

## How the tools plug in

- **Story-arc board** (`web-apps/story-arc-board/`, `python server.py` → localhost:4500) — point it
  at the week's footage folder, drag clips into arc lanes, lock the spine + title (the Monday Lockdown
  in visual form).
- **Scriptwriter subagent** — drafts the scripted sit-down interview in Sai's voice from the chosen
  story + the question blocks above.
- **Footage Organizer** — `tag --episode "Name"` makes the week's doc footage pullable as b-roll;
  `ship --episode "Name"` finalizes after delivery.
- **mlx-whisper** (Mac, free) — transcribe the week's raw footage to find the proof clips.

## What about Ep 1 and Ep 2?

Both are mid-edit under the old reactive template. Under v2 they each map cleanly to an archetype —
**Ep 1 = The Bet** (turning ads back on) with a Crisis subplot (Fortune 500), **Ep 2 = The Rebuild**
(systems). They were stuck on the same missing piece v2 makes mandatory: capturing the payoff in the
interview. Finish them by booking those scripted sit-downs and asking the **Block E — The payoff**
questions. No need to reshoot — just capture the resolution Sai never said on camera.
