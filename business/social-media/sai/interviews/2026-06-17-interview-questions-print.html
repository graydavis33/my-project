Sai — Interview Questions

Part 1 — Elite Open-Ended Questions

1. The Early Money Question

"You had real money before most people have a driver's license. When the money showed up that early — what did it actually change about how you see ambition, risk, and what you're chasing now?"

→ Pull threads: Did money remove fear or add it? What he wanted before vs after. Whether having "enough" young is a gift or a trap.

2. The Contrarian Belief

"What's something you believe about building a business that almost everyone in your world would disagree with — and you're convinced you're right about?"

→ Pull threads: Why everyone else is wrong. The moment he realized it. What it's cost him to act on it.

3. Creativity vs. The System

"You've said creativity is worthless until you systemize it. But every great creative person is terrified that structure will kill the magic. How do you actually hold both at once without losing either?"

→ Pull threads: A time the system did kill an idea. How he protects raw creativity inside a machine that's built for output.

4. The Bet That Looked Insane

"Walk me through a decision you made that everyone around you thought was stupid or reckless — where you were operating on something they couldn't see yet."

→ Pull threads: What he saw that they didn't. How he sat with being the only believer. What he'd tell someone facing that same loneliness.

5. What Scale Teaches You

"You've produced something like 10,000 ads. There are lessons you can only learn at that volume — things that are invisible when you've made ten. What did scale teach you that you could never have figured out small?"

→ Pull threads: The thing that only shows up at volume. What stops working when you get big. The skill that mattered at 10 that became useless at 10,000.

6. The Self You Had to Kill

"Who did you have to stop being in order to become who you are now? What's the version of you that you had to leave behind?"

→ Pull threads: The habit/identity/relationship that didn't survive the growth. Whether he misses that person. What it cost.

7. Reading People

"You build teams, you hire, you bet on people. What do you see in someone in the first ten minutes that tells you everything — that other people completely miss?"

→ Pull threads: A specific hire that proved his read right (or wrong). The tell he trusts. How he learned to read it.

8. What the Money Actually Bought

"Everyone thinks they want the money. But once you have it — what does it actually buy you that nobody talks about? And what did you assume it would fix that it didn't?"

→ Pull threads: The thing money quietly solved. The thing it made worse. What he'd tell his 17-year-old self about it.

9. The Honest Ledger

"What have you genuinely sacrificed for this — not the humble-brag answer, the real one? And on the days it's hard, do you think it was worth it?"

→ Pull threads: The relationship/health/experience that paid the price. Whether he'd do it again. The day he questioned all of it.

10. The Long Game

"Forget this quarter or this year. What are you actually building toward over the next twenty years — and what do you want to be true about you and your work long after the current stuff is forgotten?"

→ Pull threads: The version of the empire he sees. What "winning" finally looks like. What he's afraid of never getting to.

Part 2 — EP2 Deep Interview Questions

1. The Missing System (C2656 — the epiphany)

"You had an epiphany this week — that every stress in your life right now is really just a system you haven't built yet. So let me push on that: is stress actually information? A signal pointing straight at the thing that's broken? And if that's true — what is the stress you feel right now trying to tell you?"

→ Threads: Is all stress solvable, or is some of it just the price of caring? The stress that has no system answer. How he tells the two apart.

2. The Productivity Trap (C2655 — the confession)

"You said something most founders won't admit out loud — that you tie your sense of worth to whether you moved the business forward that day. Where did that wiring come from? And have you ever genuinely felt like enough on a day you did nothing?"

→ Threads: When it started. Whether he'd give it up if he could. The line between drive and self-punishment. What he says to himself on a zero day.

3. Structure and the Muse (C2656 — "structure frees creativity")

"You said something that sounds like a flat contradiction — that building rigid structure is what sets your creativity free. Most people think a system is where ideas go to die. So make the case: why does the cage make the bird sing louder?"

→ Threads: An idea that only existed because of a constraint. A time structure killed the magic. Where the line sits.

4. Firing Yourself (C2643 — handing off the onboarding call)

"This week you watched someone else run an onboarding call — a job you used to do, and probably still do best. There's something almost painful about handing off the thing you're great at. What does it actually feel like to make yourself unnecessary on purpose?"

→ Threads: The fear of being replaceable vs. the goal of being replaceable. What he still refuses to let go of. How you trust someone with your baby.

5. A Company Is Just People (C2669 — "it's just people")

"You said building an agency is uniquely brutal because in the end it's just people — you can't write a process that fully captures a human being. So if the whole thing comes down to people, what are you really in the business of: marketing, or human behavior?"

→ Threads: The role-change SOP nightmare. Why he loves it anyway. The hardest people-problem he's ever solved. Whether every great founder is secretly just great at people.

6. The Belonging You're Building (C2669 — the UGA story)

"You told a story that stuck with me — that in college you had no friends because all you did was work, and what you really wanted was people who loved building as much as you. So be honest with me: are you building a company, or are you building the belonging you never had?"

→ Threads: The loneliness of the obsessed. Is the dream office about success or connection? What he'd say to that kid at UGA. The risk of needing your company to love you back.

7. Nothing to Sell (C2685 — the raw-content ethos + the trillionaire bit)

"You keep making these raw videos — no hook tricks, no pitch, nothing to sell. In an attention economy built on the exact opposite, that's almost irrational. Why document the climb before you've reached the top — and what will you have that the people who waited to post don't?"

→ Threads: The "one day I'll be a trillionaire and say I showed you how" bet. Building in public vs. hiding until you win. What he wants this footage to mean in 10 years. Is it confidence, or a hedge?

8. The Thing You Keep Not Doing (C2614 — the launch slips again)

"For two weeks straight the plan has been to go live — turn the ads on, launch the site — and for two weeks it's slipped. At some point a delay stops being logistics and becomes something else. What's really underneath the thing you keep putting off?"

→ Threads: Perfectionism, fear, or honest priority? What going live actually risks. The story he tells himself about why it's "not time yet." What finally flips it.

9. The Highest-Leverage Point (C2668 — formats)

"You said the biggest lever on creative — above almost everything — is the format you test, not the individual video. Most people pour everything into the wrong layer. Where else in business do people obsess over the visible thing while the real leverage sits one level up, invisible?"

→ Threads: Formats as the actual product. How he finds leverage points. The thing he stopped caring about that everyone else still optimizes. Leverage in hiring, money, attention.

10. One Game, One Whole Day (C2655 — Knicks loss → broken routine)

"You traced an entire wasted, stressful day back to one thing — staying up for a Knicks game, getting too fired up, skipping your night routine. That's a lot of power to hand one small input. Do you think high performers are actually more fragile than people realize — that the machine only runs if every little thing is protected?"

→ Threads: The non-negotiables. What happens when one domino falls. Discipline as self-respect, not self-control. Is the routine actually the real product?
