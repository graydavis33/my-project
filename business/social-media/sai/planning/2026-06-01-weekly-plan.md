## 🗓️ The Weekly Rhythm

### Saturday — PLAN + SCRIPT day
- Sai scripts out the week's work + content.
- **~1-hour recording session** with Gray (the story riff, like the May 26 batch).
- **Gray's Saturday job:** organize footage, decide where A-roll is needed, define the
  hooks. Think in the arc: **hook → story → conflict → resolution → lesson.**

### Sunday — FILM day
- Pure filming, all chunked into one block:
  - Ads (if needed)
  - Short-form content
  - A sit-down talking YouTube video (if doing one that week)
- Capture the hooks + A-roll here.

### During the week — RECORD EVERYTHING
- Raw capture: spot to spot, talking, at the desk, skyline, walking to calls.
- Use Sai's natural **downtime** (e.g. the extra 30 min after a call is covered) to film
  raw moments and let Gray synthesize footage / prep questions.

## 📺 YouTube Long-Form (the new bet)

**Style:** documentary vlog — "building an agency in New York City."

- **Cadence:** aim for **1 vlog out**, plus a sit-down talking video **every other week**
  (~3 hrs of Sai's time: 1 script + 1 record + 1 review).
- **Format idea:** open on a montage hook ("this week was crazy, we did all this") → cut
  to black → "what's up YouTube." As raw as possible, but with **mini-arcs**
  (problem → solution, repeated) running throughout.
- **Future channel structure:** main channel (desk talking videos) + a **sidecar** of
  vlogs/logs.
- **Expectation set:** first episodes won't blow up — it's a build. Improve storytelling
  over time.

### 🎨 Gray's primary focus — the AESTHETIC
This is explicitly called out as Gray's forte:
- Lean into **NYC**: skyline B-roll, intentional transition shots, montages of transitions.
- Make Sai's day-to-day **feel aesthetic** — sounds, transitions, establishing shots, then
  going in on the actual moment. Sai's bet: aesthetic quality is what makes it take off.
