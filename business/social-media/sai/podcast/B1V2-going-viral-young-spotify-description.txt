In high school, Sai Karra went viral — hundreds of millions of views a month and millions of followers — and was on the verge of making a million dollars overnight. It looked like every teenager's dream. It turned out to be the worst thing that ever happened to him… and the most important lesson of his life.

In this episode of Building Blocks with Sai Karra, Sai tells the full story: growing up as an Indian kid in Georgia who just wanted to fit in, going viral on TikTok in high school, and pouring everything into an NFT project he was certain would make him millions overnight — until the market crashed, the company collapsed, and his entire sense of self collapsed with it.

These are the biggest takeaways:

• Why tying your identity to "proving yourself" will eventually break you
• The difference between tying your identity to your business vs. tying it to an outcome
• How a total collapse can be a blessing in disguise — and the Steve Jobs "you can only connect the dots looking backward" idea behind it
• Why building for yourself, not to impress others, is the thing that actually makes people buy from you

If you're young, ambitious, and chasing the next big win to prove something to the world — this one's for you.
