## Voice Rules Applied to Every Script

From `memory/feedback-sai-linkedin-voice.md`:

- "Founder" never "CEO"
- No "most founders…" punching down — attack ideas, markets, or his past self
- No AI-flavored summary closers ("the lesson is", "what this comes down to is")
- No invented copywriter details (specifics must be real — $10K, year four, etc., not "$60 tub of powder")
- No 3-beat "no X, no Y, no Z" parallel — cap at 2 beats with intensifier ("definitely", "honestly")
- No identity-wrapped restatement closers ("For a founder, that's a cheat code")
- Don't drop the verb in callbacks ("Before I started **meditating**" not "Before I started")
- Question CTAs optional — only on framework/long-form-shaped posts, default ends on punchline

## The 7 Scripts

### 1 — Time Horizon (IN-LANE — proven topic)

**Source:** Sai's LinkedIn 2026-04-23 ("Do Over / Time Horizon") — his own voice, already proven on text
**Format:** Hot Take / Confession (verbal template V17 + V20)
**Visual:** Eye-Lock Close-Up (Vis3) — single take, intimate
**Length:** ~50s

> I dropped out of college, did four years of high school online, and gave up my whole social life to build a business.
>
> If I could go back, I wouldn't change much.
>
> Except one thing.
>
> When you first start out, the opportunities feel endless. And they are.
>
> But that same feeling convinces you success is months away. Not years.
>
> So even when you're winning on paper — everything still feels miserable.
>
> Because the timeline in your head is wrong.
>
> I stopped thinking in weeks.
> I stopped thinking in months.
>
> Now I try to think in 3, 5, 10-year windows.
>
> When you zoom out that far, the bad days stop feeling like you're doomed.
> The slow months stop feeling like failure.
>
> If I could go back and tell my younger self one thing, it'd be this:
>
> It's going to take way longer than you think.
>
> That's the whole point.

### 2 — We Scaled DOWN to Scale Up (IN-LANE — ethical business / receipts)

**Source:** Newsletter 1 (Sep 2024) — the Miami high-rise → crash → rebuild story. Sai's most personal business story, never told on video.
**Format:** Confession with personal numbers (V17 + V7 receipt drop)
**Visual:** Walking through NYC OR eye-lock at desk. Could intercut B-roll of office walk if shooting at Trendify.
**Length:** ~60s

> In 2023, I was making more money than I knew what to do with.
>
> Living in a high-rise in downtown Miami. Ten grand a month on personal expenses. I was living my childhood dream.
>
> Five months later, it all came crashing down.
>
> We'd scaled the agency too fast. Signed too many clients. Built zero processes.
>
> Clients started churning. And we'd already spent the money.
>
> So we did something most people would never do.
>
> We scaled the business back down. Cut clients. Cut my own income.
>
> Spent the next year rebuilding the whole thing brick by brick.
>
> Biggest ego hit of my life.
>
> Here's what I learned about ethical business:
>
> If you can't actually deliver what you promised, you're not running a business.
>
> You're running something that takes longer to collapse.
>
> I'd rather build something real and make less for a year...
>
> Than scale something broken and have it crash on me twice.

### 3 — Inputs Over Outputs (IN-LANE — proven mindset post)

**Source:** Sai's LinkedIn 2026-04-16 ("Input Over Output") — proven text post
**Format:** Hot Take confession (V17)
**Visual:** Eye-Lock Close-Up (Vis3). Could also do walk-and-talk.
**Length:** ~40s

> The more I chase the outcome, the less it comes.
>
> I've watched it play out enough times to actually believe it.
>
> When I lock in on results — the growth slows down.
>
> When I shift back to the inputs — the work, the process, the customer — it flows.
>
> Every time.
>
> But here's the part nobody tells you:
>
> Training your brain to think this way is brutal.
>
> Your brain wants the scoreboard. It wants to know if it's working.
>
> You have to actively fight that.
>
> Just obsess over what you did today.
>
> Not what it'll produce.
> Not when it'll pay off.
>
> Just: did I do the work?
>
> Inputs compound. Chasing the outcome just burns you out.

### 4 — The $10 Guilty Pleasure (IN-LANE — customer obsession TACTIC, save-bait)

**Source:** Newsletter 3 (Oct 2024) — actual Trendify operating tactics, never told on Sai's video before. This is the kind of specific, tactical content that gets SAVED.
**Format:** How-I-built-my-client-list / specific tactic reveal (Format 13 / Format 17 hybrid; verbal V45 framework)
**Visual:** Eye-lock + (optional) B-roll insert of a care package being assembled. If filming at Trendify office, grab over-shoulder of someone writing a handwritten note.
**Length:** ~55s

> Here's something we do at my agency that nobody else does.
>
> When we hire someone — before their first day — we send them a $10 guilty pleasure.
>
> In the interview, we ask: what's your $10 guilty pleasure?
>
> A snack, a drink, whatever it is.
>
> Day one, it's waiting for them. With a handwritten note.
>
> Costs us ten bucks. Takes five minutes.
>
> But it tells every new hire one thing: we actually listened to you.
>
> Same energy with clients.
>
> For every five videos we make, we donate one to the client's nonprofit of choice. Free.
>
> None of this scales "efficiently." That's not the point.
>
> My hypothesis on the companies that win the next 50 years:
>
> They won't optimize for money.
>
> They'll optimize for the experience.
>
> And they'll make more money because of it.
>
> Try this one this week.

### 5 — Going Into Business With My Brother (IN-LANE — differentiator, underused on video)

**Source:** Strategy doc — brother dynamic is the #1 differentiator and is underused on short-form. Newsletter 6 confirms "year of my life building with my brother."
**Format:** Hot Take (V20) + Confession (V17)
**Visual:** Eye-lock — or shot at Trendify office with Srikar visible in background (powerful if doable). Could also do a 2-shot insert if Srikar passes through.
**Length:** ~50s

> Everyone says don't go into business with family.
>
> Year four with mine.
>
> We're closer now than we were before we started.
>
> Here's what nobody tells you about working with a sibling:
>
> You skip the trust-building phase.
>
> When I disagree with a normal cofounder, I have to manage how it lands. Be diplomatic. Worry I'll damage the relationship.
>
> When I disagree with my brother, I just say it. He says it back. We move on.
>
> No politicking. No ego management.
>
> The downside?
>
> The bad days hit twice.
>
> If the business is struggling, my brother is struggling.
>
> If he's struggling, I'm struggling.
>
> But honestly?
>
> I'd rather build with someone who has my last name...
>
> Than with someone I'm still figuring out.

### 6 — Fear Is the Most Useless Emotion (NEW TERRITORY — vulnerable, provocative)

**Source:** Newsletter 6 (Feb 2025) — Sai literally wrote it "mainly for myself." Most viscerally honest piece in his corpus. Has never been said on his video. New emotional register.
**Format:** Hot Take / Personal reckoning (V17 + V20)
**Visual:** Eye-Lock Close-Up (Vis3) — intimate, tight framing, minimal background. Could film at home or a quiet office corner.
**Length:** ~55s

> Fear is the most useless emotion you have.
>
> For the last year I've been scared to aggressively market my business.
>
> Scared something would go wrong.
> Scared a client would be upset.
> Scared an employee would mess up.
>
> So I just... didn't.
>
> Even though I'm genuinely proud of what we built.
>
> Then I realized something.
>
> Fear is your brain bracing you for survival.
>
> That's the entire purpose of it.
>
> But none of what I just listed is going to kill me.
>
> So what am I actually scared of?
>
> Your ancestors didn't beat diseases and fight wars for you to be scared of damaging your reputation.
>
> Bravery isn't being fearless.
>
> It's taking the action anyway.
>
> I'm choosing to be brave.

**Source:** Newsletter 7 (May 2025) — Sai's freshest, most original framework. Timely (AI is the biggest topic of 2026). Has real receipts (DoorDash, Reddit, Macy's, Kroger). Never said on his video.

> I lost sleep over AI.
>
> Like, seriously. Multiple spirals about how it kills my business. Kills entire industries.
>
> Then I figured something out.
>
> Everyone's racing to build AI. Or get smarter at using it.
>
> I'm doing the opposite.
>
> The more we automate, the more people will pay for what can't be automated.
>
> The more reality gets blurred, the more we'll chase what feels undeniably real.
>
> So in my business, we doubled down on the most un-AI thing you can do.
>
> We hyper-personalize everything.
>
> We spend time. We spend money. We send things that prove a human actually thought about you.
>
> Within one month of doing this...
>
> We got into talks with DoorDash. Reddit. Macy's. Kroger.
>
> A VP at DoorDash literally said: "Sai, best pitch I've ever received."
>
> While everyone's trying to look smart with AI...
>
> Just being undeniably human is the flex.
