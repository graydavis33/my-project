### 1 — Every Stress Is a Missing System

B. I stopped trying to fix my stress. I started building systems instead.
**Visual:** walking-and-talking down the NYC street, handheld, no title card — raw/candid, eyes occasionally off-camera.

C. Stress isn't a feeling problem. It's a systems problem.
**Visual:** at a notebook/whiteboard, writes "STRESS → SYSTEM," zoom punch-in on the words as he says the line.

**Script:**

> Every single stress in my life, at least right now, can be solved with a system.
>
> Money stress. A system for money.
>
> Time stress. A system for time.
>
> Team stress. A system for the team.
>
> I used to treat stress like something I had to feel my way out of.
>
> Sit with it. Wait for it to pass.
>
> Then I noticed something.
>
> Every single thing I was stressed about could be built into a better system.
>
> The stress wasn't telling me I was failing.
>
> It was telling me a system was missing.
>

>

>
