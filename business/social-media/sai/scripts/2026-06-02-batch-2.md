>

>

>

>
> 40% goes into basic indexes, treasuries, and international bond markets.
>

>
> I set a budget every month and follow it like my life depends on it.
>
> End of every week, I review everything I spent money on.
>
> End of every month, I review my total assets. What I made. What I lost.
>
> You can only change something if you track it.

>

### 6 — How to Build a Better Onboarding

A. A month ago, we hired creators to our team. The onboarding call went awful.

B. Yesterday we ran the best onboarding call I've ever run. Here's the exact framework.

C. If your onboarding is broken, you can't retain employees. Let me explain.

**Script:**

> A month ago, we hired creators to our team.
>
> The onboarding call went awful.
>
> Yesterday, we ran another one. And it was probably the best onboarding call I've ever run.
>
> Here's the exact framework you can follow to build a better onboarding experience for the people you bring onto your team.
>
> The first onboarding was incredibly complicated. Over 10 pages for one simple thing. Nothing was properly organized.
>
> So we sat down as a team.
>
> Step one. Review everything that went wrong during the first call. Make clear action items on what to improve.
>
> Step two. Implement the changes.
>
> Step three. Reorder everything so it makes chronological sense.
>
> Step four. Simplify 90% of the information. Cut anything that wasn't needed.
>
> We went from 10 pages to 3.
>
> Pro tip. Send an onboarding feedback form after every new hire so you keep improving the system.
>
> Better onboarding is just delegation at its finest. Everyone owns their own avenue.
>
> The creators we just onboarded are going to crush. Because they understand exactly what we want.

**Lane tag:** team-culture / scaling

### 10 — Redoing Finance

A. If you're even moderately stressed about your company's finances, you can follow the same framework that I did.

B. Q3 of last year, I couldn't sleep because of the finances. Here's the framework I built to fix it.

C. I cleared an entire quarter of my calendar to rebuild my company's finances. Here's exactly what I did.

**Script:**

> If you're even moderately stressed about your company's finances, you can follow the same framework that I did.
>
> Q3 of last year, I couldn't sleep almost every night. I was constantly thinking about the finances. It felt like a shit show.
>
> I had no idea what was coming in. What was going out.
>
> How much we had in receivables and payables.
>
> It was all over the place.
>
> Until I came up with a framework.
>
> Step one. Audit your own feelings toward finance. Finance is very correlated with feelings. The two F's.
>
> On one side of a piece of paper, write down everything that currently feels stressful about your business's finances. Everything.
>
> On the other side, write down what an ideal world would look like for each one.
>
> Step two. Use AI to learn the fundamentals of finance. Every term. P&L. Cash-based P&L. Accrual. Accounts receivable. Accounts payable.
>
> Step three. Build the system yourself. Without a CPA. So you fully understand it.
>
> Step four. Deploy the system. Get a VA's feedback. Then hire a CPA on a two-week sprint to make sure your T's are crossed and your I's are dotted.
>
> I cleared my calendar for three months. Told myself I would only work on this. And that's it.
>
> Now I don't have to worry about finances again.

**Lane tag:** finance / scaling
