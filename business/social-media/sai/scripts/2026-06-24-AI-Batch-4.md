## Foundation

Viewer: aspiring and early-stage founders who want the real journey, not guru advice.

Three pillars — every video hits at least one:
- Value: they leave with something they can use.
- Relatability: aspirational but grounded — "I want to be like him."
- Authority through experience, not instruction: "here's what I did / where I'm at," never "here's what you must do." Sharing alongside, not teaching down.

Most reference videos are teacher-mode. Our core job is to convert prescriptive into first-person experiential.

Production constants:
- Shot horizontal. Footage moves freely between shorts and long-form, and horizontal shorts stand out in-feed. Captions and graphics sit clean in 16:9. Exception: a prop-only video that won't be reused can be vertical.
- Every reference below was shot vertical. We borrow the flow, graphics, captions, pacing, and value — not the framing. Re-stage each into 16:9.
- Captions in Sai's house style: Montserrat SemiBold, lowercase except I, no punctuation, hard cuts with no flicker.

Retention rules:
- Physical beats digital. A real prop or action holds attention better than a graphic.
- A number is a finish line. "5 things" promises a payoff, so they stay.
- Lead with the most surprising point, save the strongest for last (triggers the save and rewatch).
- The visual hook rhymes with the verbal hook, it does not repeat it.

## Video 1A — 5 small things I did to grow my business

Format: Numbered cause-and-effect listicle. Recurring series look: "[#] ___ I did, and here's what it got me." Editor builds one reusable graphic and caption style we reuse every time.

Purpose: Value. The viewer leaves with small, repeatable tactics they could copy this week. "Business" not "Trendify" keeps it broad and instantly understood — the curious go find what his business is (bio or next video). "Small" promises low effort and high relatability: "I could do that too."

Topics: small, repeatable growth tactics (e.g. post daily, DM 50 founders, change one line on the site).

Verbal hook: "5 small things I did to grow my business." Variants to test: "5 small things that actually grew my business" / "the 5 smallest things that grew my business the most."

Visual hook: Blurred-boxes reveal (locked). He zooms in on camera, then 5 text boxes spawn in a row below him, all blurred. As he explains each one, that box un-blurs to show 1-2 words or a small graphic. Five open loops at once — visible finish line plus curiosity for every item. Keep all boxes on screen the whole time; reveal number 5 last and tease it ("the last one's the one nobody does"). Mouth says "5 small things," the boxes show the count but withhold the content — that gap is the hook.

Style: smart and practical. Clean, fast, useful.

Camera shots: talking head with a zoom-in to trigger the boxes. B-roll of him doing each small thing (easy to grab any shoot day; footage-organizer can pull existing clips). A clean row of 5 boxes sits better in 16:9 than stacked vertical.

Graphics and effects: HyperFrames orange-glass boxes. Pop/scale spawn animation, all blurred. Each box un-blurs on cue, synced to his words; the active box lifts and glows.

Physical mechanic: none — graphics-driven.

## Video 1B — Bets I made at 15 that actually worked

Format: Same listicle engine as 1A, but big risky bets instead of small tactics — inspirational, not copyable. Stays separate from 1A: no overlapping items, different emotional register, different pillar.

Purpose: Relatability and aspiration ("I want to take my shot"). The 15-to-21 age gap is unique to Sai — nobody else can make this exact video, which makes it the stronger of the two. This one is a must.

Topics: big, gutsy bets that shaped his trajectory.

Verbal hook: "Bets I made at 15 that actually worked." Fork to A/B test: "...that actually worked" (humble, fits sharing-not-telling) vs "...that made me successful at 21" (stronger payoff promise, slightly braggy).

Visual hook: AI match-cut, then-to-now. Old photo of young Sai, a slick match-cut transition, then a photo or clip of him now working or on a stage. The transition itself is the spectacle.

Style: inspired and gutsy.

Camera shots: talking head, old footage/photos of young Sai, and conference/stage footage as the "arrival" payoff (kid to on a stage). Have these on hand before filming.

Graphics and effects: AI match-cut transitions — one micro then-to-now cut per bet, not just at the top, to keep the hook energy all the way through. A subtle age-ticker through-line (15 to 16 to ... to 21) climbing as the story moves. End on the stage/conference footage.

## Video 2 — Decision / comparison with a two-bucket toss

Format: Comparison or binary (this vs that) built around one quotable, contrarian-but-true principle, delivered flat and confident. Hyper-shareable — people tag someone.

Purpose: Authority through experience, where Sai has real reps. Keep the comparison structure; change the topic to something he's actually done.

Topics: candidates are what makes a good ad, how to acquire clients, or how to find mentors. Sai picks the one he has the most hands-on experience with.

Verbal hook: the question itself, no preamble.

Visual hook: a titled statement of what the video is, then straight into the bucket pulls.

Style: confident and flat. Genuine live reactions; misses are funny, keep some.

Camera shots: Sai at a table with two buckets. He pulls a slip, reads it, reacts, and tosses it.

Graphics and effects: the question pops on-screen above him after he reads each slip.

Physical mechanic: two-bucket toss (locked, signature candidate). Pull a slip, read it, the question pops on-screen, toss it into the YES or NO (or GOOD or BAD) bucket, explain why. Make this Sai's recurring signature mechanic. Banked alternatives for future videos, don't stack them: mystery-paper T-graph, the dice (6 questions, roll and answer), physical tier-list board, APPROVED/TRASH stamp.

## Video 3 — Trendify growth journey (carousel)

Format: Growth-journey breakdown, build-in-public. Carousel only, no reel — the story breathes better slide by slide and carousels win saves. This is a design deliverable, not an edit (different skill: slide design and consistency).

Purpose: The purest build-in-public piece. Sai tells his own Trendify journey — real numbers, real milestones, the turning point. Max authority through experience, zero borrowed advice. Framing is "what I changed" (his receipts), not "what you should do."

Topics: Trendify before-to-after, the turning point ("most people quit here, we didn't"), and the 5 things that changed.

Verbal hook: the cover slide. Options: "First month on social: 10K views. This month: 200K." / "At 15 I had $0 and an idea. At 21 I run a company." / "My first paycheck from the internet: $X. Last month: $Y." / "3 years ago I posted to nobody. Now: [Trendify metric]."

Visual hook: split-frame young photo and now photo, big numbers, a subtle "swipe →".

Style: clean, saveable. Number-tip slides (1/5 ... 5/5) act as a finish line.

Camera shots: not a filming job — needs a young photo and a now photo, plus a small photo or graphic per change.

Graphics and effects: slide design. Cover (then/now hook), bridge slide ("Nothing changed for months... until I did 5 things differently"), 5 change slides (each: big number, bold headline, 1-2 lines of his real experience, small photo/graphic), close slide (lesson plus "Save this" CTA plus soft follow). Cover and close use the same then/now visual language as 1B.

Physical mechanic: none.

## Video 7 — King of the hill (physical cards)

Format: king-of-the-hill comparison run with physical cards. Not fixed pairs — a gauntlet: the champion stays and new challengers cycle through one at a time until one idea survives everything.

Purpose: continuous open loops ("will the champ survive?"), rewatchable, comment-bait ("you're wrong, X should've won"). Each "why" is a quick personal receipt ("I learned this when ___"), never a law.

Topics: pick one coherent theme (e.g. "what matters most as a founder") and run items from the topic pool through the gauntlet.

Verbal hook: double-slam plus verbal. Open on Sai slamming both cards face-up on the table at once (motion and sound), straight into the first duel ("Speed or perfection?"). No slow ramp.

Visual hook: the two cards slammed face-up at once.

Style: fast and energetic. ~3 seconds to choose each round (gut-honest answers). Genuine live reactions — Gray controls the pile, so Sai doesn't know what's next.

Camera shots: Sai at a table with a card pile. He holds up two cards, Gray reads them, Sai picks the winner and explains, discards the loser, draws a new card to challenge the champ. The champion sits in a throne slot, always on screen; discards pile up in a visible graveyard.

Graphics and effects: minimal — physical beats digital here. Optional on-screen label for the reigning champion.

Physical mechanic: physical cards, king-of-the-hill gauntlet (locked, signature). Throne slot for the champion, visible graveyard of discards, satisfying flick/toss on each loser.
