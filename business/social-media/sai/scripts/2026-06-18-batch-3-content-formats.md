# Sai — Batch 3 Content Formats (Sandcastles-sourced)

Revised lineup of 5 replicable short-form formats for Sai, each backed by a real
high-performing video analyzed on Sandcastles. Replaces the original 5 in
`../interviews/2026-06-17-replicable-formats.md` (kept #1 + #3, replaced #2/#4/#5).

## Voice rules (apply to every format)
- **Never put down other people, brands, or products.** No teardowns or react-and-mock. Reframe any "this is bad" angle into a positive one ("here's what makes this work").
- **Sai is NOT the all-knowing expert.** Write first-person and experience-based: "here's what's worked for us / what I'm still figuring out," never prescriptive "here's the rule / anybody can do this." Let him be wrong, admit uncertainty. Sharpest on the confident formats (#1, #3, #4).
- Avoid em-dashes in anything Sai says out loud (his voice).

## 1. Rapid-fire "keep or cut" — decisive judgment calls
Proven: @tommoneymays, 7.8M views, 1,464x outlier. Watch: https://tiktok.com/@tommoneymays/video/7644299725675564318 · Breakdown: https://app.sandcastles.ai/video/057c2c7e-992d-4ac6-b05c-dd534a824408
- Format: punchy, rapid-fire business judgment calls.
- Sai's version: "good client vs bad client," "keep this ad or kill it," "hire or pass." Humble framing: "here's my gut after a lot of reps, you might see it differently."

## 2. Isolate-one-variable demo — change one thing, show the impact
Proven: @askvinh "the way you speak changes how people listen," 30M views, 202x. Watch: https://www.youtube.com/shorts/6fHoN6MR6MI · Breakdown: https://app.sandcastles.ai/video/44cdf0d5-9618-451f-9756-4ea5e2bbdf78
- Format: hold everything constant, change ONE variable, prove its impact through direct comparison (control → isolate → combine).
- Sai's version: the **A/B ad test reveal**. "We ran the same ad twice and changed only the first 3 seconds. Watch what happened." Show version A, version B, and the real result. Data does the talking (keeps him humble); every test he runs is another video.

## 3. "Never say these words" — sharp do/don't list
Proven: @richard_hale_ "never say these words in business," 6.5M views, 125x. Watch: https://www.instagram.com/reel/DYxT-zQzMjm/ · Breakdown: https://app.sandcastles.ai/video/cf1b06bb-e9c5-4f00-9740-ffe2031ae9ac
- Format: a tight list of exact phrases/mistakes. Very saveable + shareable (Sai's priority).
- Sai's version: frame as HIS own mistakes, not commands. "Phrases I had to train myself out of." Topic bank:
  - Phrases I had to stop saying to clients
  - 5 words that quietly kill a marketing campaign
  - Words that make an ad sound like an ad
  - Phrases that used to make clients ghost me
  - Words I cut from every hook (they tank retention)
  - Things I stopped writing in ad copy
  - Words that made my brand forgettable
  - Phrases that made me sound junior

## 4. Value-first give-away — one genuinely useful thing, no pitch
Proven: @edward.builds "the easiest marketing hack," 10.5M views, 495x. Watch: https://www.instagram.com/reel/DXs4k6nD3V6/ · Breakdown: https://app.sandcastles.ai/video/9a9c6da5-1404-41ef-8461-883ac575cd58
- Format: give ONE specific, do-it-today thing; full value lives IN the video; no "comment a keyword" bait.
- Sai's version: **give away a real piece of his actual process.** "Here's the exact checklist we run every ad through before it goes live." Walk through the real thing on screen. Humble: "this isn't a secret, it's just what we do." Variations: the 3 questions before a shoot, how they use AI to research/script, etc.
