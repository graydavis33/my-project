## SHORTS

### Keep (working)
- **Script the day before, film everything in one day.** Sai's favorite part of the process ("I love that"). Same for editing — film one day, edit one day. Keep this principle; batching efficiency is *not* the current bottleneck, don't over-optimize it yet.
- **The scripts themselves are good** — beyond format, he wouldn't change the writing.

### Dislike (problems)
- **Everything looks too similar.** Scripts "all seem very similar to each other," graphics are "the same thing over and over." His IG page "looks really blank" / samey. **This is the #1 problem.**
- **Couldn't build scripts off "review the week that just happened."** That ideation method stalled — needs a better front-end.
- **TikTok underperforms** (<1k views) while IG + YouTube reliably clear. His read: TikTok is the most personal/community-driven platform (people want raw "see you," not produced), IG is value/tips/stories-driven. Don't chase all three equally.

### Change / build
- **Format variety is the whole game.** Move beyond static talking-head. Wants a rotating menu of structures:
  - S-tier / A-tier / B-tier rankings
  - "Choose between X or Y" (Sharran-style: *"What's more important, the media buying or the audience?"*)
  - Reply-to-comment
  - Comparison topics
  - Different physical setups: subway/holding the phone, green-screen pointing at stuff, walking, etc.
- **More tips / tricks / hacks content** — "very saveable, very shareable." Lean into saveable/shareable value.
- **Instagram is the priority platform** ("biggest leverage, without a doubt"). Changing formats helps most there.
- **New ideation workflow** (this is the big process change):
  1. **Gray takes notes at the end of every day** on interesting moments worth talking about (Gray is with Sai all day = context nobody else has).
  2. **Gray builds the FORMAT/STRUCTURE + suggests topics** for the week (~7 videos). Sai writes the actual script / what he says.
  3. Lives in **Notion**: a weekly page of the 7 formats + topics + shot/location/theme ideas.
  4. Sai reviews it morning-of or night-before — checks off what he likes, swaps topics he doesn't. Ideal UX: a **printed sheet he checks off**, or laptop ready with the week's ideas for a quick back-and-forth. Minimize headspace required from Sai.
- **He wants the AI to prompt him** the way the LinkedIn tool does — guided questions that pull the script out of him. Build that for shorts.
- **Competitor/trend research:** keep a list of ~10 IG accounts to study formats they're testing. Sandcastles lets you pin specific accounts (Sai: "Sweet") — Gray already has a competitor list. Feed format ideas from this.
- **Content-type variety on IG:** mix talking-head shorts + carousels + stories.

### Stories (new, IG)
- Sai wants help **posting stories — ~3/day.** Personal, behind-the-scenes, show his face, relatable (e.g. *"couldn't script yesterday — I'm not a machine"*).
- **Polls + questions** to drive engagement.

### Metrics
- For shorts, **Sai only cares about retention.** "In shorts metrics I don't give a fuck... just retention." Wants an at-a-glance retention view across all videos, **flag outliers**, study why they worked, repeat it.

## LONG-FORM

### Keep (working)
- **Keep the RAW long-form** — definitely keeping it. Easy to produce (just film your day), tightly correlated to the business, on-brand, true to who he is. ("beat fiesta" concept stays.)
- Regardless of views, it's a **diary of his life on video** — that alone justifies it. Also inspiring for internal team + recruiting (can retarget ads to it).
- **Transition shots — loves them, wants MORE.** Specifically: when attention starts to drift, a transition shot travels to a new location *showing the journey there* → feels like a new arc, "transported to a different world."

### Dislike (problems)
- **This week's long-form came out "more of a tutorial than a story."** He does NOT want tutorial. He's been merging Netflix-reality-TV and "Alex-from-Arizona tutorial" — wants to drop the tutorial side. **People should learn the lesson THROUGH the story.**
- **Storyboarding is weak** — wishes they did it much better.
- Not happy with the A-roll he wrote this week ("wasn't that great").

### Change / build
- **Story arc is the driver.** Build every video on a clear arc: rising action → conflict/climax → character arc → resolution. Make it feel like a **Netflix film / documentary**, not a how-to.
- **Physically map the arc before recording the A-roll.** Whiteboard + sticky notes (or laptop with a clip per moment): take every plot point from the week (~20), place each onto its spot on the arc, watch/reorganize until it's right. **Don't record the A-roll until the arc is mapped** — the A-roll dictates how the story is told.
- **One pure story per video.** (Last week's = "recruiting creators.") Build everything off that single spine; the rest are supporting clips between. Also drives the title + thumbnail.
- **Title + thumbnail FIRST** — before recording the A-roll, not a last step. ("Totally agree," said many times.)
- **Organize footage as the week goes** — build the timeline/storyboard progressively instead of dumping it all at the end.
- **Cadence: post every ~2 weeks, using 2 weeks of raw footage** (more to work with, more precise).
- **No hard 8-min cap** — if it's engaging, longer is fine.
- **Film the A-roll interview the same day as the batch videos (Sunday)** — so by then you have the week's raw footage + Sunday's A-roll together.
- **Open/parked:** the other editor's long-form — maybe just keep those as **pods** (podcast), possibly post with minimal edits. Caveat: needs strong thumbnails or there's no point posting. Undecided.

## Cross-cutting themes
1. **Variety** is the recurring demand (formats, graphics, content types) — most acute on shorts.
2. **Story over tutorial** on long-form; **format menu over single template** on shorts.
3. **Front-load the creative decisions** — title/thumbnail and story arc (long-form), format + topics (shorts) — before filming.
4. **Gray owns structure + context; Sai owns the script/words.** Minimize Sai's headspace cost (printed checklists, guided prompts).
5. **Instagram is the priority platform. Retention is the only shorts metric that matters.**
