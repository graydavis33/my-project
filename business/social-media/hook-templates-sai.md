# Hook Templates — Sai's Niche (2026 Edition)

**Audience:** founders, entrepreneurs, agency owners, gen z builders, hardworking 18–28 year olds chasing the founder path.
**Identity:** 21-year-old founder running an ad agency. Discipline-coded, schedule-coded, anti-hustle-culture-but-pro-grind.

**Voice rules baked in (from `feedback-sai-linkedin-voice.md`):**
- "Founder" never "CEO"
- No dunking on peer founders — attack ideas, markets, or your past self
- No AI-flavored summary closers
- Keep concrete details (Q3 last year, 30%, real ritual names)

---

## Why This File Was Cut Down (2026-05-26)

Previous version had 50 verbal hooks and 20 visual hooks. The list ran two problems:
1. **Too long to actually use** — agents and humans drifted to templates 1–10 and ignored the rest
2. **Half the templates peaked in 2022–2023 and don't hit anymore** — POV formats, "stop doing X right now" commands, "5 things I wish I knew" listicles, "hot take:" prefaces, countdown gimmicks. Viewers see the engineering now.

Kept the ~25% that still land in 2026 + added new patterns that observable winners (and Sai's own voice memos) are using. **Total now: 12 verbal + 5 visual.**

Patterns explicitly retired are at the bottom under **AVOID — 2023-fatigued**.

## VERBAL HOOKS — 2026 Survivors + Replacements

| # | Template | Sai Example | Why it works in 2026 |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | "This is the [SUPERLATIVE] thing I've [VERB] [TIMEFRAME]." | "This is the single greatest hack I've made in my life this year." | Personal superlative + present-tense ownership. Viewers trust real conviction over engineered hot takes. *(Pulled directly from Sai's 2026-05-26 voice memo — proven in his voice.)* |
| 2 | "My [SPECIFIC FAILURE] failed because [REASON]. Here's what I learned." | "My first hire failed because I had no systems. Here's what I learned." | Failure-first opener disarms skeptics + sets up the framework. Beats success-flex openers. |
| 3 | "I used to [PAST BEHAVIOR]. I flipped it to [NEW BEHAVIOR]. [SPECIFIC RECEIPT]." | "I used to spend 90% of my time working and 10% planning. I flipped it. I've made more progress in my career than ever before." | Before/after WITH the receipt. The receipt sentence is what makes it work — without it, this is generic. |
| 4 | "When you're [STATE], you can usually backtrack it to [SOURCE]." | "When you're afraid of something, you can usually backtrack it to something." | Observation-as-hook. Frames a universal mental pattern, then drops Sai's story as evidence. From his own voice memo. |
| 5 | "I used to [BAD HABIT] until I realized [INSIGHT]." | "I used to chase the outcome until I realized inputs are the only thing that compounds." | Confession structure with a sharp pivot. Classic but evergreen. |
| 6 | "Here's how you know [DECISION POINT]: [SIGNAL]." | "Here's how you know it's time to scale your business. Your customers are happy AND your team is happy." | Direct instruction with implicit ownership. No "most founders…" punching down. |
| 7 | "[OUTCOME] doesn't come from [COMMON BELIEF]. It comes from [REAL THING]." | "Money doesn't come from working harder. It comes from picking better clients." | Reframe — the original belief gets named so viewers feel the snap. |
| 8 | "You're not [SURFACE PROBLEM]. You're [ACTUAL PROBLEM]." | "You're not unmotivated. You're under-scheduled." | Diagnosis hook. Empathy, then sharp. Doesn't punch down. |
| 9 | "Why I [DECISION] even though everyone said [OPPOSITE]." | "Why I scaled my business DOWN even though everyone said grow." | Courage hook + counter-narrative. Specific decision beats vague "hot take." |
| 10 | "If I had to start over, I'd [ACTION] first." | "If I had to start over, I'd learn finances before I learned sales." | Retrospective wisdom. Implies hard-won experience without explicitly bragging. |
| 11 | "[SPECIFIC NUMBER/DOLLAR]. That's [WHAT IT REPRESENTS]." | "Ten thousand dollars a month. That's what I was spending on ads in 2024 — and they stopped working." | Number-first stop-the-scroll. Replaces the dying "5 things I wish…" listicle pattern. |
| 12 | "I'll never [ACTION] again. Here's why." | "I'll never take a client without a retainer again. Here's why." | Finality + obligation to deliver the reason. Pulls saves and shares. |

## VISUAL HOOKS — 2026 Survivors

| # | Template | Sai Execution | Why it survives |
|---|---|---|---|
| V1 | Eye-Lock Close-Up | Tight, no cut, founder-stare. No background distraction. Best for confessions, frameworks, and superlatives. | The most direct viewer connection — gaining share in 2026 as overproduced content gets distrusted. |
| V2 | Unexpected Activity | Walking NYC streets, doing dishes, making coffee, eating breakfast — delivering the line deadpan while doing something mundane. | The "rawness" winner — Sai discussed exactly this format at the end of his 2026-05-26 voice memo. Keeps viewer attention without distracting from the lesson. |
| V3 | Walk-Toward-Camera | Walking out of the agency office or down a Midtown street, hits the mark, delivers the line. | Combines location energy + delivery confidence. Reads "going somewhere" — implicit forward motion. |
| V4 | Whisper-Lean | Lean slightly into camera, drop voice, deliver the line like a secret. | Highest-engagement opener for confessions, tactical reveals, and any "nobody tells you" content. |
| V5 | Number-On-Screen | Big text appears (e.g. "30%", "$10,000", "5 YEARS") full-screen behind Sai or as a cut-in. Reinforces the number that the script also says aloud. | Specificity = save-bait. Visual + verbal repetition of the same number doubles retention. |

## AVOID — 2023-Fatigued Patterns (Explicitly Retired)

These performed in 2022–2023 but viewers now read them as engineered. Don't draft scripts using these even when they "feel right" — they're scroll triggers in 2026.

- ❌ **"Stop [ACTION] right now"** — generic command, overused
- ❌ **"Nobody talks about [TOPIC]"** — viewers know people talk about it; reads as dishonest framing
- ❌ **"5 things I wish I knew before [MILESTONE]"** — listicle saturation; people scroll past number-headed hooks now
- ❌ **"Here's the truth about [TOPIC] nobody will tell you"** — too vague to commit, viewers don't trust it
- ❌ **"Most people get [TOPIC] completely wrong"** — punches down at the audience
- ❌ **"POV: you're [AGE] and you just [MILESTONE]"** — POV format peaked early 2023, viewers screen it out
- ❌ **"Watch this before you [ACTION]"** — clickbait-coded, suppresses CTR now
- ❌ **"Hot take: [OPINION]"** — explicitly meta phrasing; viewers see the engineering
- ❌ **"Day [N] of [HABIT]"** — only works if Sai actually commits to a numbered journey AND the milestone is visible (otherwise it's filler)
- ❌ Countdown overlays ("3… 2… 1…")
- ❌ Destruction visuals (tearing things up)
- ❌ Split-screen "wrong vs right" (peaked 2022)
- ❌ Pull-out-of-frame "reveals"

## AVOID — Voice Rules (From Sai's Own Revisions)

These came from Sai editing his AI-generated drafts. Don't reintroduce them.

- ❌ "Most founders fail because…" — punches down at peers (voice rule)
- ❌ "CEO" anywhere — Sai is "founder," always
- ❌ Cute parenthetical asides ("yes, really", "spoiler: it didn't work") — AI-flavored
- ❌ "The lesson is…" / "What this comes down to is…" closers — synthesized energy, not native
- ❌ Generic "hustle harder" / grindset language — Sai is discipline-coded, not hustle-coded
- ❌ "For a [role], that's a [thing]" identity-wrapped closers
- ❌ Three-beat "no X, no Y, no Z" — cap at 2 beats with intensifier ("definitely", "honestly")
- ❌ Dropping the verb in callbacks — "Before I started **meditating**" not "Before I started"
- ❌ Invented copywriter specifics — every number, every detail, must come from Sai's actual words

## When To Add To This File

Add a hook ONLY if:
1. It's appeared in 2+ winning Sai videos (cross-check `winning-patterns.md`), OR
2. It's appeared in 3+ winning outlier videos from creators in adjacent niches (founder vlogs, agency owners, gen-z business creators)

Don't add hooks because they "feel like Sai" — only because they've actually performed. The file stays at ~12 verbal + ~5 visual unless something earns its slot.
