# Shorts Hook Bank

A swipe file of every verbatim hook example pulled from the 8 reference videos, organized by type. When you sit down to write a short, do not start from scratch. Open this doc, find a hook in the slot you need, and adapt the structure to your topic (videography, editing, business advice for editors). The wording is the steal-worthy part. The topic is yours.

## How to use this
Grab the closest-fit hook, swap in your own subject (editing/video/clients), and run it through the 3-Hook Stack (verbal + written + visual) before you record.

## Hook types

---

### 1. Contrarian / pattern-break (Everyone says X, but actually Y)

**What it is:** You name the common belief in your niche, then immediately punch a hole in it. The viewer's brain locks because their existing model just got challenged.

**Formula:** `[Common belief everyone holds] BUT [opposite/surprising truth]` — connector words to memorize: **but, however, yet, although, therefore, on the other hand** (from 04-irresistible-hooks, Kallaway's "Scroll Stop Interjection").

**Verbatim examples:**

1. "Today we're talking about hooks. If you want your videos to perform better you have to make better hooks. But here's the thing — I'm not going to give you a list of 25 proven viral hooks, cuz that's not what you need." *(04-irresistible-hooks — self-applied to his own opener)*
2. "The tech in the Vegas sphere is insane — biggest screen ever built, 20 times bigger than an IMAX. But get this: the screen is actually the least impressive part of the whole thing. Because the most impressive part is the audio." *(04-irresistible-hooks, 8M-view example)*
3. "Now most people think it's because of high interest rates, but it turns out it has nothing to do with that. Because the biggest waste of money is actually coming from…" *(04-irresistible-hooks, real estate B2B example)*
4. "Most editors think it's because of his camera gear. But it turns out his entire edge comes from one boring 90-minute habit he does every Monday morning." *(Kallaway 3-step formula applied to Gray's niche, from 04 apply-notes)*
5. "Jaguar's rebrand is one of the worst design blunders in the history of the world." *(05-10x-faster, Contrarian hook format example)*
6. "I left [the hook] for last because everybody always talks about the hook first." *(02-master-storyteller — flips listener expectation)*
7. "This is the measure twice, cut once of content." *(01-killer-script — flips "just start writing" with carpenter discipline)*
8. "Sitting down and talking videos are boring. Okay, that worked in 2022, y'all." *(07-killer-short — punches the dominant short-form format)*
9. "A lot of people just copy other people word for word." *(06-killer-script-2026 — setup for "do it smarter" contrarian flip)*
10. "Never throw spaghetti at the wall." *(07-killer-short and 08-irresistible-hooks — Ava's anti-creativity rallying cry)*

### 2. Curiosity gap / open loop (I did X for Y days and here is what happened)

**What it is:** Open a question in the viewer's brain and refuse to close it until they keep watching. The dopamine of "wait, what?" carries them through the first 5–10 seconds.

**Formula:** `[Action / experiment / setup that implies a hidden outcome]` — leave the outcome unstated for at least one beat. From 04-irresistible-hooks: "The deeper the loop, the more curious you are, the more I've hooked you."

**Verbatim examples:**

1. "Today, we're going to cover step by step how you can get the same results with a strategy I bet you never heard of before." *(06-killer-script-2026)*
2. "Did you notice anything similar about these two videos?" *(06-killer-script-2026 — forces active looking)*
3. "What do these successful creators know that you don't?" *(08-irresistible-hooks — explicit insider-knowledge gap)*
4. "But Ava, what is a viral outlier hook? I got you." *(08-irresistible-hooks — self-asked open loop)*
5. "This is how you just find insane outliers that you never would have found unless you did this." *(08-irresistible-hooks)*
6. "When I was in college, I used this sleep technique to only have to sleep 3 hours per night." *(05-10x-faster, Personal Experience hook format)*
7. "I'm going to do a magic trick — because I used the same psychology formula on you for the hook in this video, and if you're still watching, that proves it worked." *(04-irresistible-hooks)*
8. "There is a critical first piece that you have to understand and this alone will level up your script writing in a massive massive way." *(01-killer-script — Kallaway opens a loop he closes 2 minutes later)*
9. "This is deep deep deep psychology that you will not hear anywhere else on YouTube." *(01-killer-script)*
10. "The black magic that makes all this stuff really work." *(01-killer-script)*
11. "600 days ago…" / "6 years ago…" / "A year ago…" *(08-irresistible-hooks — time-stamped open loop template)*
12. "This is a picture of my first date ever with this guy named Ben." *(08-irresistible-hooks — implies a story has to follow)*

### 3. Listicle / number-led (3 things that..., 7 reasons...)

**What it is:** A specific, finite number creates a closed container for the viewer's attention — their brain wants to complete the set. Numbers also signal "this is structured, not a ramble."

**Formula:** `[Specific number] [thing they want]` — odd numbers (3, 5, 7) tend to feel more "authored" than rounded ones (10, 20).

**Verbatim examples:**

1. "I've studied literally thousands of videos. And it turns out if your scripts are not working, there's three massive mistakes you might be making." *(03-cant-stop-watching)*
2. "These are going to be the six best ways to improve your hooks immediately." *(04-irresistible-hooks)*
3. "This is the proven five-step framework for how to write killer scripts that keep viewers hooked." *(01-killer-script)*
4. "Six favorite storytelling techniques that will massively improve your content." *(02-master-storyteller)*
5. "This is the four-step blueprint for building a script writing system that will help you make videos 10 times faster." *(05-10x-faster)*
6. "Every short consists of seven different factors." *(07-killer-short)*
7. "These are the five best Premiere Pro editing templates you can use for free." *(05-10x-faster, List hook format)*
8. "Three levels of tomato pasta." / "Three levels of cookies." / "Three levels of cookies / tomato pasta / mountains / skincare." *(06-killer-script-2026 and 08-irresistible-hooks — "three levels of X" is a universal viral template)*
9. "There are three massive mistakes people are making with their mortgage. The average person pays an extra $122,000 per year." *(04-irresistible-hooks)*
10. "You can turn one video idea into seven videos because of what we call angles." *(07-killer-short)*

### 4. Pain / problem lead (If you are struggling with X...)

**What it is:** Name your target viewer's exact pain in sentence one. Anyone with that pain stays. Anyone without it leaves — which is fine, they were never your viewer.

**Formula:** `If you [pain/struggle], [here is what fixes it]` — or `[Pain stated as fact about "most people"]`.

**Verbatim examples:**

1. "If you struggle with acne, this is one obvious solution that will clear it right up." *(05-10x-faster, Problem hook format)*
2. "If your scripts are not working, there's three massive mistakes you might be making. If you solve for these three things, your retention and views will skyrocket immediately." *(03-cant-stop-watching)*
3. "The reason is you don't have a system for converting raw ideas into winning scripts." *(05-10x-faster)*
4. "When it comes to content, most people really struggle with script writing hooks and storytelling." *(05-10x-faster)*
5. "Most people that make content are not getting to the value or the mention of value quick enough before the viewer bounces." *(03-cant-stop-watching)*
6. "If people churn on the hook, the rest of what I talked about doesn't really matter." *(02-master-storyteller)*
7. "If you want clients to actually book you…" *(04 apply-to-Gray — benefit-led-as-pain for editor/videographer audience)*
8. "If you want your wedding edits to feel cinematic…" *(04 apply-to-Gray)*
9. "If you're like me, you probably feel like the AI writing just doesn't hit." *(05-10x-faster)*
10. "Making content is actually pretty hard for smart people with big vocabularies because they struggle to dumb themselves down." *(03-cant-stop-watching — names the pain of the smart audience specifically)*

### 5. Benefit lead (How to X without Y)

**What it is:** State the outcome the viewer wants, frame it as achievable without the obstacle they fear. From 04-irresistible-hooks: "Even if someone is against [your method], they will likely wait and hear you out because their desire to solve the pain is so strong."

**Formula:** `If you want [outcome], you need [unexpected method]` — or `How to [outcome] without [common obstacle]`.

**Verbatim examples:**

1. "If you want better sleep you need to be taking magnesium." *(04-irresistible-hooks — the gold-standard benefit lead)*
2. "If you want your content to perform better you need to learn how to tell better stories." *(02-master-storyteller)*
3. "If you want your videos to perform better you have to make better hooks." *(04-irresistible-hooks)*
4. "If you want to learn how to make content faster, this is going to be one of the most helpful videos you ever watch." *(05-10x-faster)*
5. "I'm going to show you how to create killer short form content that will actually get you meaningful results like this, this, and this." *(07-killer-short)*
6. "In this video, you will learn how to go from blank page to fully optimized killer short." *(07-killer-short)*
7. "Here's the easiest way to use ChatGPT to create content." *(05-10x-faster, Education hook format)*
8. "If you want to achieve X, don't do Y — do Z." *(04-irresistible-hooks — Kallaway's universal phrasing wrapper, fill-in template)*
9. "Today, we're going to cover step by step how you can get the same results…" *(06-killer-script-2026)*
10. "Here's the framework in 60 seconds." *(implied template from 04-irresistible-hooks compress-speed-to-value section)*

### 6. Question hooks (Why do X?, What if...?)

**What it is:** Ask a direct question that implants curiosity in the viewer's head. The brain involuntarily tries to answer — and stays watching to verify or learn the real answer.

**Formula:** `[Direct question that the viewer cannot immediately answer]` — works best when the question itself is provocative or counterintuitive.

**Verbatim examples:**

1. "Can you bake sourdough bread in under an hour using only household ingredients?" *(05-10x-faster, Question hook format)*
2. "What do these successful creators know that you don't?" *(08-irresistible-hooks)*
3. "Did you notice anything similar about these two videos?" *(06-killer-script-2026)*
4. "Is it possible to [outcome] in [timeframe] even though [obstacle]? Yes." *(06-killer-script-2026 — storytelling structure template — e.g., "Is it possible to color-grade a wedding video in 20 minutes even though you've never used DaVinci? Yes.")*
5. "Is it possible…" *(08-irresistible-hooks — fill-in template)*
6. "Did you know if you…" / "Did you know that if…" *(08-irresistible-hooks — fill-in template)*
7. "What's the difference between a beginner creator versus an experienced one." *(05-10x-faster, Comparison hook format)*
8. "How would you order those facts so they would understand it the most?" *(05-10x-faster — invokes the "tell it to a friend" frame)*
9. "Why does this work?" *(02-master-storyteller — Kallaway uses this as a mid-script re-hook, also works as an opener)*
10. "But Ava, what is a viral outlier hook?" *(08-irresistible-hooks)*

### 7. Data / proof / credibility (I studied 7356 hooks, Here is what 100 viral videos have in common)

**What it is:** Drop oddly specific numbers in the first sentence. The specificity itself feels researched. Round numbers feel guessed; "7,356" feels like someone actually counted.

**Formula:** `I [studied/analyzed/built] [specific large number] [things] and [what I found]` — or `[Specific outcome] in [specific timeframe]`.

**Verbatim examples:**

1. "I studied over 7,356 viral short form content videos, took 117 pages of notes, and have created hundreds of viral short form content videos for myself and clients." *(07-killer-short — three oddly-specific numbers in one sentence)*
2. "I'm Kallaway, I've done over a billion views, I have a million followers, and this stuff is all I do all day long." *(01-killer-script)*
3. "I've analyzed hundreds of the top creators across every niche and they all run the exact same script writing process." *(01-killer-script)*
4. "I've studied literally thousands of videos. And it turns out if your scripts are not working, there's three massive mistakes you might be making." *(03-cant-stop-watching)*
5. "I grew my Instagram account from this to this not once but twice along with multiple different client accounts in multiple different niches." *(06-killer-script-2026)*
6. "I have almost a million followers, billions of views, and these are the secrets for how I go viral whenever I want." *(04-irresistible-hooks)*
7. "Alex Hormosi just sold $100 million worth of books in 3 days using this five-step method." *(05-10x-faster, Case Study hook format)*
8. "The average person pays an extra $122,000 per year." *(04-irresistible-hooks — specific dollar amount as proof anchor)*
9. "And the video got 40 million views in 5 days." *(05-10x-faster — Iron Man / Russian engineer case study)*
10. "It's the same playbook I've personally used to generate billions of views and over a million followers." *(05-10x-faster)*

**Anti-pattern note:** Specific numbers should be real. If you say "I studied 7,356 videos" you actually have to have done that. Faking specificity damages trust if anyone calls you on it.

### 8. Speed-to-value (Here is the framework in 60 seconds)

**What it is:** Promise compression. The viewer knows their time is being respected — they get the full lesson, fast. From 04-irresistible-hooks: "60 minutes of script writing work distilled down into 60 seconds — that's what we're going for."

**Formula:** `[Big thing] in [short timeframe]` — or `[Whole transformation/workflow] in this [short video]`.

**Verbatim examples:**

1. "In this video, you will learn how to go from blank page to fully optimized killer short." *(07-killer-short)*
2. "60 minutes of script writing work distilled down into 60 seconds — that's what we're going for." *(04-irresistible-hooks)*
3. "This is the four-step blueprint for building a script writing system that will help you make videos 10 times faster." *(05-10x-faster)*
4. "This was a master class on how to write scripts 10 times faster." *(05-10x-faster — outro version of speed-to-value)*
5. "Once you have the hook format chosen, the shocking facts pulled, and the story structure outline decided, it's really just about layering the facts in order, and adding transitions to smooth it out. That's really all you have to do to write a banger script." *(05-10x-faster)*
6. "Is it possible to edit a YouTube video in under an hour?" *(08 apply-to-Gray template, fitted to editor niche)*
7. "Edit 5 clips in 5 minutes." *(07 apply-to-Gray — challenge angle applied to color grading)*
8. "Three levels of color grading." *(08 apply-to-Gray)*
9. "This is the proven five-step framework for how to write killer scripts that keep viewers hooked." *(01-killer-script)*
10. "After watching this I guarantee you will think differently about the way you write scripts and make videos." *(02-master-storyteller — guarantee + outcome promise compresses payoff)*

### 9. Time / urgency (Watch this before X)

**What it is:** Implies that the information has a shelf life or that there is a window the viewer is about to miss. Use sparingly — overused, it reads as clickbait.

**Formula:** `[Watch this/Do this] before [event/deadline/loss]` — or time-anchored callouts like "in 2026" / "in the next 12 months."

**Verbatim examples:**

1. "How to Write a Killer Script in 2026" *(06-killer-script-2026 — title-level time anchor that implies "old advice is stale")*
2. "Today we're talking about writing killer scripts." *(05-10x-faster — soft "today" framing)*
3. "Sitting down and talking videos are boring. Okay, that worked in 2022, y'all." *(07-killer-short — explicitly date-stamps the obsolete approach)*
4. "When you make a piece of content you should feel like you have a timer counting down in your head before that content explodes and no one pays attention to it." *(04-irresistible-hooks — urgency as scripting principle)*
5. "The first 3 seconds of your video will determine the results on your entire short." *(08-irresistible-hooks — implicit time-urgency stakes)*
6. "The first point in the body — the first thing they hear after the intro — has to be amazing. It has to be so good that it blows their mind." *(01-killer-script — urgency framed at the script-craft level)*
7. "Nobody else has given away this kind of stuff for free." *(05-10x-faster — implies "before someone gates it" urgency)*
8. "It only takes one video for you to completely change your channel." *(08-irresistible-hooks — implies the next video could be the one, watch now)*
9. "Watch this before your next short flops." *(template, derived from 04-irresistible-hooks anti-fluff framing)*
10. "Anything you make after they click off might as well be a black screen, cuz they're not going to see it." *(04-irresistible-hooks — stakes/urgency about the hook itself)*

### 10. Visual / on-screen text hooks (the hook lives in the on-screen text, not the spoken word)

**What it is:** A 3–5 word phrase on screen in big bold font that does the actual hooking. The viewer's eyes hit it before their ears process the audio. From 04-irresistible-hooks: "Visual hooks are probably 100 times more powerful than just spoken word hooks." From 02-master-storyteller: "People's eyes perceive faster than their ears can hear."

**Formula:** `[3–5 word bold on-screen phrase]` paired with `[Spoken hook that complements, does not duplicate]` — and ideally `[Subtle on-screen motion]`.

**Verbatim examples (the on-screen text itself):**

1. "Life-Size Floor Plans" *(04-irresistible-hooks — 15M-view example. Note: the spoken version was "Future of Home Design" because the term "life-size floor plans" was unfamiliar — pre-translate jargon for the audio.)*
2. "Future of Home Design" *(04-irresistible-hooks — the bridging phrase used as the spoken hook to introduce an unfamiliar term)*
3. "Hairstyle neckline guide" *(08-irresistible-hooks — paired with verbal "This is the best hairstyle for different tops")*
4. "How to dress for your proportions" *(08-irresistible-hooks — paired with verbal "How to dress for your body type")*
5. "Jeans for different body types" *(08-irresistible-hooks — paired with three on-screen clones in labeled jeans)*
6. "I Studied 7,356 HOOKS" *(08-irresistible-hooks — title/thumbnail data hook)*
7. "Three levels of [color grade]" *(08 apply-to-Gray — fill-in template)*
8. "Smart editor vs dumb editor" *(08 apply-to-Gray — fill-in template, plus visual of two side-by-side editor caricatures)*
9. "$40K per wedding" *(04 apply-to-Gray — on-screen number as the visual stop)*
10. "60-min edit in 6 minutes" *(template, fits 04-irresistible-hooks Compress Speed to Value tactic)*

**Visual hook motion principle (04-irresistible-hooks):** "Too much motion and you're going to overwhelm them. Too little motion and you're going to bore them. You need just enough motion." Examples cited: GRWM creator leaning forward/back while putting hair up; Kevin from Epic Gardening holding a vivid red strawberry up to the camera before speaking; Colin & Samir's Ray-Ban Meta clip (60M views) opens on subtle hand motion.

**Visual hook freeze-frame principle (02-master-storyteller):** Start on the most visually striking frame possible. Kevin/Epic Gardening shows the vivid red strawberry before saying a single word — the eye is hooked before the audio even starts.

## Hook formulas (fill-in-the-blank templates)

### Formula 1 — The 3-Step Hook (Kallaway, from 04-irresistible-hooks)

Three lines. Roughly 4 seconds total for a short.

1. **Context Lean** — `Today we're talking about [TOPIC]. If you want [BENEFIT/SOLVE PAIN] you need to [DESIRED OUTCOME].`
2. **Scroll Stop Interjection** — `But here's the thing — [PATTERN BREAK].` *(use one connector: but, however, yet, although, therefore, on the other hand)*
3. **Contrarian Snapback** — `[The actual surprising answer that goes opposite of where the viewer expected].`

**Worked Gray example (editor niche):**
> Today we're talking about color grading. If you want your wedding edits to look cinematic you need a $500 LUT pack. **But here's the thing** — the top wedding editors I've studied don't use LUTs at all. **They use one free Premiere Pro setting** that takes 4 clicks to dial in.

---

### Formula 2 — The 5-Part Intro Hook (Kallaway, from 01-killer-script)

Best for sit-down talking-head and the opening 30s of long-form documentary. Compresses into 8–15 seconds for shorts.

1. **Immediate context (click confirm)** — Sentence one tells the viewer exactly what the video is about, confirming the title.
2. **Common belief** — Restate what most people in the niche believe.
3. **Contrarian take** — Flip the common belief with your actual position.
4. **Proof** — Authority stack (years of experience, results, client count, view count).
5. **Plan** — Promise the structure of what's coming ("the 3-step framework for…").

> Parts 4 and 5 can swap order. Either pattern works.

**Worked Gray example (sit-down talking head):**
> Today we're talking about how to land your first paid videography client. *(immediate context)* Most editors think you have to build a Behance portfolio or cold-DM 100 people a week. *(common belief)* But it turns out the fastest path is the exact opposite — you cold-call 10 local businesses by phone in one Tuesday afternoon. *(contrarian take)* I've helped 4 of my buddies do this and three of them booked their first client inside 2 weeks. *(proof)* So in this video I'm going to walk you through the exact 3-step phone-call script. *(plan)*

---

### Formula 3 — The Universal Phrasing Wrapper (Kallaway, from 04-irresistible-hooks)

When you can't think of a hook, default to this. It always works — *if* Z actually delivers.

> **If you want to achieve [X], don't do [Y], do [Z].**

Filled in for Gray:
> If you want your wedding edits to look cinematic, don't buy a LUT pack, change your project frame rate to 23.976 before you cut.
> If you want clients to actually book you, don't post your portfolio, post a 30-second case study of how you cut their competitor's last video.

## Hook stack tactic (PBL/Ava — verbal + written + visual together)

From 07-killer-short and 08-irresistible-hooks: **every short should have all three hooks landing inside the first 3 seconds.** One hook is amateur. Three hooks stacked is professional.

| Layer | What it is | Where it lives |
|---|---|---|
| **Verbal hook** | What the viewer HEARS in the first 3 seconds | The voiceover / spoken first line |
| **Written hook** | What the viewer READS in the first 3 seconds | Bold on-screen text (3–5 words, big font) |
| **Visual hook** | What the viewer SEES in the first 3 seconds | The opening frame: subject, prop, motion, color, composition |

**Rule:** "The more hooks you use in your video, the bigger chance of winning." *(08-irresistible-hooks)*

**Worked Ava example (styling niche, from 08-irresistible-hooks):**
- Written: "Hairstyle neckline guide"
- Verbal: "This is the best hairstyle for different tops"
- Visual: Ava cloned three times on screen, each clone in a different neckline

**Worked Gray example (editor niche, applied):**
- Written: "Smart editor vs dumb editor"
- Verbal: "This is the difference between a beginner colorist and a paid one."
- Visual: Split-screen — same clip, one ungraded, one cinematic — with subtle zoom-in on the bad side

**The three should reinforce each other, NOT duplicate.** If the verbal hook says "Hairstyle neckline guide" and the written hook says "Hairstyle neckline guide," you've wasted a hook slot. Use the three layers to compress more information into 3 seconds than spoken word alone could carry.

**Hook stack discipline (07-killer-short):** Write the verbal, written, and visual hook BEFORE you write the script body. The three-hook stack is the gate that decides whether you record at all.

## Anti-patterns (hook moves that kill retention)

The references warn against these explicitly. Avoid all of them.

1. **"Wait till you see this" / "You're never going to believe this."** *(02-master-storyteller)* — These are not hooks. They are throat-clearing. "Your first line should be as punchy and as indicative of the plot as possible." Get to the point.

2. **Throat-clearing intros: "What's up guys," intro music, "before we get started…"** *(02-master-storyteller)* — Kallaway is "into the topic by word 4." Zero greeting. Zero ramp.

3. **Leading with résumé before pain/promise.** *(03-cant-stop-watching)* — Kallaway "doesn't lead with credentials; he leads with the pain and the promise, then drops credentials as a closer. This is the inverse of the bad-creator pattern of opening with résumé." Sequence: PAIN → PROMISE → PROOF. Not PROOF → PAIN → PROMISE.

4. **Listicle without psychology.** *(04-irresistible-hooks)* — "I'm not going to give you a list of 25 proven viral hooks, cuz that's not what you need. What you need is to understand the psychology behind why those hooks worked." If you give listicles without the WHY, viewers can't generalize and they churn.

5. **Big vocab and complicated sentences.** *(03-cant-stop-watching)* — "Big words are only valuable to the people that know them." "People do not feel good when they feel dumb." Target sixth-grade reading level. Use "or simple" inline to translate jargon ("This is rudimentary — or simple — color theory").

6. **Fluffy value with no measurable action.** *(07-killer-short)* — "Fluffy value is unclear and not specific. People do not know what to do with it after." If the viewer can't name the action you just taught, you didn't teach anything.

7. **Sit-down-and-talk format with no visual variety.** *(07-killer-short)* — "Sitting down and talking videos are boring. Okay, that worked in 2022, y'all." Rotate film formats: clone, green screen, multitasking, shot change every 2 seconds, voiceover, props/visual.

8. **Spaghetti-at-the-wall hook creativity.** *(07-killer-short and 08-irresistible-hooks)* — Don't invent. Steal from 5x outliers in your niche. "Never throw spaghetti at the wall."

9. **Trends/aesthetic/fluff for cheap viral wins.** *(07-killer-short)* — Will get views from viewers who do not care about you. Wrong followers = no leads = no business. "Make sure you are not recreating fluffy trends."

10. **Burying the value past the first body point.** *(01-killer-script)* — "The first point in the body — the first thing they hear after the intro — has to be amazing." Use the "second best first" cheat code: second-best point goes in slot one, best point in slot two, third-best in slot three.

11. **Story flow tangents / red herrings.** *(03-cant-stop-watching)* — "Adding extra unnecessary details is kind of like throwing barrels at someone that's trying to walk down the sidewalk." Audit every line: necessary context, or distraction?

12. **And-then storytelling (no causality).** *(02-master-storyteller, citing Matt Stone & Trey Parker)* — "If the words 'and then' belong between those beats, you got something pretty boring. What should happen between every beat is either the word 'therefore' or 'but.'"

13. **Hook that promises a payoff the body doesn't deliver.** *(04-irresistible-hooks)* — "If you don't actually have anything at the end of the rainbow, people would just churn and you're a fraud." The contrarian snapback has to actually be true and actually valuable.

14. **Visual hook with too much or too little motion.** *(04-irresistible-hooks)* — "Too much motion and you're going to overwhelm them. Too little motion and you're going to bore them. You need just enough motion." Goldilocks principle.

15. **Saying "link in bio."** *(07-killer-short)* — Replace with ManyChat trigger word. Comment-triggered DM doubles as algo fuel (more comments = more reach) AND captures the lead inside the platform.

16. **Run-on sentences in the hook.** *(03-cant-stop-watching)* — "You wouldn't speak in a run-on sentence, so don't write in a run-on sentence." Staccato. Short. Punchy. Then a longer one. Then short again.

17. **Pet/family humanizing beats that are too long.** *(06-killer-script-2026)* — Ava's "Isn't that right, Bearsy?" works because it's under 1 second. Lingering on the pet for 3+ seconds kills momentum. Keep humanizing beats sub-second.

18. **Common-belief stage with no flip.** *(01-killer-script)* — Naming the common belief is step 2 of the 5-part intro hook. If you state the common belief and DON'T then flip it with a contrarian take, you've just confirmed what the viewer already thought and given them no reason to stay.
