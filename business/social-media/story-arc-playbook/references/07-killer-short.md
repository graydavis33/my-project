# How to Make a Killer Short
**Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PG0X3SjEmfU · **Channel:** Personal Brand Launch · **Length:** 13:14 · **Views:** 36K · **Format:** Talking-head

## Why this video matters for our playbook
This is the most directly applicable reference in the entire library for Gray's shorts work — the entire 13 minutes is a tactical breakdown of how to build a viral short from blank page to upload. It introduces a 7-factor framework (topic, hooks, value, script angle, CTA, format, editing style), defines a measurable "5x rule" for finding outlier hooks to swipe, and catalogs 11 engaging film formats and 7 script angles Gray can rotate through. It also pre-empts common beginner traps (fluffy value, link-in-bio, sit-and-talk format).

## Key teaching points / frameworks (extracted verbatim where possible)
1. **The 7 Factors of a Viral Short** — every short consists of: (1) Topic, (2) Hooks (verbal + written + visual), (3) Value, (4) Script angle, (5) CTA, (6) Format, (7) Editing style.
2. **The Three Hooks Rule** — "The verbal hook, what you hear in the first three seconds. The written hook, what you read in the first three seconds. And the visual hook, what you see in the first three seconds." → Every short needs all three working together inside 3 seconds.
3. **The 5x Outlier Rule** — "I like videos to follow the 5x rule, meaning they have 5x views than that creator's following. That is how you truly know you have an outlier." Use only 5x outliers as your hook swipe file.
4. **Three Research Methods for Hooks** — (a) Creator research (find aligned creators, pull their top 5–10 videos using Sortfeed Chrome extension), (b) Keyword research (search topic in IG explore → reels tab), (c) Intentional scrolling of the Reels recommendation tab after methods 1 and 2 prime the algorithm.
5. **Research Platform Choice** — "I do all my research on Instagram for hooks simply because it typically is the hardest to go viral on Instagram with short form content. So if they did viral, you really know they're good." → Hardest platform = highest signal.
6. **Fluffy vs Measurable Value** — "Fluffy value is unclear and not specific. People do not know what to do with it after. Measurable value is very specific, actionable, and clear."
7. **The 7 Script Angles** — Tutorial, Comparison, Mythbust/Common Mistake, Do's vs Don't, Educational Tip/Hack, Transformation, Challenge. One topic × 7 angles = 7 distinct videos.
8. **Educational vs Storytelling Overlay** — Independent of angle, you can deliver as educational reel or as a story. Doubles the matrix (7 angles × 2 modes).
9. **No Link in Bio — Use ManyChat** — Specific tactic: trigger word in comments under 5–7 letters, auto-DM the link. Increases comment count (algo signal) AND captures lead.
10. **The 11 Film Formats** — Talking back-and-forth, Visual (props), Voiceover, Multitasking, Setting change, Shot/angle change (new shot every 2 sec), Clone (double yourself), Whiteboard, Q&A, Green screen, Reaction. "Sitting down and talking videos are boring. Okay, that worked in 2022."
11. **Editing Style Formula** — Choose once, reuse forever: 2–3 colors, 2–3 fonts (primary for hooks, secondary for captions, optional accent), 2–3 visual types (B-roll, screenshots, graphs, etc.).
12. **Order is Flexible** — "If you want to pick out your hooks before you determine your topic or you want to do your value before you find your hooks, that is totally fine. In fact, that's what I do most of the time."

## Shorts-specific structural insights
- **The 3-second triple-hook standard:** Verbal + written + visual hook all must land inside the first 3 seconds. Not one hook — three, simultaneously.
- **Hook research > hook creativity:** She never tells you to "be creative." She tells you to swipe from 5x outliers. The entire factor #2 section assumes you copy proven structures and refit them to your niche.
- **Outlier definition is measurable:** 5x views vs. follower count — gives Gray a concrete filter when building his own swipe file.
- **Instagram > TikTok/YouTube for hook research:** Instagram is hardest to go viral on, so an IG outlier is a higher-confidence signal than a TikTok outlier. Counterintuitive but valuable.
- **One idea → 14+ videos:** 7 angles × (educational OR storytelling) = at least 14 distinct shorts from one topic. This is the antidote to Gray's "repetitive content creation taking too long" pain point.
- **ManyChat over link-in-bio for shorts CTAs:** Comment-triggered DM. This doubles as algo fuel (more comments = more reach) AND lead capture. Gray should adopt this immediately.
- **Film format is a retention lever:** Eleven listed formats; the implication is that the *format itself* drives watch time at least as much as the script. Sit-and-talk is dead.
- **Editing style = decide once, execute forever:** Removes per-video decision fatigue and builds brand recognition (the "perfect recognizable editing style" creators).
- **Order is not sacred:** Hook → topic, or value → hook, both fine. Gray can find a banger hook first and reverse-engineer the topic.

## CTA / ending structure
The video ends with a soft values-driven close, not a hard sell. Sequence:
1. Permission to fail slowly: "While you can't ever guarantee results… the only way you do not fail is if you keep posting."
2. Receipts: personal account = 2 weeks to first hit, business page = months, clients = varies.
3. Integrity warning: don't post trends/aesthetic/fluff for cheap viral wins — you'll get followers who don't care about you.
4. Aspirational close: "These will get you the best quality followers who actually care about you."
5. Meta-honesty break: "Sorry, I didn't have it in my notes to say that, but I really do want to emphasize that…" — confessional aside that increases trust.
6. Soft CTA: "Thanks for watching and follow for more social media marketing education."

No ManyChat trigger word, no lead magnet — just a values close + follow ask. The video itself *is* the lead magnet (frameworks + tool walkthroughs).
