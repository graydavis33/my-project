# Story Arc Playbook

The single most-referenced doc for every script Gray writes — long-form documentary, sit-down talking head, or short. This is the opinionated synthesis of 9 elite-creator references (6 Kallaway, 2 Personal Brand Launch / Ava, 1 Tim Runia / story-driven documentary). Open this first. Pick the arc that fits your format. Then go.

## How to use this playbook

**When to open this doc:**
- You have a video idea and a blank page. Open this BEFORE you start writing.
- You finished a draft and want to audit it (use the section: "How to actually use this when writing a future script").
- You forgot which framework to use for a documentary vs. a short. The section "The story arc by format" tells you.

**How this connects to the rest of the folder:**
- `playbook.md` (this doc) = the master. The "why" + the "when to use what."
- `references/01-...md` through `references/09-...md` = the source breakdowns. Cite them in any future script doc as `from 04-irresistible-hooks`, etc. Go back to them when you want verbatim hook examples or deeper teaching from one specific creator. (`09-five-line-story-method` is the front-half / story-finding reference — the only story-driven-documentary source in the set.)

**Beginner translation:** Think of this doc as your *recipe book*. The references are *the chef's notebooks* you learned from. The templates are *the pre-cut ingredients*. When you sit down to write, you read this, grab the right template, and refer back to a reference only if you need the deep "why."

## The universal laws of scripts that hold attention

These are the things ALL 8 references agree on, regardless of creator or format. If a script breaks one of these, retention drops. No exceptions.

### Law 1 — Expectations vs. Reality (everything is a contract)

**What it means:** Every viewer clicks with an expectation set by your title/thumbnail/first-frame. Your script either beats that expectation or loses them. From Kallaway: *"When reality beats expectations and things go better than expected, people are happy, they keep watching, and you win. When expectations beat reality, they leave."*

**Which videos support it:** 01-killer-script (named "the master concept"), 03-cant-stop-watching ("speed-to-value problem"), 04-irresistible-hooks ("if you don't have anything at the end of the rainbow people would just churn and you're a fraud"), 06-killer-script-2026 ("shock score" = distance between what they know vs. what you tell them).

**Concrete example for Gray:** Title: "How a videographer made $40K/month editing weddings." Bad delivery: "It takes hard work and consistency." That meets expectations at best. Good delivery: "He spent 90 minutes every Monday doing one boring thing nobody else does — here's the exact process." That beats expectations because the viewer was expecting generic advice and got a specific, unexpected mechanism.

### Law 2 — Topic clarity in the first sentence (no mystery, no fluff)

**What it means:** Within the first 1-3 seconds your viewer must know exactly what the video is about. If the wrong viewer self-selects out, that's a win — algorithms reward audience match, not raw clicks.

**Which videos support it:** 01-killer-script ("click confirmation in first sentence"), 02-master-storyteller ("zero throat-clearing — he's into the topic by word 4"), 03-cant-stop-watching ("topic-first clarity"), 04-irresistible-hooks ("Context Lean = topic clarity"), 06-killer-script-2026 ("the hook must be CLEAR"), 07-killer-short ("verbal + written + visual hook all inside 3 seconds"), 08-irresistible-hooks ("the first 3 seconds will determine the results on your entire short").

**Concrete example for Gray:** Bad opener: *"What's up guys, today I want to talk about something really cool I've been thinking about lately..."* Good opener: *"Today we're talking about why your wedding edits look amateur — and the one color setting that fixes it."* Topic in word 4. Pain named. Solution promised.

### Law 3 — Open loops + curiosity gaps (every line must pull to the next)

**What it means:** A script is a chain of dominos. Each sentence must make the next sentence feel necessary. Kallaway calls this the **Curiosity Loop**: *"If you want to tell a great story you need the viewer to watch the first line and feel so compelled that they can't dream of doing anything else than watching the second."* Open a loop early. Close it late. Open new ones in between.

**Which videos support it:** All 8. 01-killer-script (re-hooking template "that point was important but if you don't also couple it with this one the magic is lost"), 02-master-storyteller (Context-Conflict "dance"), 03-cant-stop-watching (Hook → Body → **Rehook** → Body → Outro architecture, rehook at 20-25s in shorts, 2-3 min in YouTube), 04-irresistible-hooks (Scroll Stop + Contrarian Snapback), 05-10x-faster (loops nested 4 deep), 06-killer-script-2026 ("storytelling-as-hypnosis — red herrings break the trance"), 07-killer-short (the 7 factors), 08-irresistible-hooks ("dummy account reset" loop opened in Step 2, closed in Step 5).

**Concrete example for Gray:** In a documentary about a videographer's workflow: at 0:30 say *"There's one Monday-morning habit that took him from $3K to $40K per month — but it's not what you think. I'll show you what it is in a second."* Then go shoot 3 minutes of B-roll on his workspace before paying off the loop. The viewer cannot leave until that promise pays.

### Law 4 — Speed to Value (frontload, don't backload)

**What it means:** You are not making a movie. You don't get a slow build. The viewer is a slot-machine pull on a free pool of attention. From 03-cant-stop-watching: *"You got a lucky slot machine pool for your video to show up in front of someone for free, and you have to earn the right for them to keep watching."*

**Which videos support it:** 03-cant-stop-watching (named "Speed to Value problem"), 04-irresistible-hooks (literal timers: **4 seconds for shorts, 1-2 minutes for YouTube**), 05-10x-faster (Hook Commandment #2), 06-killer-script-2026 (commandment of hooks), 07-killer-short ("3-second triple hook rule"), 08-irresistible-hooks ("first 3 seconds determine the results on your entire short").

**Concrete example for Gray:** A short titled "How to fix muddy wedding footage" should not start with "I've been editing for 5 years..." It should start: *"Your wedding footage looks muddy. Here's the fix in 12 seconds."* Then show the fix on screen by second 4. The credentials, if you need them, go AFTER the value.

### Law 5 — Name your frameworks (give every concept a sticky label)

**What it means:** Don't say "the way you should think about editing." Coin a term. *"The Pillar Stack."* *"Atomic messaging."* *"The Last Dab."* *"Cult Hopping."* People remember labels, not paragraphs. From 03-cant-stop-watching: *"People can relate to a single word name more than a long definition."*

**Which videos support it:** 01-killer-script ("Expectations vs. Reality" / "Value Loop" / "Click Confirmation"), 02-master-storyteller ("The Dance" / "Last Dab" / "Story Lenses"), 03-cant-stop-watching ("Atomic Messaging" / "Story Flow Problem"), 04-irresistible-hooks ("Context Lean" / "Scroll Stop Interjection" / "Contrarian Snapback" / "Cult Hopping"), 05-10x-faster ("Law of Interesting" / "Shock Score" / "4 Hook Commandments" / "6 Emotion Buckets"), 06-killer-script-2026 (every section coins a new label), 07-killer-short ("7 Factors" / "5x Rule" / "Hook Stack"), 08-irresistible-hooks ("7 Hook Angles").

**Concrete example for Gray:** Instead of "I do research before posting," say *"Every Sunday I run the 5x Outlier Sweep."* Instead of "I edit in passes," say *"This is the 3-Pass Edit System."* Names make tactics shareable, screenshot-able, and re-watchable.

## The story arc by format

### Documentary (raw week-of-footage + talking-head A-roll inserts)

This is the format where most footage is captured live, with minimal edits except the first 30 seconds, plus talking-head inserts to fill gaps or add context.

**Recommended arc (step-by-step):**

0. **Write the Five Lines FIRST (from 09-five-line-story-method) — before the hook, before filming the A-roll.** This is the fix for "doc reads like a tutorial, not a story." Lock the emotional core in five sentences: **Situation → Desire → Conflict → Change → Result.** (e.g., for a week with the $40K wedding videographer: *Situation* — a videographer who looks like every other freelancer; *Desire* — he wants to charge premium and be booked solid; *Conflict* — everyone competes on gear and price, a race to the bottom; *Change* — he builds one boring Monday business habit; *Result* — $40K/wedding, fully booked, by doing the thing no one films.) Then derive the cold open from line 1 + line 3, declare the Story Lens from line 2 vs line 3, and **tape the five lines above your timeline as the cut-filter** — any clip that can't connect back to a line gets cut. Expand 5 → 10 → 20 lines for the full beat sheet; the core never changes, only the richness.

1. **A-roll cold open hook (0:00–0:30) — heavily edited.** Run Kallaway's intro framework (from 01-killer-script):
   - Line 1 — **Immediate context** (click confirmation): name the topic in the first sentence. *"This is a week with a videographer who charges $40K per wedding."*
   - Line 2 — **Common belief**: what people walk in assuming. *"Most editors think it's because of his camera gear."*
   - Line 3 — **Contrarian flip**: the unexpected reveal. *"But it turns out his entire edge comes from one boring habit he does every Monday morning."*
   - Line 4-5 — **Proof + plan**: name your authority + tease the structure. *"I spent a week embedded with him. Here's exactly what he does."*

2. **Story Lens declaration (within the first minute).** From 02-master-storyteller: pre-decide your *prism* — the angle no one else takes. Don't just document a week; document a week *through one specific lens* (e.g., "the business side everyone ignores"). Say the lens out loud early so the viewer knows what they're watching for.

3. **Open a long loop you'll pay off in the last third.** *"I'll show you that Monday habit at the end — but first..."* This is what carries the viewer through 8-15 minutes of raw footage.

4. **Build the body with the But/Therefore test (from 02-master-storyteller).** Sequence your raw clips by audit: between every two clips the connector must be *"but"* or *"therefore"* — never *"and then."* If consecutive clips connect with "and then," cut one. The dance of context-and-conflict is what keeps a doc from being a vlog.

5. **Insert A-roll talking-head whenever:**
   - You need to **re-hook** (every 2-3 minutes in long-form — from 03-cant-stop-watching). Verbatim template: *"That was important — but if you don't also understand this, the magic is lost."*
   - You need to **explain context** the live footage couldn't capture.
   - You need to **zoom out** ("let's pull back — here's why this matters"). From 05-10x-faster: zoom-outs every 3-5 minutes.

6. **Apply the "second-best first" rule to vignette order (from 01-killer-script).** Your most surprising vignette goes EARLY (vignette 1 or 2). Your climactic payoff goes mid-to-late. Save your absolute best for the third spot — the brain commits to staying after seeing two great things in ascending order.

7. **Re-hook before the final reveal.** *"Now this is the part I promised you at the start..."* Pay off the long loop from step 3.

8. **Outro — end on a high note (from 01-killer-script's "free chocolate" principle).** Recap the through line in one sentence. Restate the pain solved. Soft CTA — no hard pitch. Documentaries do not end with "smash subscribe" — they end with a reflection.

**Cite the Kallaway Hook → Body → Rehook → Body → Outro architecture (03-cant-stop-watching) but stretched:** in a 15-min documentary, expect 3-4 rehooks (one every 3-4 minutes), not just one.

### Sit-down talking head (Sharran / Hormozi style — heavy effects)

This is the format Kallaway, Hormozi, and Sharran live in. Heavy on-screen text, fast cuts, named frameworks, visible structure.

**Recommended arc (step-by-step) — uses Kallaway's 5-step process from 01-killer-script:**

**Step 0 — Pre-script lockdown.** Before you write a single word, lock these three:
- **Idea** — one-line pain point or rabbit hole
- **Title** — must be locked before writing (it sets the expectation contract)
- **Outline** — bullet points of your body with unique angles

From 01-killer-script: *"Measure twice, cut once. Don't break ground on the script until idea, title, and outline are locked."*

**Step 1 — Hook (Intro) using the 5-Part Framework (from 01-killer-script):**
- Part 1 — **Immediate context** (click confirm the title in the first sentence)
- Part 2 — **Common belief** (state what the viewer walks in assuming)
- Part 3 — **Contrarian take** (flip it)
- Part 4 — **Proof** (drop your credentials — but AFTER the promise, not before, per 03-cant-stop-watching)
- Part 5 — **Plan** (numbered roadmap: "five steps," "three mistakes," etc.)

**Layer in Kallaway's 3-Part Hook (from 04-irresistible-hooks) inside the first 25 seconds:**
- **Context Lean** — topic clarity + lean-in via common ground / pain / metaphor / mind-blowing fact
- **Scroll Stop Interjection** — *"But here's the thing..."* / *"However..."*
- **Contrarian Snapback** — sentence that goes opposite the lean while staying on topic

**Step 2 — Body (the meat).** From 01-killer-script:
- **First body point must be amazing** — the viewer is hunting for the first sign of novelty. If your first point is generic, they leave.
- **Use the "second-best first" cheat code** — your second-best point goes in slot 1, best point in slot 2, third-best in slot 3. Music-album analogy: best track is never #1.
- **Each body point uses the Value Loop:** Context (what + simple definition) → Application (how + example) → Framing (why it matters / how it fits).
- **Apply the But/Therefore test (from 02-master-storyteller)** between every body point — never "and then."
- **Inject pattern interrupts every 60-90 seconds (from 02-master-storyteller):** external clip, cross-domain analogy, self-deprecating joke, strawman objection ("crazy guy waving hands…"), fourth-wall break.

**Step 3 — Rehooks between body points.** From 03-cant-stop-watching, rehook every 2-3 minutes in YouTube long-form. Verbatim templates:
- *"That point was super important, but if you don't also couple it with this one, the magic is lost."* (01-killer-script)
- *"Hey, what I said is good, but what's coming next is great."* (03-cant-stop-watching)
- *"All right, the next most important piece..."* (05-10x-faster)

**Step 4 — Atomic Messaging (from 03-cant-stop-watching) — apply to every sentence:**
1. Sixth-grade vocabulary
2. Short sentences (staccato — see 04-irresistible-hooks)
3. Active voice ("the dog jumped" not "the jumping was done by the dog")
4. Restate twice — *"It was rudimentary, or simple"*
5. Name your frameworks (see Law 5 above)

**Step 5 — Outro (from 01-killer-script):**
- Recap the points (memory consolidation)
- Remind viewer of the pain solved (closes the expectation contract)
- "Free chocolate" — end on a high — leave them feeling reality beat expectations
- Triple CTA close (from 04-irresistible-hooks): tie-back to teaching → soft product pitch → free resource grab

**Pre-plan native embeds in the outline (from 01-killer-script).** Don't bolt CTAs on at the end. Identify which body point naturally opens a pain loop your product/freebie solves, then drop the embed there. Two patterns:
- Pattern A: Open loop with pain → offer your resource as the solve.
- Pattern B: Speedrun a framework too fast → offer your resource as the recap/compression tool.

**Architecture summary:** Hook → Body Point 1 → Rehook → Body Point 2 → Rehook → Body Point 3 → Native Embed → Outro. This is Kallaway's full architecture from 03-cant-stop-watching, made explicit.

### Shorts

The unforgiving format. 30-90 seconds. One swing, one miss = invisible.

**Recommended arc — uses Personal Brand Launch's 7-Factor framework from 07-killer-short, layered with Kallaway's hook formulas:**

**The 7 factors (every short must have all 7):**
1. **Topic** — niche + specific subniche
2. **Hooks (verbal + written + visual)** — the **Hook Stack** from 07-killer-short and 08-irresistible-hooks
3. **Value** — measurable, not fluffy (you can name the takeaway in one sentence)
4. **Script angle** — pick one of the 7 (tutorial / comparison / mythbust / dos vs don'ts / tip-hack / transformation / challenge)
5. **CTA** — ManyChat trigger word, not "link in bio"
6. **Format** — pick from the 11 film formats (NOT sit-and-talk — per 07-killer-short: *"sitting down and talking videos are boring, that worked in 2022"*)
7. **Editing style** — locked once, reused forever (2-3 colors, 2-3 fonts, 2-3 visual types)

**The 3-second triple-hook rule (from 07-killer-short and 08-irresistible-hooks):** Verbal + Written + Visual hook all land in the first 3 seconds. Not one — three, simultaneously.

**Hook architecture (Kallaway's 3-step from 04-irresistible-hooks, compressed):**
- **Context Lean** (1 line, ~2 seconds): topic clarity + benefit/pain. *"If you want your wedding edits to feel cinematic..."*
- **Scroll Stop** (1 line, ~1 second): *"But here's the thing..."* / *"But it turns out..."*
- **Contrarian Snapback** (1 line, ~2 seconds): the unexpected mechanism. *"...it has nothing to do with your camera."*

**Body — pick a script structure (from 06-killer-script-2026's 7 structures), pair with a hook:**
- Best combos for Gray's audience (videographers/editors):
  - **Contrarian hook + Problem Solver structure** = bold take → why it's true → fix
  - **Question hook + Case Study structure** = provocative Q → "here's what happened when..."
  - **Secret Reveal hook + Breakdown structure** = unknown insight → context → what/how → why
  - **List hook + Listicle structure** = "3 settings every videographer gets wrong"

**Rehook at 20-25 seconds (from 03-cant-stop-watching).** Verbatim trigger: *"That's good — but what's coming next is great."* For a 60-second short this is the only rehook you get; for a 90-second short you get one, maybe two.

**The Last Dab outro (from 02-master-storyteller).** Short-form loops on replay. Write the last line so it sets up the first line as bait for a re-watch. *"...and that's why every editor I know now does this every Monday."* loops back to *"This is a week with a videographer who charges $40K per wedding..."*

**Editing checklist (from 07-killer-short):**
- Big bold 3-5 word on-screen text in frame 1 (visual hook)
- Subtle motion (lean in/out, walking, gesture) — Goldilocks principle from 04-irresistible-hooks: *"just enough motion"*
- New shot every 2 seconds (shot-change format)
- Staccato sentences (short → medium → longer as the short extends — from 04-irresistible-hooks)

**Cross-niche viral templates to swipe (from 08-irresistible-hooks):** Translate any of these directly to Gray's videographer/editor niche:
- *"Is it possible to [outcome] in [timeframe] even though [obstacle]? Yes."* → *"Is it possible to color-grade a wedding video in 20 minutes even though you've never used DaVinci? Yes."*
- *"Three levels of [thing]"* → *"Three levels of color grading"*
- *"Smart [person] vs dumb [person] when it comes to [activity]"* → *"Smart editor vs dumb editor when it comes to transitions"*
- *"Did you know if you..."* → *"Did you know if you slow your B-roll to 80%..."*

## Cross-format principles (always true)

These apply to documentary, talking head, and shorts equally. Treat as non-negotiable.

1. **The Hook Stack is universal.** Every video (regardless of format) needs Verbal + Written + Visual hook in the opening seconds. Even a documentary cold open needs all three (07-killer-short, 08-irresistible-hooks).

2. **Apply the 5x Outlier Rule before swiping hooks (from 07-killer-short and 08-irresistible-hooks).** A video is a valid swipe candidate only if its views are at least 5x its creator's follower count, organic only. Anything less is a fluke; anything more is a proven template. Build a swipe file on Instagram (hardest platform to viral on = highest-signal swipes — per 07-killer-short).

3. **Write the desired emotion at the top of the page (from 05-10x-faster).** Pick from the 6 buckets: awe/inspiration, amusement/humor, excitement/joy, anger/outrage, surprise/shock/curiosity, sadness/empathy. Every line should drive that one emotion. Then read the finished draft and ask: *"What emotion do I actually feel when I finish reading?"* — if it doesn't match, rewrite.

4. **Use the Shock Score gate (from 05-10x-faster).** Before any fact goes in your script, rate it 1-100: *"Out of 100 people in my audience, how many would have heard this before?"* If 4 out of 5 already know it, cut it. *"What do you know to be true about this topic that most people don't? That's where true interestingness lives."*

5. **Cult Hop early when you're still growing (from 04-irresistible-hooks).** Wrap unknown ideas in known references. For Gray's editor audience: MrBeast's editors, A24, Casey Neistat, Christopher Nolan. *"This is how A24 grades skin tones..."* gives the viewer comfort + borrowed credibility.

6. **The Last Dab (from 02-master-storyteller).** Write the LAST line first. Especially for shorts (loops back to the hook on replay). For long-form, the last line should be the one-sentence summary that's screenshot-worthy — the line that gets shared.

7. **Storytelling = hypnosis (from 03-cant-stop-watching).** Every red herring, irrelevant tangent, or self-indulgent detail breaks the trance. Run the **line-by-line audit** on every draft: *"Is this line necessary context or a distraction?"* Cut everything that doesn't drive the through line.

8. **Tell it to a friend (from 05-10x-faster).** Before recording, ask: *"If I were telling this to a friend who asked me the question this video answers, how would I say it?"* If your script reads stiffer than that, rewrite it. The most successful creators (Emma Chamberlain, Casey Neistat, Steve Jobs) all have a conversational tone — *"talking with you, not at you"* (02-master-storyteller).

## Anti-patterns (what NOT to do)

Things the references explicitly warned against. If you catch yourself doing one of these, stop and rewrite.

1. **Don't open with credentials.** From 03-cant-stop-watching: lead with PAIN + PROMISE first, drop credentials AFTER. Bad: *"Hey guys, I'm Gray, I've been editing for 5 years and today I want to..."* Good: *"Your wedding edits look amateur. Here's the one setting that fixes it. I'm Gray — I edit weddings for a living."*

2. **Don't write "and then" between beats.** From 02-master-storyteller (Trey Parker / Matt Stone rule): between every two beats the connector must be *"but"* or *"therefore"* — never *"and then."* If "and then" fits, the beat is disposable.

3. **Don't use "wait till you see this" / "you're never going to believe this" hooks.** From 02-master-storyteller: *"Your first line should be as punchy and as indicative of the plot as possible."* Vague tease hooks are dead. Lead with the actual subject.

4. **Don't write a sit-and-talk short.** From 07-killer-short: *"Sitting down and talking videos are boring. Okay, that worked in 2022, y'all."* Use one of the 11 film formats — clone, green screen, multitasking, voiceover over editing screen, walking-and-talking, etc.

5. **Don't say "link in bio."** From 07-killer-short: use a ManyChat trigger word. Increases comment count (algo signal) AND captures the lead. *"Comment EDIT and I'll DM you the LUT."*

6. **Don't ship fluffy value.** From 07-killer-short: fluffy = unclear, not actionable. Measurable = the viewer knows exactly what to do after. *"Be consistent"* is fluffy. *"Post one clone-format short every Tuesday at 7pm"* is measurable.

7. **Don't post a hook you invented from scratch.** From 08-irresistible-hooks: *"Never throw spaghetti at the wall."* Always swipe from a 5x outlier and adapt by 1-2 words. Adapt — don't word-for-word copy.

8. **Don't backload the value.** From 04-irresistible-hooks: *"Anything you make after they click off might as well be a black screen — they're not going to see it."* You're not going for 100% completion. You're going to eke out an extra 30 seconds at a time by frontloading the value.

9. **Don't try to demonstrate how smart you are.** From 03-cant-stop-watching: *"People do not feel good when they feel dumb. Your job with content is to make them feel smart. The goal is not to demonstrate how much more than them you know — it's to demonstrate how big of a gap you can help them close."* Drop the big vocabulary. Sixth-grade words.

10. **Don't treat the outro as an afterthought.** From 01-killer-script: *"The outro is the free chocolate that the restaurant gives you when they bring the check."* Recap, restate the pain solved, end on a high. The outro is what drives shares, saves, and rewatches.

11. **Don't break the through line for a cool moment.** From 03-cant-stop-watching's prince-in-the-forest example: every line/clip must serve the ONE plot. Cool tangents that don't serve the through line break the hypnosis. Cut them no matter how much you love them.

12. **Don't post without a Story Lens.** From 02-master-storyteller: *"Finding a cool topic is not enough — unless you're super niched down, there's likely dozens of other people talking about that same topic."* Pre-decide your *prism* (unique angle) before shooting/writing. For Gray: don't make "another color grading tutorial" — make "color grading like a wedding cinematographer who hates weddings" or whatever your specific angle is.

## How to actually use this when writing a future script

This is the operator manual. When Gray says *"write me a doc-style script about X"* (or talking-head or short), Claude should follow this exact process. It is built from the 6-step system in 05-10x-faster, the 4-step swipe workflow in 05-10x-faster and 08-irresistible-hooks, and Kallaway's 5-step process in 01-killer-script.

### Step 1 — Lock the inputs (before writing one word)

Confirm with Gray:
- **Format:** documentary / sit-down talking head / short?
- **Topic + Story Lens:** what's the unique angle no one else in the videographer/editor niche is taking?
- **Target emotion** (pick one from the 6 buckets — awe, amusement, joy, outrage, surprise/curiosity, empathy)
- **Target viewer pain or benefit** (must be specific — *"wants leads as a wedding videographer"* not *"wants growth"*)
- **Working title** (lock before writing)

### Step 2 — Build the fact bank (research + Shock Score gate)

Research the topic. Write down every fact, statistic, anecdote, and angle you can find. Then run each one through the **Shock Score test** (05-10x-faster): rate 1-100 — *"how many of Gray's audience already know this?"* Keep only the facts that score 70+ (i.e., 70%+ of viewers would NOT have heard it).

For Shorts: also build a **5x Outlier swipe file** on Instagram (08-irresistible-hooks workflow: keyword search → Most Viewed reels → save only the ones at 5x follower count or higher).

### Step 3 — Write the desired emotion at the top of the script doc

Literally: line 1 of the doc = the target emotion. Every sentence written below it is filtered through *"does this drive [emotion]?"*

### Step 4 — Pick the hook format (from 05-10x-faster's 9 options)

Choose ONE: Secret Reveal / Case Study / Comparison / Question / Education / List / Contrarian / Personal Experience / Problem. Study what's working in the videographer/editor niche on Instagram and IG — pick the format already winning there. Don't invent.

Write the hook using Kallaway's 3-step formula (04-irresistible-hooks):
- Context Lean (topic + lean-in)
- Scroll Stop Interjection (but / however / yet / although / therefore)
- Contrarian Snapback (opposite direction, still on topic)

Then run the **4 Hook Commandments** filter (05-10x-faster):
- Alignment (verbal + written + visual all match)
- Speed to Value (zero delay)
- Clarity (if viewer hears only sentence 1, it's clear what's coming)
- Curiosity (opens a loop)

### Step 5 — Pick the story structure (from 05-10x-faster's 7 options)

Choose ONE: Breakdown / Newscaster / Case Study Explainer / Listicle / Problem Solver / Tutorial / Educational-Personal Story. Pair it with the hook format using 05-10x-faster's pairing rules.

For Gray's three formats:
- **Documentary** → usually Case Study Explainer or Educational/Personal Story (long form, character-driven)
- **Talking head** → usually Breakdown or Listicle (5-step, 3-mistakes, etc.)
- **Shorts** → usually Problem Solver or Case Study Explainer (compressed)

### Step 6 — Write the outline (NOT the script yet)

Bullet points only. For each body point, layer the Value Loop (01-killer-script):
- Context (what + simple definition)
- Application (how + example)
- Framing (why it matters / how it fits)

Apply **second-best first** (01-killer-script): second-best point in slot 1, best in slot 2, third-best in slot 3.

**Pre-plan native embeds** at the outline stage — which body point naturally opens a pain loop a CTA can solve?

### Step 7 — Write the script

Now write prose. Follow the architecture for your format:
- Documentary: cold open hook → Story Lens → body with But/Therefore connectors → 3-4 rehooks → long-loop payoff → outro
- Talking head: 5-part hook → body point 1 → rehook → body point 2 → rehook → body point 3 → native embed → outro
- Shorts: 3-sec triple hook → body (one structure) → rehook at 20-25s → Last Dab outro

Apply **Atomic Messaging** to every sentence (03-cant-stop-watching):
1. Sixth-grade words
2. Short sentences (staccato)
3. Active voice
4. Restate twice
5. Name the frameworks

### Step 8 — Audit pass (the 4-question checklist from 05-10x-faster)

Read through the finished script and answer:
1. Is this story interesting? (Shock Score 70+ throughout?)
2. Is this story as compressed as it can possibly be? (line-by-line audit — cut anything that doesn't drive the through line)
3. Does this hook actually hook me on its own? (if you read only the first 3 seconds, do you want to watch the rest?)
4. What emotion do I feel when I finish reading? (matches the target emotion at the top of the doc?)

### Step 9 — Run the jagged-edge test (from 02-master-storyteller)

Reformat the script with **one sentence per line**. Look down the right margin. If it forms a "jagged edge" (some short, some medium, some long) — good rhythm. If sentences end at roughly the same point → monotonous → rewrite for rhythm.

### Step 10 — Tell-it-to-a-friend test (from 05-10x-faster)

Read the script aloud. If it sounds stiffer than how you'd explain it to a friend over coffee, it's still too formal. Loosen it.
