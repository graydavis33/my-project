# 40 Tested Hook Templates — Verbal + Visual

**Built from patterns used by Kallaway, Dan Koe, Justin Welsh, Alex Hormozi, Jay Clouse, Ali Abdaal, and the broader personal-brand-launch genre on YT/IG/TikTok.**

How to use: each hook is a **template** with `[BRACKETED VARIABLES]`. Swap the variable, keep the structure. The structure is what's proven — your variable just plugs in your topic.

Pair one verbal hook with one visual hook. Different combos = different videos from the same template.

---

## VERBAL HOOK TEMPLATES (first 1–3 seconds spoken)

### 1. The Permission-to-Stop
**Template:** "Stop [COMMON ACTION] right now."
**Example:** "Stop editing on a laptop right now."
**Why it works:** Direct command + interrupts scroll. Polarizing because it implies the viewer is doing it wrong.

### 2. The Insider Reveal
**Template:** "Nobody is talking about [TOPIC]."
**Example:** "Nobody is talking about color grading shortcuts."
**Why it works:** Implies secret knowledge. Curiosity gap.

### 3. The Mistake Frame
**Template:** "The biggest mistake [AUDIENCE] make is [MISTAKE]."
**Example:** "The biggest mistake editors make is overcutting."
**Why it works:** Names the viewer + promises a fix.

### 4. The Time-Save
**Template:** "I'm going to save you [TIMEFRAME] of [PAIN]."
**Example:** "I'm going to save you 6 months of bad editing."
**Why it works:** Quantified value upfront.

### 5. The Listicle Wisdom
**Template:** "[NUMBER] things I wish I knew before [MILESTONE]."
**Example:** "5 things I wish I knew before going full-time."
**Why it works:** Proven curiosity format. Numbered = finite = watchable.

### 6. The Hard Truth
**Template:** "Here's the truth about [TOPIC] no one will tell you."
**Example:** "Here's the truth about freelancing no one will tell you."
**Why it works:** Authority + secrecy. Frames you as the brave one.

### 7. The Receipt Drop
**Template:** "I [OUTCOME] in [SHORT TIMEFRAME]. Here's exactly how."
**Example:** "I hit 100k followers in 90 days. Here's exactly how."
**Why it works:** Proof + promise. Concrete number = credibility.

### 8. The Wrong-Belief Flip
**Template:** "Most [AUDIENCE] get [TOPIC] completely wrong."
**Example:** "Most creators get hooks completely wrong."
**Why it works:** Polarizing. Anyone who agrees has to keep watching.

### 9. The POV
**Template:** "POV: you just realized [INSIGHT]."
**Example:** "POV: you just realized your camera settings are wrong."
**Why it works:** Native to short-form. Puts viewer inside the moment.

### 10. The Mentor Quote
**Template:** "[AUTHORITY FIGURE] told me [INSIGHT] and it changed everything."
**Example:** "My first client told me one thing and it changed everything."
**Why it works:** Borrowed authority + transformation promise.

### 11. The Warning Label
**Template:** "Watch this before you [ACTION]."
**Example:** "Watch this before you buy another camera."
**Why it works:** Protective frame. Implies cost of not watching.

### 12. The Counterintuitive
**Template:** "The fastest way to [OUTCOME] is [UNEXPECTED THING]."
**Example:** "The fastest way to grow is posting less."
**Why it works:** Surprise + utility. Pattern interrupt.

### 13. The Experiment
**Template:** "I tried [THING] for [TIMEFRAME]. Here's what happened."
**Example:** "I tried posting daily for 30 days. Here's what happened."
**Why it works:** Story promise + curiosity gap on outcome.

### 14. The Belief Inversion
**Template:** "Why [COMMON BELIEF] is actually wrong."
**Example:** "Why posting consistently is actually wrong."
**Why it works:** Direct contrarian frame. Forces engagement.

### 15. The Stat Punch
**Template:** "[NUMBER]% of [AUDIENCE] will fail at [THING]. Here's why."
**Example:** "97% of editors will quit in year one. Here's why."
**Why it works:** Specific stat = credibility. Fail-frame = curiosity.

### 16. The If-Then
**Template:** "If you're a [ROLE] and you're not doing [THING], you're losing."
**Example:** "If you're a videographer and you're not on LinkedIn, you're losing."
**Why it works:** Targets viewer + threat of loss = stronger than promise of gain.

### 17. The Confession
**Template:** "I used to [BAD HABIT] until I learned [INSIGHT]."
**Example:** "I used to charge $200 a video until I learned this."
**Why it works:** Vulnerability + transformation arc.

### 18. The Question Bait
**Template:** "Why do [AUDIENCE] always [BEHAVIOR]?"
**Example:** "Why do new editors always overuse zooms?"
**Why it works:** Names the viewer's tribe + opens a loop.

### 19. The Comparison
**Template:** "[OPTION A] vs [OPTION B] — and the winner will surprise you."
**Example:** "Sony vs Canon — and the winner will surprise you."
**Why it works:** Tribal opinion bait + curiosity gap.

### 20. The Hot Take
**Template:** "Hot take: [POLARIZING OPINION]."
**Example:** "Hot take: cinematic B-roll is killing your engagement."
**Why it works:** Self-labels as polarizing → invites both sides into the comments.

## VISUAL HOOK TEMPLATES (first 1–3 seconds on screen)

### 1. Walk-Toward-Camera
**Template:** Start mid-walk approaching camera, hit your mark, deliver line. Swap [LOCATION].
**Example:** Walking out of an edit suite, into frame, "Stop using that LUT."
**Why it works:** Movement = motion = retention. Establishes confidence.

### 2. Whiteboard Reveal
**Template:** Camera on you. You step aside revealing a whiteboard with [BOLD CLAIM] written on it.
**Example:** Reveal: "90% of editors are getting paid wrong."
**Why it works:** Visual surprise + text reinforces the verbal hook.

### 3. Eye-Lock Close-Up
**Template:** Tight close-up on your face, no cut, direct eye contact for 3+ seconds while you say [LINE].
**Example:** Close-up: "We need to talk about your portfolio."
**Why it works:** Mimics 1-on-1 conversation. Hardest hook to scroll past.

### 4. Object Reveal
**Template:** Hold up [PHYSICAL OBJECT] related to topic, camera focuses on it.
**Example:** Hold up an SD card: "This card cost me a $5,000 client."
**Why it works:** Physical objects pattern-interrupt. Tells the brain "story incoming."

### 5. Split-Screen Wrong vs Right
**Template:** Screen split. Left labeled [WRONG WAY], right labeled [RIGHT WAY].
**Example:** Left: amateur edit. Right: pro edit. Same clip.
**Why it works:** Instant comprehension. Side-by-side is undefeated.

### 6. Big-Text Bold Claim
**Template:** Massive on-screen text saying [BOLD CLAIM] while you do something mundane (sip coffee, sit down).
**Example:** Text: "YOU'RE OVERPAYING FOR YOUR EDITOR." You sip coffee.
**Why it works:** Contrast between calm visual and aggressive text creates tension.

### 7. Screen-Recording Overlay
**Template:** Phone or laptop screen recording showing [PROOF/RECEIPT] as overlay or full-frame.
**Example:** Show Stripe payout screen → "$8,400 from one client."
**Why it works:** Visual proof front-loaded. No "trust me bro."

### 8. Outdoor B-Roll Voiceover
**Template:** B-roll of you in [LOCATION] (city, gym, set), voiceover delivers hook.
**Example:** B-roll walking through Manhattan, VO: "I quit my agency job 30 days ago."
**Why it works:** Cinematic + lifestyle aspiration baked in.

### 9. Unexpected Activity
**Template:** Doing [MUNDANE/ABSURD ACTION] (eating, lifting, dancing) while delivering serious claim.
**Example:** Eating cereal, deadpan: "Most freelancers are charging 80% too little."
**Why it works:** Tonal mismatch = pattern interrupt = retention.

### 10. Before/After
**Template:** Side-by-side or quick cut of [BEFORE STATE] → [AFTER STATE] within 1 second.
**Example:** Raw footage cut → graded footage cut.
**Why it works:** Transformation visual is the most clicked format on the platform.

### 11. Countdown Overlay
**Template:** "3… 2… 1…" overlay before [PAYOFF].
**Example:** "3… 2… 1… the edit that got me hired."
**Why it works:** Engineered anticipation. Brain holds attention for the payoff.

### 12. Live-Annotated Screenshot
**Template:** Static screenshot on screen, your hand or cursor draws arrows/circles around [HIGHLIGHT].
**Example:** Screenshot of a viral video, you circle the hook frame.
**Why it works:** Active visual = brain treats it like a lesson.

### 13. Destruction Visual
**Template:** Tearing, breaking, or throwing away [OBJECT REPRESENTING OLD WAY].
**Example:** Rip up a printed price list: "Stop charging by the hour."
**Why it works:** High-intensity visual emotion sticks.

### 14. Whisper-Lean
**Template:** Lean in close to camera, lower volume, deliver [INSIDE INFO] like a secret.
**Example:** Lean in: "Here's the rate every editor should be charging."
**Why it works:** Intimacy + secrecy = unscrollable.

### 15. Jump-Cut Same-Line
**Template:** Same line delivered in [3–5 LOCATIONS/OUTFITS] in rapid jump cuts.
**Example:** "Charge more." (kitchen) "Charge more." (gym) "Charge more." (set).
**Why it works:** Repetition + visual variety = engagement loop.

### 16. POV Shot
**Template:** First-person view of [TASK/SCENE] viewer would experience.
**Example:** POV editing timeline, hands on keyboard, "Here's the cut nobody teaches you."
**Why it works:** Puts viewer in your seat. High immersion.

### 17. Outfit-Swap Blink Edit
**Template:** Stand still, jump cut between [OUTFITS/DAYS] in same frame to imply [TIME PASSING].
**Example:** Day 1, Day 30, Day 90 — same shot, different look.
**Why it works:** Compresses transformation into 2 seconds.

### 18. Reaction Overlay
**Template:** Your face in corner reacting to [SCREENSHOT/STAT/COMMENT] taking up the rest of frame.
**Example:** Stat: "$0 made in year one." Your face: shock.
**Why it works:** Emotion + data = memorable.

### 19. Pulling-Out-Of-Frame Reveal
**Template:** Reach off-camera, pull out [OBJECT/PROP] revealing topic.
**Example:** Pull a book out of frame, hold it up: "This changed how I price."
**Why it works:** Off-screen reveal triggers "what is it" reflex.

### 20. Number-On-Forehead / Body Text
**Template:** A number or word visibly written/projected on [BODY/SHIRT/WALL].
**Example:** "$10K" sticky-note on forehead: "This was last month's revenue."
**Why it works:** Absurd visual = memorable + thumbnail-ready.

## How to Stack Them

Pick **1 verbal + 1 visual** per video. Same verbal template + different visual template = a fresh-feeling video. Same visual + new verbal = same.

**Recommended starter combos:**
- Hot Take (V20) + Eye-Lock Close-Up (Vis3) — your bread & butter polarizing format
- The Receipt Drop (V7) + Screen-Recording Overlay (Vis7) — credibility post
- The Experiment (V13) + Outfit-Swap Blink Edit (Vis17) — story arc post
- The Mistake Frame (V3) + Split-Screen Wrong vs Right (Vis5) — tutorial post
- The Confession (V17) + Whisper-Lean (Vis14) — vulnerability post

## Testing Protocol

1. Pick 5 verbal × 5 visual = 25 combos.
2. Post 1 combo per day.
3. Track 3-second retention + watch-time in `video-log.csv`.
4. Top 5 retention combos = your repeatable formats. Loop those.
5. Retire any combo that drops below your account average twice in a row.
