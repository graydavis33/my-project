# Instagram Stories SOP

**Production tier:** NONE
**Cadence:** ≥3/day on IG AND TikTok
**Owner:** Sai self-films most; Gray captures Sai when together

---

## Why this exists

The 400 followers gained in Month 1 need somewhere to come back to. Stories are the daily touchpoint that keeps the audience engaged between posts. Without stories, new followers churn within weeks.

**Hard minimum: 3 stories/day on each of IG + TikTok.**

---

## Content types

| Type | Owner | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Day-in-life snippets | Sai | What he's working on, where he is, who he's meeting |
| Polls / questions | Sai | "Should I post this or this?", "What should I work on tomorrow?" |
| Process / desk shots | Sai or Gray | What's on screen, what's being edited, etc. |
| Photos of Sai | Gray (when together) | Visually pleasing — Gray as photographer, good light, good frame |
| Reposts / shares | Sai | Tagged content, mutual creators |

---

## Photos (Gray's contribution)

When Gray is with Sai, capture story-ready photos:
- Good light, clean background
- Sai engaged in something — working, walking, talking
- Vertical 9:16 or near-square that crops cleanly
- Send to Sai's Drive folder daily; Sai picks which to story

Quote from the recap: *"You taking pictures of me. And that can be very visually pleasing, really good pictures, really good photos, very much so engaged with our audience."*

---

## Don't

- Don't over-edit stories. Native IG/TikTok Story editor only.
- Don't repost shorts as stories — stories are a distinct format, not a content multiplier
- Don't skip a day. Three stories minimum. Even on travel days, even on busy days.
- Don't post identical stories on IG and TikTok — small differences (caption, sticker) make each native to the platform
