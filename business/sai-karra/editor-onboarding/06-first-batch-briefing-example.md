## Batch Overview

This week's theme is **Founder Foundations** — the stuff Sai learned the hard way that nobody tells new founders. Each video tackles one real, unvarnished truth about building a business.

**Why this matters:** These videos are designed to build credibility + connection with new founders who follow Sai. Raw honesty outperforms polished takes.

### Vibe checklist for this batch
```
✅ Conversational (not corporate-speak)
✅ Vulnerable when appropriate (Sai admitting mistakes)
✅ Confident when teaching (no apology)
✅ Fast-paced energy (holds attention, not slow/boring)
✅ Minimal graphics (they're noise — keep it simple)
✅ Focus on Sai's face + expressions (trust-building)
```

### What this batch is NOT
```
❌ No cinematic B-roll montages
❌ No complex motion graphics
❌ No overly-produced vibes
❌ No trying to be funny (if it's funny naturally, keep it — if it's forced, cut it)
❌ No making Sai look like someone he's not
```

## Per-Video Direction

### Video 1: "How I Hire" (C2700, take session)

**Raw footage:** C2700.mp4 (3 hooks + body + outro)

**What Sai talks about:** His hiring process — how he decides who to bring onto the team, what he looks for, what he learned from hiring mistakes.

**What I want:** 
This is a "teaching" video, but it should feel like Sai's just explaining his approach to a founder friend. Not preachy, not a masterclass. Just "here's what I do."

**Structure:**
- **Hook (0–4 sec):** One of Sai's natural hooks from the take session. Should grab attention with a surprising statement or question. (Reference: see link below)
- **Body (4–35 sec):** Sai walks through his hiring philosophy. Should feel like a conversation — breathing room between ideas.
- **Payoff (35–40 sec):** "Why I do this" or "here's what changed."

**Mood:**
Conversational, confident, teaching-but-not-preachy.

**Reference:** 
[Sandcastles outlier] https://www.instagram.com/reel/[LINK] (742× viral)
**What to notice:** At 0:08–0:20, this creator teaches something complex but makes it feel conversational. Notice how long he holds each thought (usually 3–4 sec) before moving on. There's silence/breathing room. Apply that pattern to Sai's philosophy.

**Graphics:**
Minimal. If you add a graphic, it should be:
- A simple icon (e.g., a small team silhouette)
- Appears for only 3–5 sec during the key teaching moment
- Fades out before the payoff
- Keep it SIMPLE — one idea, one graphic. Don't overload.

**Captions:**
Standard (see BRAND_SPEC.md):
- Montserrat SemiBold, 64px, white
- 3–5 words per chunk
- Lowercase (except I, names, acronyms)
- No punctuation
- Position: bottom (default), unless graphic is at bottom — then move to top

**Target duration:** 40 sec

**Notes for editor:**
- Sai did 3 hooks in the take session. Pick the one that feels most "real" (not over-rehearsed).
- If Hook A feels stale (he's reciting it), use Hook B (more natural).
- Cut any moments where Sai starts a sentence and stops to restart — take the complete version.

## Overall Batch Direction

### Pacing expectations
- **Videos 1–3:** Teaching/advice feel. Slower cuts, more breathing room.
- **Videos 4–6:** Mixed. Some slow (vulnerability), some faster (payoff/energy).
- **Video 7:** Slow. Serious. Vulnerable.

### Graphics philosophy
- Use graphics only if they clarify (not decorate).
- Keep them simple. Rounded shapes, minimal animation.
- Color: Trendify orange (#F28129) + white only.

### Caption density
- Don't over-caption. If Sai's words are clear, lighter caption coverage is okay.
- Only caption the key phrases (not every word).
- Let silence/breathing room exist (captions don't need to fill every second).

### What makes this batch special
This is the "Founder Foundations" series launch. These videos are meant to build trust and credibility. Authenticity > Polish. Sai's realness is the asset.
