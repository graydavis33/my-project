## 📌 Key Principles (Apply to Everything)

### 1. **Consistency over novelty**
Every Sai video should look & feel cohesive. Same fonts, same colors, same caption style, same vibe. Don't introduce variety for variety's sake.

### 2. **References are mood boards, not templates**
Match the feeling/rhythm of a reference video, not the exact cuts/timing. Your interpretation matters.

### 3. **Ask, don't guess**
If a brief is unclear, Slack Gray immediately. Better to ask than re-edit the wrong direction.

### 4. **Communicate early**
Daily status updates, ask questions early, report blockers immediately. Transparency prevents surprises.

### 5. **Quality over speed**
A carefully edited video with thoughtful captions beats a rushed video. The 48-hour deadline is the goal, not the maximum.

### 6. **Gray approves batches, not one-by-one**
You deliver all 7 videos at once. Gray watches all 7 at once. Revisions are batch-based (not "redo video 3, keep 1–2").

### 7. **Authenticity over polish**
Sai's realness is the asset. Over-production kills trust. Keep it simple. Keep it genuine.

## 📊 Success Metrics

### How you know you're doing well:
- Gray approves videos with minimal revisions (most videos pass on V1)
- Your batches deliver on time (within 48 hours)
- Captions are readable and well-placed (Gray doesn't ask to move them)
- Graphics match the brand style (Gray doesn't ask to redo them)
- Videos feel authentic (Gray says "nailed the vibe")
- Communication is clear (Gray never has to ask for clarification twice)

### Red flags (ask for help if you see these):
- Multiple revision rounds for the same issues (e.g., caption placement keeps getting rejected)
- Confusion on brief direction (you're re-editing for the same video multiple times)
- File organization problems (you can't find files, uploads are slow)
- Technical issues (videos export at wrong resolution or frame rate)
- Communication breakdowns (Gray's feedback is unclear and you're stuck)

If you see red flags, flag them. Slack Gray: "I'm struggling with X. Can we talk about how to solve this?"
