## Overview

Each video in a batch comes with **reference videos**. These are NOT what you're re-editing or copying. They're **mood boards** — showing you the *vibe, pacing, energy* Gray wants for that video.

**Think of them as:** "Cut your video to feel like THIS."

---

## What's a Reference Video?

### Types of References You'll See

#### 1. **Sandcastles Outlier Videos**
These are SHORT-FORM videos (usually 15–60 sec) from Sandcastles — a research tool that finds viral short-form videos across TikTok, Instagram, YouTube Shorts.

**Example:** A Sandcastles link might say:
```
https://www.tiktok.com/@username/video/123456
Outlier score: 742× (very viral)
Category: "productivity tips"
Why Gray sent it: "Notice the pacing at 0:15-0:30 — quick 2-sec cuts that match the energy level"
```

**How to use it:**
1. Open the TikTok/Instagram/YouTube link
2. Watch the WHOLE video, but pay special attention to the timestamp Gray mentioned
3. Notice:
   - How long are the cuts? (Are they snappy or slow?)
   - Does the energy peak at certain moments?
   - When does the speaker pause vs. talk rapidly?
   - How many graphics/B-roll cuts per concept?
4. Apply that *rhythm* to your cut of Sai's video (not the content, just the feel)

**Important:** You're NOT trying to look like these videos. You're learning the *pacing pattern*.

---

#### 2. **Mood Board Videos**
Gray sometimes creates a custom video that shows the exact mood he wants.

**Example:** A mood board might be a 30-sec compilation of different YouTube Shorts showing:
- Casual conversational tone (no corporate speaking)
- Slow cuts on emotional moments
- Fast cuts when sharing advice
- Lots of breathing room between sentences

**How to use it:**
1. Watch it all the way through
2. Pause at moments that feel like what Sai should be doing
3. Note the rhythm: "Slow here, fast here, pause here"
4. Apply those patterns to Sai's video

---

#### 3. **Platform Best Practices**
Sometimes Gray sends general guidance like:
```
"For Instagram Reels, people scroll fast. Keep cuts to 2–3 seconds max on the hook.
Slow down when you're teaching something."
```

This is less about a specific video, more about **platform norms**. Apply it to all videos on that platform.

## How to Study a Reference Video

### Step 1: Initial watch (full video)
```
Watch the ENTIRE video without pausing.
Get the overall feeling.
```

### Step 2: Identify the pattern
```
Pause and note:
- Hook (first 3–5 sec): How does it grab attention?
- Body (middle section): What's the pacing? Quick or slow?
- Payoff (last 3–5 sec): Does energy peak? Go soft?
- Transitions: How does one idea flow to the next?
```

### Step 3: Note specific timings
```
Example: "0:00-0:03 is the hook (quick cuts, 1.5s each). 
          0:03-0:20 is the teach section (slower cuts, 3s each). 
          0:20-0:30 is the payoff (fast cuts again, 1.5s each)."
```

### Step 4: Apply the pattern to Sai's footage
```
Don't copy the content. Copy the rhythm.

Reference pattern:    Quick → Slow → Quick
Apply to Sai's video: Hook (quick) → Teaching (slow) → Payoff (quick)
```

## Common Reference Patterns

### Pattern 1: "Hook Energy"
**What it means:** The first 3–5 seconds grab attention with fast cuts and visual interest.

**How to cut it:**
- Cuts every 1.5–2 seconds
- Visual variety (B-roll, graphics, Sai's expressions)
- Builds momentum

**When Gray sends this:** For videos where the hook is critical (e.g., "Turn Your Creativity Into a System")

---

### Pattern 2: "Teaching Vibe"
**What it means:** Slow, deliberate cuts when Sai is explaining something.

**How to cut it:**
- Holds each take 3–5 seconds
- Less B-roll, more focus on Sai's face
- Breathing room between ideas
- Captions time out so viewers can read

**When Gray sends this:** For educational/advice videos (e.g., "Here's How I Manage My Money")

---

### Pattern 3: "Story Pacing"
**What it means:** Varied cuts that match the emotional arc of a story.

**How to cut it:**
- Slow on vulnerable/emotional moments
- Quick on payoff/realization moments
- B-roll shows action (not just talking head)
- Audio and visuals sync

**When Gray sends this:** For narrative videos (e.g., "Why I Took This Risk")

---

### Pattern 4: "Trending Format"
**What it means:** A proven format from Sandcastles that's getting high engagement.

**Example:**
```
"Problem vs. Solution" format:
- 0:00-0:10: Problem (Gray shows a pain point)
- 0:10-0:20: Sai's solution (what he would do instead)
- 0:20-0:30: Payoff (why it matters)

Cuts: Quick on problem (creates tension), slower on solution (teaches), fast on payoff (satisfying).
```

**When Gray sends this:** When he wants Sai to adopt a structure that's already proven to work.

## Red Flags (What NOT to do)

### ❌ Don't copy the content
```
WRONG: Reference shows a productivity tip. You add the exact same tip to Sai's video.
RIGHT: Reference shows a teaching rhythm. You apply that rhythm to Sai's different idea.
```

### ❌ Don't ignore the reference
```
WRONG: Gray sends a reference video, you don't watch it.
RIGHT: Watch it before you start editing.
```

### ❌ Don't assume one reference applies to all videos
```
WRONG: Video 1's reference has fast cuts, so you cut all 7 videos fast.
RIGHT: Apply each video's specific reference to only that video.
```

### ❌ Don't match the reference perfectly
```
WRONG: You mimic the exact cuts, same length, same timing.
RIGHT: You match the feeling/rhythm while making it unique to Sai's footage.
```

## How to Use References During Editing

### Workflow

**Before you start editing Video 1:**
```
1. Open reference video in browser
2. Watch it 2–3 times (absorb the feeling)
3. Read Gray's notes on what to notice
4. Start editing Sai's footage
5. Keep the reference open in another window (watch it again mid-edit if you get stuck on pacing)
```

**While editing:**
```
Stuck on pacing?
→ Rewatch the reference at the exact moment you're confused
→ Pause the reference and note the cut length
→ Apply that cut length to Sai's footage
→ Play back and see if it feels right
```

**After you finish Video 1:**
```
Review your cut against the reference:
- Does your hook have the same energy as the reference hook?
- Does your pacing match the teaching moments?
- Does your payoff land the same way?
(Not identical, but same vibe?)
```

## Sandcastles Outlier Deep Dive

### What makes a Sandcastles outlier?
Sandcastles scores every short-form video on the internet. An **outlier** is a video that got disproportionately high engagement compared to the creator's usual performance.

**Example:**
```
Typical creator's video: 5,000 views
Outlier video: 745,000 views (149× their average)
Why it outlier'd: The hook, pacing, or format resonated unexpectedly
```

### Why Gray sends them
When Gray sends a Sandcastles outlier, he's saying: **"This format/pacing/structure works. Apply it to Sai."**

### How to analyze an outlier

**Level 1 (Quick analysis — 2 min):**
```
Watch the video. What jumps out?
- Is it fast or slow?
- Does it have graphics?
- Is Sai talking or is there B-roll?
- How long is the hook?
Write down 1–2 observations.
```

**Level 2 (Detailed analysis — 5 min):**
```
Timestamp the video:
- 0:00-0:05: Hook (what's the strategy?)
- 0:05-0:20: Body (how does the content sit?)
- 0:20-0:30: Payoff (how does it end?)
- Note any graphics, B-roll, or editing tricks
- Note the speaker's pace (fast talking vs. slow talking)
```

**Level 3 (Reverse-engineer the formula — 10 min):**
```
Why did this video go viral?
- Is it the hook that's unique?
- Is it the structure (problem/solution)?
- Is it the pacing (makes you feel something)?
- Is it the visuals (graphics, B-roll, Sai's expressions)?
Apply the most impactful element to your cut.
```

## Common Questions

**Q: Do I have to follow the reference exactly?**  
A: No. Match the *vibe*, not the exact timing. Your unique interpretation is good.

**Q: What if the reference video is a different platform (TikTok vs. Instagram)?**  
A: The pacing patterns are similar enough. Apply the rhythm, adjust for platform norms.

**Q: What if I don't understand what Gray means by "match this energy"?**  
A: Ask. Better to clarify in Slack than re-edit the wrong direction.

**Q: Should I show my cut to reference before delivering?**  
A: Up to you. If you're unsure, do a quick comparison (watch reference, then watch your cut). Does the rhythm match?
