# Editor Team — Communication Protocol

**For:** Sai Karra shorts editing team  
**From:** Gray Davis (Creative Director)  
**Effective:** 2026-06-24  

---

## Quick Start

- **Slack:** `#sai-shorts-editing` — async feedback, questions, daily status
- **Google Meets:** Batch review calls (typically Tuesday + Friday, 15 min each)
- **Turnaround:** 48 hours per batch (no revisions on weekend)
- **Max revisions:** 2 rounds per batch, then lock for publish

---

## Slack Etiquette

### What goes in Slack
- Daily status ("Received 7 videos today, starting editing tomorrow AM")
- Quick questions ("Is the orange hex code #F28129 or #F29129?")
- Progress updates ("Videos 1–4 ready for review")
- Clarifications ("On video 3, when Gray says 'faster pacing,' does he mean cut every 2s vs 3s?")
- Blockers ("Don't have the footage file for video 5")

### What does NOT go in Slack
- Do not send draft/in-progress videos — wait for batch completion
- Do not ask for feedback on unfinished work — finish the batch first
- Do not tag Gray on every message — batch your questions into threads
- Do not ask "is this good?" — finish the batch, then Gray watches it all and gives feedback

### Slack response time
- Gray will respond within 24 hours
- Urgent blockers: mention `@gray` once, then wait — no double-pings
- If you need an answer TODAY before end of business, use the phrase "**URGENT — need answer by EOD**" in the subject line

---

## Batch Review Calls (Google Meets)

### Format
- **When:** Typically Tuesday + Friday, 15 min each (scheduled in advance)
- **What:** Gray watches the entire batch of videos on screen share; you're muted unless answering a question
- **Length:** 5–10 min for Gray to watch all videos, 5 min for feedback discussion
- **Outcome:** Gray gives batch-level feedback + specific video notes + any revisions

### During the call
- Gray will pause and give real-time feedback
- Take notes on revisions — don't argue back during the call
- If you don't understand a note, ask for clarification: "Can you describe what you mean by 'tighter pacing on the middle section'?"
- After the call, Gray will send a written summary on Slack with detailed feedback

### After the call
- Slack message with written notes for each video (3–5 sentences each)
- Video timestamps if there are specific edits to redo
- Priority: which videos get revised first

---

## Revision Workflow

### Revision 1 (after first batch review)
- Editor makes fixes and re-delivers ENTIRE batch within 48 hours
- Gray reviews again on the next scheduled call
- Feedback typically lighter ("Tighter pacing on 3–5, looks great; video 2 caption needs line break")

### Revision 2 (if needed)
- Editor makes final fixes and re-delivers
- Gray watches and approves or asks for minor tweaks
- Goal: lock all videos by revision 2 so publish can happen

### After revision 2
- All videos should be locked and ready to publish
- If major changes are still needed (e.g., complete re-edit), it's a sign the direction wasn't clear — Gray will clarify in writing and ask for revision 3 (rare)

---

## Feedback Style

### What Gray gives you
- **Specific, not vague.** Not "faster pacing" — "cut every 2.5 seconds instead of 3 seconds" or "tighten the pauses between takes"
- **Section-specific.** "The hook is great; tighten the middle section; the closer feels rushed"
- **Comparative.** "Like the pacing in video 3, apply that to video 5" or "Reference video #7 at 0:15–0:30 shows the mood I want for video 2"

### What Gray needs from you if feedback is unclear
- Ask in Slack: "When you said 'tighter pacing,' did you mean X or Y?"
- Propose an example: "Should I cut this like [reference video], or did you mean something else?"
- Do NOT guess — unclear feedback wastes revision time

## Types of Feedback You'll Receive

### Technical feedback
"Video 2: The caption 'if you want to' is bleeding into the graphic at 0:15–0:20. Move it higher or shorten it to 'if you want'."

**Your action:** Fix the exact technical issue, re-deliver video 2 in the revision batch.

### Creative feedback
"Video 5 feels flat. Reference video #6 (the Sandcastles mood board I sent) — it has more energy in the cuts. Match that pacing and feel."

**Your action:** Study the reference video, feel the rhythm, apply that cut pattern to video 5.

### Tone feedback
"Video 3 is reading too corporate. Make it feel more casual — like Sai's just talking to a friend, not presenting. Slower cuts, more breathing room."

**Your action:** Rethink the pacing and energy. Slower, more conversational feel.

### Redundancy feedback
"Video 4 says the same thing as video 2 — they're back-to-back in the publish schedule. Can we tighten video 4 to just the first sentence + payoff? Or should we hold this one for a different week?"

**Your action:** Either tighten it or flag that it should skip this batch.

## Revision Round Rules

### What CAN change in a revision
- Pacing / cut timing
- Caption wording (minor adjustments)
- Caption position (move up/down/left/right)
- Graphic placement
- Audio levels
- Overall tone / mood

### What CANNOT change in a revision (starts a new batch)
- Completely different structure (e.g., reordering takes or removing entire sections — that's a re-edit)
- New B-roll or graphics (uses new assets not included in the original brief)
- Different hooks or hooks swapped (goes back to pre-production)

If something needs that level of change, Gray will tell you in the feedback: "This needs a structural rethink — let's discuss on the next Meets call instead of revising."

---

## Escalation (When Things Aren't Working)

### If feedback is unclear
→ Slack Gray immediately with a specific question. Don't wait, don't guess.

### If the revision takes longer than 48 hours
→ Update Slack: "Running 12 hours behind due to [reason]. Revised batch ready by EOD [date]." No penalty — just transparency so Gray can plan publishing.

### If you're not sure what the final quality should look like
→ Schedule a 15-min call to watch reference videos together. Gray can show you 2–3 examples of "nailed it" videos so you know the target.

### If the direction feels contradictory
→ Example: "First call you said 'faster cuts,' but second round feedback said 'too rushed.' I'm confused on what you want." Ask directly. Gray will clarify.
