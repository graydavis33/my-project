# Healthy Meals — NYC Budget (<$300/month)

For Gray: recomp macros (~2,850 kcal, ~190g protein/day), Type 1 diabetic (glucose-friendly = slow carbs, fat/protein/veg first, carbs last), gluten-free-ish, whole foods. Healthiest version + cheapest quality product for each.

## The #1 truth: cooking IS the budget AND the health fix
Currently eating out ~daily. One Chipotle = ~$13 = ~$390/mo for ONE meal a day. $300/mo total is only possible cooking ~everything. Same move that saves money flattens glucose (control the ingredients). This is THE lever.

## COFFEE (fix this first — your current version is the worst offender)
Current: coffee + creamer + heavy cream + honey.
- KILL the flavored creamer — it's seed oils + sugar (junk) and spikes you.
- KILL the honey — pure sugar spike (biggest glucose hit in your day for zero food value).
Healthy version: black coffee, OR coffee + splash of heavy cream or whole milk (heavy cream = zero sugar, T1D-friendly, keep it) + CINNAMON for sweetness (helps glucose slightly). Want it sweet? A few drops monk fruit or stevia = zero glucose impact.
Cheapest quality: store-brand/Trader Joe's whole beans or ground (~$6-8/bag, lasts weeks); heavy cream (~$5, lasts); cinnamon; monk fruit/stevia (one bag lasts forever).
Cost: pennies per cup.

## PROTEIN SHAKES (cheapest quality protein there is)
Base: 1-2 scoops whey + water or milk + ice. Then add:
- "Chocolate PB": + 1 tbsp PB + 1 tsp cocoa powder (+ milk)
- "Berry recovery": + frozen berries + Greek yogurt + handful spinach (you won't taste it)
- "Banana": + ½ frozen banana + PB (use berries if you want it flatter on glucose)
T1D tip: berries > banana for a flatter spike; add fat/fiber (PB, chia) to slow it. Post-lift shake pairs with your "lifting lowers me" pattern.
Cheapest quality whey: buy a 5 lb tub unflavored or lightly flavored (Nutricost, NOW, bulk brands, or ON on sale) = ~$0.70-1/serving vs ~$2 for single-serve. Look for 3rd-party tested (Informed Sport/NSF) when possible.

## PROTEIN DESSERTS (satisfy the sweet tooth, low glucose, high protein)
- Cottage cheese "ice cream": blend cottage cheese + scoop whey + frozen berries + sweetener, freeze 1-2h. High protein, low sugar, cheap, trendy for a reason.
- Greek yogurt bark: spread Greek yogurt on a tray + berries + dark chocolate chips, freeze, break into pieces.
- Chia pudding: chia + milk + whey + berries overnight. Slow carb = great T1D dessert.
- Protein mug cake: 1 scoop whey + 1 egg + 1 tsp cocoa + pinch baking powder, microwave ~60s.
- Dark chocolate (85%+): low sugar, T1D-friendly treat, a couple squares.
- Frozen banana + PB + dark chocolate bites.
Sweeten all with monk fruit/stevia = zero glucose. Cocoa powder is cheap and makes everything chocolate.

Proteins: Eggs (complete protein, choline, glucose-neutral anchor) · Chicken thighs (cheap muscle fuel, iron/zinc/flavor; air-chilled breast is a value upgrade — less water weight, better sear) · Ground beef 85/15 (iron/zinc/B12/natural creatine; zinc aids insulin) · Albacore tuna (cheap lean + omega-3; cap ~3-4 cans/wk = mercury) · Sardines (omega-3 + calcium + D + B12, very low mercury — elite food) · Whole-milk Greek yogurt (~17-20g/cup + probiotics, blunts glucose, sour-cream swap) · Cottage cheese (~24g/cup casein = slow, pre-bed muscle repair).
Carbs/produce: Fire-roasted peppers & onions (main veg now — fiber slows glucose) · Frozen brown rice (slower carb, portion-controlled, eat last) · Black beans (fiber + resistant starch = very slow glucose) · Sweet potatoes (slow carb, vit A, flatter than white potato) · Corn tortillas (GF, portion-controlled) · Frozen mixed berries (lowest-sugar/highest-antioxidant fruit = honey replacement) · Bananas (potassium, higher-GI — use half + pair with protein/PB).
