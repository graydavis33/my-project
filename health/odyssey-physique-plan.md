## ⚠️ READ FIRST — Type 1 Diabetes changes this
A sustained calorie deficit + fat loss + fewer/slower carbs = your insulin needs will DROP. Same doses on less food = hypos (esp. overnight + around lifting).
- Get endocrinologist sign-off BEFORE starting; expect to actively LOWER insulin (basal + carb ratios) as weight comes off.
- Dexcom is your referee — watch trends weekly, adjust with your endo.
- Gray's pattern (per endo): RUNNING spikes him, LIFTING lowers him → keep fast carbs handy when lifting.
- Gluten-free + whole-food + modest deficit = basically Protocol #1 (glucose flattening) turned up. Works WITH the diabetes.

## DELIVERABLE 2 — Training Split (4 days, calisthenics-biased, shoulder/back-friendly)
Equipment: home kit (adjustable DBs, KB, pull-up bar, bands) + dojo weights + bodyweight. Martial arts = SEPARATE/occasional (his cardio when he does it). Min effort on max-effort heavy barbell.
Frequency: 4 days/week (Upper/Lower x2 — each muscle 2x/wk). Add a 5th athletic/conditioning day when schedule allows.

### Day 1 — Upper A (push emphasis)
- Weighted or incline push-ups — 4 x 8–12 (rest 90s)
- DB shoulder press, seated neutral-grip (shoulder-friendly) — 3 x 8–12 (90s)
- Weighted pull-ups (band-assist if needed) — 4 x 6–10 (2 min) ← KEY progressive lift
- 1-arm DB row — 3 x 10–12 (75s)
- DB lateral raises — 3 x 12–15 (60s)
- Plank — 3 x 30–60s

### Day 2 — Lower A
- Bulgarian split squat (weighted) — 3–4 x 8–10/leg (90s) ← single-leg, knee/back-friendly
- DB/KB Romanian deadlift (moderate, hinge) — 3 x 10–12 (90s)
- Goblet squat — 3 x 10–12 (90s)
- KB swings — 3 x 15–20 (60s) power/conditioning
- Standing calf raise — 3 x 15 (45s)
- Hanging knee raise — 3 x 10–15

### Day 3 — Upper B (pull emphasis)
- Weighted pull-ups — 4 x 6–10 (2 min) ← KEY progressive lift
- DB floor/bench press — 3 x 8–12 (90s)
- Chin-ups or inverted rows — 3 x AMRAP (90s)
- Face pulls / band pull-aparts — 3 x 15–20 (45s) ← shoulder health
- DB curls — 2 x 12 / Triceps ext — 2 x 12 (60s)
- Side plank — 3 x 30s/side

### Day 4 — Lower B
- Weighted single-leg squat progression / Bulgarian split squat — 3–4 x 8–10/leg (90s)
- Single-leg RDL (DB/KB) — 3 x 10/leg (75s)
- Weighted step-ups — 3 x 10/leg (75s)
- Nordic curl / hamstring curl or extra KB swings — 3 x (60s)
- Bear crawls — 3 x ~20m
- Core circuit — 3 rounds

### Progressive overload across the block (double progression)
- Add reps within the range; when you hit the TOP of the range with good form on all sets → add weight (+2.5–5 lb) or +load on weighted calisthenics, drop back to bottom of range.
- Phase 1 (wk 1–4): Foundation. Groove movements, RPE 7, establish deficit. Log everything.
- Phase 2 (wk 5–8): Build. Add load to weighted pull-ups/push-ups, RPE 8.
- Phase 3 (wk 9–12): Intensify. Heaviest weighted calisthenics, tempo/dropsets, RPE 8–9. Definition showing.
- Phase 4 (wk 13–16, optional): Polish. Hold strength, push conditioning, final lean-out.
- DELOAD every 4–5 weeks (cut volume ~40%) — extra important in a deficit to protect recovery + avoid lows from overtraining.
- Cardio: daily NYC rucking = Zone 2 base (keep it). Martial arts when you do it = conditioning. Optional 1 extra Zone 2 session.

### T1D training rules (every session)
Fast carbs in your bag; check Dexcom before/during/after; lifting tends to LOWER you (his pattern) so watch for lows mid/post-session + overnight; ask endo about insulin around workout meals.

## DELIVERABLE 3 — Gluten-Free High-Protein Meal Framework
Whole foods, ~2,650 kcal, ~190g protein. Aligns with Protocol #1 (veg first, carbs last, walk after). Gray already eats mostly GF.

### Sample day (~2,650 kcal / ~190g protein)
- Morning: black coffee (drop/minimize honey). Optional if breaking fast: whey shake. (Fasted-ish AM is fine for him.)
- Meal 1 (12–2): Chipotle-style bowl — DOUBLE chicken, fajita veggies, lettuce, black beans, ½ portion rice (or skip), cheese, guac, salsa. No tortilla. (~650 kcal, ~55g protein) — eat rice/beans LAST.
  - Swap option: protein pancakes (4 eggs, 1 banana, whey scoop, 1–2 tbsp PB, ≤1 tbsp honey/berries).
- Meal 2 (post-workout / afternoon): whey + berries, OR Greek yogurt + berries + handful nuts. (~400 kcal, ~40g protein)
- Meal 3 (dinner, 2–3h before bed): lean protein — salmon/steak/chicken OR his tuna+sardine+egg mashup — + big salad/veg + olive oil + small potato or rice. (~700–800 kcal, ~60g protein) — veg first.
- Snacks to fill gaps: sardines, cottage cheese, Greek yogurt, jerky, portioned nuts, berries.

### Gluten-free swaps (mainly = cut bread/beer/pasta/pizza calories)
- Bread/wrap → lettuce wrap or certified GF bread
- Pasta → chickpea/lentil/edamame pasta or zucchini noodles
- Pizza → cauliflower crust or skip
- Tortilla → bowl
- Beer → limit alcohol anyway (delayed lows); if drinking, spirits/wine in moderation
- Breaded/fried → grilled/baked

### Grocery list
Protein: chicken breast/thigh, lean ground beef/turkey, salmon, canned tuna + sardines, eggs, Greek yogurt, cottage cheese, whey.
Veg/fruit: broccoli, spinach, mixed greens, peppers, onions, avocado, tomatoes, berries, banana.
Carbs (controlled/slow): sweet potato, white/brown rice, black beans, oats (GF).
Fats: olive oil, nuts, natural peanut/almond butter, cheese.
Staples: salsa, spices, GF seasonings, coffee, electrolytes (or salt).

## DELIVERABLE 5 — Medical Sign-Off Callouts (do these)
1. BEFORE starting: endocrinologist reviews the plan. RECOMP at maintenance = less insulin disruption than a cut, but training changes + fewer/slower carbs still shift needs. Get sign-off.
2. Hypo watch: lifting lowers Gray, esp. OVERNIGHT. Fast carbs always on hand; dial Dexcom low alarm. (Lower risk than a deficit, but still real.)
3. Creatine + kidney labs: tell doctor he takes creatine (mildly raises serum creatinine — don't misread as kidney decline). Check kidney function.
4. Bloodwork baseline (piggyback): Vit D, ferritin/iron, B12, omega-3 index, magnesium, zinc, full metabolic panel, A1c, lipids, BP.
5. Stop/reassess if: lows increase, energy/mood/recovery tanks, losing >1 lb/wk, or lifts collapse → likely deficit too big or insulin not adjusted.
6. Floor: don't go under ~2,200 kcal or skimp protein — muscle loss + poor glucose control.
