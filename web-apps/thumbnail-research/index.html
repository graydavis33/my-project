01 CTR Cheat Sheet

Hard numbers from the 1of10 / Quasa 323,000-video study (62.6B views analyzed). These are load-bearing — every pattern below rests on them.

+36%
CTR lift from a face in finance/business specifically — vs near-zero broadly and -3% in gaming. Faces matter MORE in Sai's niche than most.
−19%
CTR penalty for thumbs with text — UNLESS the text is under 10 characters and covers less than 7% of the image. The exception is exactly what Hormozi/Gadzhi exploit.
2–5
Dominant text word-count range across all top performers. 6+ is rare and usually only when one element is a captioned screenshot.
100–110
Optimal brightness on the 0–255 scale. Dark thumbs disappear in feed; blown-out thumbs read flat. Goldilocks zone.
cyan / green / yellow / orange
Highest-performing accent colors against YouTube's white/gray UI. Sai's brand orange is a tailwind, not a coincidence.
2026 shift
Cluttered shock-face + heavy text is fatiguing. Cleaner composition, bolder contrast, more negative space is winning. Big number on minimal BG is the new dominant.

03 Browse by Pattern

The 14 reusable visual patterns that show up across the niche. Each card explains the recipe + shows real examples from creators who do it best.

⚓ Single Anchor Word

Used by: Alex Hormozi, Dan Koe, Leila Hormozi

Definition. Exactly ONE word + face. Under 10 characters, under 7% of frame area — escapes the algorithm's text penalty entirely.

Why it works. Maximum click curiosity per pixel. The 1of10/Quasa study found text adds CTR penalty UNLESS it's under 10 chars covering <7% of image — the single-anchor-word pattern hits both bounds.

recipe: Face + black or dark BG + ONE accent (yellow #FFD93D usually).

🎯 Authority Portrait + Anchor Word

Used by: Alex Hormozi, Iman Gadzhi, Sharran Srivatsaa, Leila Hormozi

Definition. Half-body shot of creator, calm/serious expression (NOT shock-face), occupying 40–50% of frame. Single anchor word or 2–4 word claim in the negative space on the opposite side.

Why it works. In finance/business specifically, 'calm authority' reads as proof. Faces lift CTR +36% in this niche. Reads as 'I know something you don't' rather than 'look at me.'

recipe: Desaturated/dark BG + face + 1 accent color (yellow or white).

$ Big Dollar Number

Used by: Charlie Morgan, Jordan Welch, Brett Malinowski, Codie Sanchez, Noah Kagan

Definition. A specific large dollar figure ($1M, $600K, $100K/month) is the visual hero — bigger than any other text, in a bright accent, with contrasting outline. Face is secondary.

Why it works. Specific number > generic adjective. '$600,000' beats 'huge amount.' The eye locks on digits because they're cognitively easier than words to process at thumbnail scale. Specificity also reads as 'real / not exaggerated.'

recipe: Number in bright accent (yellow / green / orange) with black or contrasting outline. Face/screenshot supporting.

→ Transformation Arc

Used by: Jordan Welch, Charlie Morgan

Definition. Two states shown side-by-side or with a directional arrow. Old state (cold, gray, smaller) → new state (warm, bright, larger). Number-arrow-number is the cleanest form: '$0 → $1M', 'Year 1 → Year 5'.

Why it works. Tells a micro-story in one image. The brain wants to fill the gap between the two states — that gap is the click. Especially powerful in retrospective content where you HAVE both states.

recipe: Cold/desaturated left → warm/saturated right, with one accent color (often orange or yellow) as the arrow.

05 Application to Sai

Where Sai's existing "Year 5 of Business" thumbnail sits relative to the patterns above — what it does right, what it leaves on the table, and two concrete variant proposals grounded in the dominant patterns.

What Sai's "Year 5" Already Does Right

  • Big orange "5" as hero number — hits single-anchor-word + authority-portrait
  • NYC skyline = aspirational lifestyle BG — hits aspirational-bokeh
  • Brand orange #F28129 is one of the algorithm's preferred accent colors against YouTube's UI
  • Avoids the shock-face mistake — calm authority is correct for the wealth-mentor lane

What's Missing vs Top Performers

  1. No specific dollar / outcome number. The "5" is years, not a result. Every other creator anchors a result — "$1M", "$600K", "30 clients". Adding a money figure or follower count unlocks big-dollar-number.
  2. No transformation-arc. A "Year 1 → Year 5" or "$0 → $X" version IS the retrospective's natural framing.
  3. No arrow / circle emphasis. Brett/Jordan/Charlie all use a red or orange marker to point at the payoff. Sai's orange brand color is begging to be used as that marker.
  4. No screenshot-as-prop. A blurred dashboard, IG follower count, or calendar would add the "receipts" coding competitors have.
  5. Single-side composition not maximized. Face is roughly centered; the "5" overlaps the right. A cleaner half-and-half (face left, big number right, full bleed) is the Hormozi/Gadzhi formula.

Variant A — Transformation Arc

Pattern stack: transformation-arc + big-dollar-number + arrow-and-circle

LEFT half: desaturated 2021-era Sai shot (B&W treatment if no archive footage). Neutral expression. Muted grey BG. RIGHT half: full-color Sai in the blue suit + orange tie. Confident smirk. NYC daylight, blurred. BETWEEN: a bold orange #F28129 arrow sweeping left→right with thin black outline. TEXT TOP-LEFT: YEAR 1 in white. TEXT TOP-RIGHT: YEAR 5 in orange. Optional small badge on the arrow: a specific number ($XM, follower count, "30+ HIRES").

Why it works: hits 3 dominant patterns simultaneously, uses Sai's orange as the arrow accent (same role Brett/Jordan use red for), year-to-year framing is the natural retrospective fit. Eye flow reads left-to-right.

Variant B — Authority Portrait + Anchor Number

Pattern stack: authority-portrait + dark-cinematic + single-anchor-word

Sai LEFT 45% of frame. Tighter crop than the current version — head + shoulders + hint of chest. Heavy Gaussian blur on the NYC window so it reads cinematic bokeh, not literal NYC. RIGHT 55%: largely empty, with a GIANT orange #F28129 "5" filling ~75% of frame height in heavy condensed black sans (Anton or Inter Black) with a 4px black outline. Above: YEAR in Montserrat Black 72pt white. Below: OF BUSINESS in 56pt white. Total text: 11 chars + 1 numeric anchor — under the algorithm's text penalty threshold.

Why it works: this is the Hormozi/Gadzhi formula reskinned in Sai's brand. The current thumb is 80% of this — the move is: tighter face crop, cleaner half-and-half split, much bigger and more dominant "5", blurred NYC instead of crisp NYC, no overlap of face + "5". The cinematic minimalist treatment is the 2026 trend the niche is converging on.

The A/B/C Test Framework (Any Sai Long-Form)

For any Sai long-form, ship 3 thumbnails to test:

  • One Authority — number-as-hero, smug-smirk, current style refined (Variant B above)
  • One Lifestyle — cinematic 3/4 profile, blurred skyline as hero, minimal text (Gadzhi school)
  • One Outcome — big specific dollar figure or follower count + screenshot prop (Morgan/Welch school)

DOAC tests 100+ thumbs via Meta ads pre-launch. We're not there yet, but 3-variant A/B testing is the minimum starting discipline.
