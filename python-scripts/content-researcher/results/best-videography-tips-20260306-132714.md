## TOP 5 HOOK PATTERNS

**Pattern 1: The Time Contract**
*Why it works:* It eliminates the viewer's #1 fear (wasting time) by naming the cost upfront and attaching a specific, measurable outcome. The more precise the numbers, the more credible the promise feels.
*Example from data:* *"Give me 9min, and I'll improve your storytelling skills by 176%"* — Philipp Humm (Video 8)

**Pattern 2: The Universal Unlock**
*Why it works:* Words like "ANY," "EVERY," and "MUST" signal that no viewer is excluded by their gear, budget, or experience level. It removes the self-selecting objection ("this probably isn't for me") before the viewer can form it.
*Example from data:* *"How to make ANY video CINEMATIC"* — lacapturevisuals (Video 9)

**Pattern 3: The Embarrassingly Basic Fix**
*Why it works:* It exploits a gap between how simple the solution is and how impactful the result is. Viewers share it because it's the kind of tip that makes you feel foolish for not knowing — and that emotional reaction drives saves and re-shares.
*Example from data:* *"how to hold the phone for a pro videography 🔥🤯"* — Shotbymobile (Video 2)

**Pattern 4: The Time-Stamped Micro-Lesson**
*Why it works:* Advertising the exact runtime ("60 seconds") functions as a zero-friction commitment device. The viewer's decision calculus shifts from "do I want to learn this?" to "do I have 60 seconds?" — almost always yes.
*Example from data:* *"The Rule of Thirds for Beginners | 60 seconds"* — Matt Johnson (Video 3)

**Pattern 5: The Curiosity-Baited Absolute**
*Why it works:* Claiming something is "PERFECT" or "MUST" triggers a fact-checking instinct — viewers click to verify or disprove, not just to learn. The click happens before the viewer even decides they're interested in the topic.
*Example from data:* *"The PERFECT Camera 📷😏"* — Anthony Gugliotta (Video 10)
