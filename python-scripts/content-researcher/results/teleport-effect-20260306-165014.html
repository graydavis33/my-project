TOP 5 HOOK PATTERNS

1. The Labor Revelation Hook

Pattern: Lead with an outrageous amount of effort before showing the result. Creates an immediate empathy + curiosity combo — viewers feel the creator's pain before they see the payoff, making the reveal hit harder.

Why it works: It reframes entertainment as a confession. You're not watching a video, you're witnessing a sacrifice. The contrast between input (hours/pain) and output (cool effect) is emotionally satisfying.

Example from data: "9 HOURS of editing later😱🔥" — Video 3, 3.7M views.

2. The Casual Witness Hook

Pattern: Write the title like a surprised bystander texting their friend. Informal, lowercase-adjacent language with an emoji that mimics genuine reaction.

Why it works: It removes the "content creator" artifice and makes the video feel like something you stumbled across rather than something marketed at you. Social proof is baked into the title itself.

Example from data: "Bro teleported 👀" — Video 4, 17.5M views.

3. The Unexplained Power Hook

Pattern: Take a well-known phenomenon (a character's ability, a visual effect) and reframe it as a mystery that needs explaining. "How does X actually work?"

Why it works: It exploits the brain's need for closure. If you already know Gojo can teleport, the title implies you're missing why — and incomplete information creates compulsive clicking behavior.

Example from data: "How did Gojo able to use TELEPORTATION??" — Video 7, 806K views.

4. The Free Asset Hook

Pattern: Lead with the deliverable and make the cost explicit: free. The word "free" is one of the most psychologically potent words in any title for creator-targeted content.

Why it works: It filters in the exact person who needs the asset right now and removes every purchase objection in two words. The viewer doesn't watch for entertainment — they watch to take something.

Example from data: "Anime Teleport Sound Effects (Free to Use)" — Video 1, 50K views on a 14-subscriber channel.

5. The Accessibility Hook

Pattern: Add "phone" or "CapCut" to a sophisticated-sounding effect to signal that it's achievable without expensive gear or software.

Why it works: It eliminates the viewer's #1 excuse for not trying ("I don't have the tools"). Democratizing a pro-level effect is aspirational and immediately actionable, which drives saves as much as views.

Example from data: "Teleport effect phone editing tutorial!" — Video 6, 10.29x outlier score with 4.6% like rate.
