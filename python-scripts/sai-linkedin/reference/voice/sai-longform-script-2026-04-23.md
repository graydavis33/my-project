# Sai Karra — Long Form Video Script (2026-04-23)

Theme: Build your business the right way

---

I'm 21 years old, my business has made millions of dollars, I've amassed millions of followers on social media and I currently run my advertising agency working with some of the biggest brands in the world.

But it wasn't always this way. In fact, the past 5 years of my life have followed a very specific theme. I would hit things big, early. All for it to completely collapse.

There's 3 specific lessons the gods of business have been trying to teach me, and once I finally understood them, I actually started to build my business the right way, which has allowed me to scale way bigger than any previous time.

And it actually all started when I was 16 when I got hit with every teenager's dream. I went ultra viral on social media, amassing hundreds of millions of views per month and getting millions of followers.

I was a freshman in high school when covid had hit, and my brother had just come home from college with a breakthrough. He had cracked virality on TikTok.

He knew it was the next big thing, so he sat me down and told me about why I should start posting alongside him.

I had just made my high school basketball team around that time and had gained a few popularity points. I did NOT want to erase that by putting my face on tiktok.

But he asked me "if you got millions of views on your videos and hundreds of thousands of followers, do you think you'd earn back your popularity points?"

And so I started posting and just within a few months, I had gained hundreds of thousands of followers.

But I quickly realized fame was a super overrated thing. Why? Because it wasn't making me any money.

So I started to do sponsorships for brands to make money. And within my first month of doing it, I made my first $10,000.

And that was when I got addicted.

Everytime a post didn't do well, I wouldn't make as much money.

So I thought to myself, regardless of how well a post does, how can I extract as much money as possible from this?

Then the idea hit, part 2 stories.

Leave viewers on a cliffhanger right before you get to the point and tell them to follow you for part 2.

This plan in my head was genius for 2 reasons.

1) I would get way more followers
2) I could promote sponsorships way more explicitly in my part 2 because TikTok wouldn't shadow ban a video that your followers watch

And as expected, I started getting an insane amount of followers, while making an insane amount of money.

But, I wanted more.

I started more accounts doing the same thing.

Just as I felt like I was on top of the world, the virality had stopped. That type of content stopped working.

Overnight, I went broke because I was spending all of the money I was making, hoping i'd keep make more and more.

That's when the gods of business taught me my first lesson.

Don't take shortcuts that break trust - even if they work.

People would watch my content hoping they'd get something valuable out of it. But I broke their trust by not sharing the full video and sometimes even sending them to other platforms for part 3's to make even more money.

When someone gives you their attention or money, don't get greedy and break their trust.

Just as I had learned this lesson, I was presented with the opportunity of a lifetime to make generational wealth.

I had gotten some of the biggest names to advise me on my NFT project. NBA all stars, founders of billion dollar companies, A list celebrities.

With these people behind my company, I knew that I couldn't fail.

We were projecting to make $5m from selling out our NFT project.

So I told the entire world about it. I hired friends as interns with promises on equity if things worked out, attended conferences all around the US, and would constantly remind family and friends about how much money we were about to make.

And I still remember to this day something I had told my intern team over call.

I said "trust me guys, if I put my mind to something, it will work. We will sell out our project and we will all be set for life"

We had spent hundreds of thousands of dollars on this NFT company and the time had finally come to launch it to the world.

But just then, the NFT market collapsed. Everything we had planned for years came crashing down.

All the money we've ever made, down the drain. All the good will we had built with our advisors, gone. And more than anything, the promises I had made to our interns, broken.

That's when I had learned my second lesson from the gods of business.

Don't rely on others to scale your business, and don't overpromise anything to anyone.

I had finally understood it. I was ready for my next venture, this time, with wisdom that wouldn't fail me.

In the midst of all of this, I was still running my advertising business with a few clients. I treated it as a side project up until that point until I realized it had real potential as a business.

But my monkey brain couldn't focus on just 1 thing. So I decided to start another arm of the agency.

You see, up until that point we had followed the basic agency model. Make content for clients on a monthly basis, a done for you model.

But I then came up with this genius idea: teach founders how to make content instead of us making it for them. A done with you model.

And it worked. We were signing clients left and right and hiring people to the team.

It had taken up 95% of my time through the day, but little did I remember that we still had clients on the done for you side that were paying us.

And one client in particular was paying us over $20k a month to help them with their content.

But I stopped caring because I was so distracted with the other side of the business.

We'd keep missing deadlines with this client, not following through on requests and kind of just getting by.

And then one day, the founder of the company hopped on a call with us and went ballistic. It was the hardest call of my life. He tore us apart and told us that we should refund all of our clients and we don't deserve to have an agency.

It sucked at the time. But it was what I needed to hear. He was absolutely right. We didn't deserve to have an agency.

And that's when I learned the crucial lesson number 3 from the business gods. Obsess over your customer, nothing else matters.

We shut down the coaching program and told ourselves from here on out that we would focus. Even if it meant we sign way less clients or don't make nearly enough money. We wanted to just build something we were truly proud of that actually served our customers.

And that's what led us to where we are today. At the time of recording this, we only have 5 clients. But those 5 clients pay us more money each month than 30 clients paid at a point of time.

These 3 experiences were all incredibly difficult at first. But they forced me to transform my business to be something truly valuable.

If I could leave you with one thing, here's what it would be.

Do truthful work and the universe will reward you accordingly.

No competition, no AI, no external forces can ever break you when you build in truth.

And more than anything, you'll go to bed peacefully at night knowing that you are doing the right thing.

This is Sai Karra, and thanks for watching.
