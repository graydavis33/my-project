## 2. Long-Form Weekly Series Design

### 🎥 Primary Visual / Editing Style Reference: Liam Ottley Vlogs

**Why it's the right reference:**
- Liam = 23-year-old AI agency CEO (Morningside AI), based Dubai
- Sai = 21-year-old UGC agency COO, based NYC
- Near-identical vertical position (young agency-founder in tech/creator space)
- Liam's **split channel strategy** (main education channel + vlog/BTS channel) mirrors exactly what we can build for Sai

**Style signature to emulate:**
- **Clean, crisp, documentary-cinematic** — NOT over-edited, NOT over-graded, NOT heavy motion graphics
- **Authentic / real** — makes real life look cinematic without staging
- **Light color grade** — feels "normal," not over-teal-and-orange
- **Voiceover over B-roll heavy** — narrates over visuals of him doing the thing, minimal cuts back to talking head
- **Location-driven** — cities, travel, meetings give show a sense of motion. NYC will carry huge weight here.
- **Chill ambient electronic music** — rarely a "big" track. Music drops out for emphasis.
- **Text overlays for context/dates** — simple, unobtrusive
- **Screen recordings** of real work — dashboards, client ads, internal tools
- **Kinetic but not aggressive cuts** — propulsive without jumpy

**AVOID (per Gray's feedback):**
- Over-the-top zooms, whoosh transitions, Instagram-core cuts
- Over-saturated grades
- YouTube-intro energy first 15s
- Motivational-montage feel

**Action before first shoot:** Gray watches 3 most-viewed Liam Ottley Vlogs episodes tonight + screenshots 10–15 reference frames per shot category.

## 3. Short-Form Strategy

### Core Philosophy — Iterate, Don't Perfect
Per memory: **test 30% wild, double down 70% on what works.** Week 1–2 tests 5–7 formats across 10+ posts. By Week 3 top 2–3 known. Week 4 we double down.

Per 2026 research:
- **Sweet spot:** 60–90 seconds (outperforms 15s on most platforms)
- **Platform tuning:** TikTok 15–30s, YouTube Shorts 60–90s, Reels 30–60s
- **Never cross-post identical files** — re-cut per platform
- **Winners post to 3–5 platforms simultaneously**

### 7 Starter Formats (Updated With Sai's Actual Story)

#### Format 1 — "Hot Take" (Counterintuitive)
Face-cam, direct address. One strong opinion.
- **Hook:** "Everyone says [conventional wisdom]. They're wrong."
- **Why:** Zuckerberg pattern → 1.55M views from 2,440 subs (635x outlier). Highest short-form hook lever in your data.

#### Format 2 — "BTS of an Ad"
B-roll of the team making a client ad + Sai VO.
- **Hook:** "Here's how we made [client brand] go viral."
- **Production:** Need client permission (start with publicly-known clients: YC, Mindvalley, Justin Kan, Checkmate).
- **Why:** proves Trendify's capability on-screen. Recruits talent + attracts clients.

#### Format 3 — "21 and…"
Age-anchored, minimal cuts.
- **Hook:** "21 and [specific milestone]." Example: "21 and running an agency with $700K in revenue."
- **Why:** age is a built-in hook right now.

#### Format 4 — "Day X of Building Trendify" (Numbered Journey)
Serialized daily short. 1 day = 1 video. One thing that happened.
- **Hook:** "Day [N] of building Trendify. Today [X]."
- **Why:** StartupboyAnish "Day 1/100" pulled 49.6x outlier. Your research: **"lowest-cost, highest-leverage format in the entire dataset."**

#### Format 5 — "Response to Creator" (Borrowed Authority)
Sai reacts to another creator's video/tweet.
- **Hook:** "[Creator] said [X]. Here's what they're missing."
- **Why:** rides existing virality. Top founder-vlog outliers are react-clips.

#### Format 6 — "Client Win / Number Reveal"
Specific Trendify win — closed client, ad approved, launch, result.
- **Hook:** "We just hit [specific number / milestone]. Here's the story."
- **Why:** DorianGroup82 specific-number reveal → 11.6M views. Specificity signals truth.

#### Format 7 — "The Meeting Clip" (Raw Access)
Compelling clip from real Trendify meeting.
- **Hook:** usually no hook — straight into the moment with context overlay
- **Why:** voyeuristic authenticity. Same "Raw" framing that drove Will Phillips' 12x outlier.

### 2 Additional Formats Added Post-Research

#### Format 8 — "Brother Banter" (if Srikar participates)
Sai + Srikar trading takes in one shot. Sibling tension/chemistry = organic virality.
- **Hook:** one brother starts a claim, the other finishes or contradicts.
- **Why:** Gen Z LOVES sibling content. Vlogbrothers precedent. Built-in differentiator.

#### Format 9 — "Lessons From BuiltGen"
War stories from the previous agency.
- **Hook:** "At BuiltGen we [made this mistake / figured out this thing]."
- **Why:** BuiltGen generated 20M+ leads — there's a library of learnings. Credibility + usefulness.

#### Format 10 — "Letter to My 15-Year-Old Self"
Sai at 21 telling his 15-year-old self — who just started his first business — what he wishes he knew. One lesson per video, direct to camera.
- **Hook:** "If I could go back to 15 when I started my first business, here's what I'd say." / "At 15, I thought [X]. At 21, I know [Y]." / "The one thing I'd tell 15-year-old me about [money / partners / failure / clients / scaling]."
- **Why:** Age-gap reflection is one of the highest-performing formats on TikTok/Reels — it's relatable (everyone has regrets), aspirational (he built TWO agencies in that gap), and infinitely repeatable (one lesson per video = dozens of episodes). The 15→21 age range hits Gen Z's exact demographic. Built-in series potential.
- **Series structure:** Each video = one specific lesson. Title pattern: "What I'd tell my 15-year-old self about [topic]." Can rotate through: first clients, money mistakes, partnerships, burnout, hiring, social media, confidence, rejection, family business dynamics with Srikar, etc.
