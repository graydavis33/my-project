# Sai Founders Series — Long-Form Structure

**Date:** 2026-05-02
**Status:** Template v1 — first long-form episode
**Series concept:** "MTV Cribs meets School of Hard Knocks." Sai interviews founders/CEOs in their offices. Interview Q&A is the spine; founder-led office tour is the brand signature.
**Companion docs:** `python-scripts/founders-series/debrief.py` (founder research), memory `project_founders_series.md` (filming SOP).

---

## Spine

- **Primary:** interview Q&A (School of Hard Knocks-style — money, journey, lessons, advice).
- **Brand differentiator:** founder-led office tour with founder mic'd up. Nobody else doing this format combines both.
- **Bed material:** silent B-roll captured separately, used under interview audio for visual texture.

## Target runtime

8–10 min sweet spot. Floor 6 min, ceiling 12 min. Runtime depends on coverage, question count, and office size — but the three-act shape stays constant.

---

## Three-Act Template

| # | Beat | Time | Footage / Audio | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Cold open hook | 0:00–0:15 | Single founder soundbite (the killer line, e.g. "I was sleeping on my friend's couch") | Pulled from the interview. This IS the thumbnail moment + title hook. Pick this clip first when editing. |
| 2 | Day montage | 0:15–0:45 | Fast-cut B-roll from the day, music-driven | Sets tone, shows production value. 6–10 shots, ~3s each. |
| 3 | Founder framing | 0:45–1:15 | Sai direct address OR text overlay + founder intro line | "Today we're with [name], CEO of [company], doing [X]." Establishes who/where/why. |
| 4 | Walk-in montage | 1:15–1:30 | Shorter B-roll burst — getting to the office | Transition device. Half the length of the day montage. |
| 5 | **Act 1: Origin** | 1:30–3:30 | Interview Q&A (2–3 questions) + B-roll bed under audio | The hook payoff. Pays off the cold open line. Questions: how they started, the early days, the couch-to-here arc. |
| 6 | **Tour interlude** | 3:30–4:30 | Founder-led on-camera tour, founder voice driving | Standalone beat. This is the brand signature — give it room. Don't bury it under interview audio. |
| 7 | **Act 2: Business** | 4:30–6:30 | Interview Q&A (3–4 questions) + B-roll bed | What the business does, revenue, growth, hard moments, biggest mistake. |
| 8 | **Act 3: Wisdom drop** | 6:30–8:30 | Closing interview Q&A + outro card | "What would you tell your 15-year-old self?" → "What's your advice for aspiring founders?" → strongest single line gets the outro card / next-episode tease. |

**Total target:** ~8:30. Pad Act 2 to extend; trim cold open + montages to compress.

---

## Editing rules

- **Cold open clip is locked first.** Identify it before timeline assembly. Everything else is built around it.
- **Tour interlude is sacred.** No interview audio over it. If the founder is talking on the tour, that's the audio. Silent moments OK.
- **Silent B-roll only goes under interview audio in Acts 1, 2, 3.** Never use it as a standalone beat — it has no voice driving it.
- **Music dips under every interview answer.** Returns under montages and tour transitions.
- **No dead air between acts.** Use a 0.5–1s sound design hit (whoosh, riser, ambient swell) to bridge act → act.
- **Pacing rule of thumb:** every interview answer averages 30–45 seconds on screen. If it's longer, it needs B-roll cuts. If it's shorter, it gets paired with the next answer.

## Shooting implications (for next shoot)

To make this template work cleanly, the day's capture needs:

- [ ] **One killer soundbite** for the cold open — actively listen during the interview and re-prompt if the line isn't there ("Can you say that again, but with..." )
- [ ] **8–10 founder-led tour beats** — mic'd up, walking through the office, naming rooms/objects/people. Even 30 seconds each gives the editor options.
- [ ] **15+ silent B-roll shots** of the office — desks, products, walls, art, employees working. Both wide and detail.
- [ ] **3 montage-worthy "day" shots** outside the office — arrival, walk-in, exterior. These feed Beat 2 and Beat 4.
- [ ] **One direct-address moment** from Sai (or founder) — for Beat 3 framing.
- [ ] **Interview structure:** roughly 2 origin questions, 3–4 business questions, 2 wisdom-drop questions. ~8 questions total. (Pull from `interview-questions.md` generated by `debrief.py`.)

## Episode workflow

1. **Pre-shoot:** Run `python debrief.py "Founder Name" "Company Name"` → review `debrief.md` + `interview-questions.md`.
2. **Shoot day:** Capture per the shooting checklist above.
3. **Organize:** Run footage organizer next day (per existing edit-same-day cadence — actually long-form follows organize-then-edit).
4. **Edit:**
   - Step 1: Find the cold-open soundbite, mark it.
   - Step 2: Lay out the three-act spine (Acts 1, 2, 3 sit-down audio).
   - Step 3: Drop the tour interlude between Act 1 and Act 2.
   - Step 4: Cut B-roll bed under each interview answer.
   - Step 5: Build cold open + day montage + framing + walk-in montage.
   - Step 6: Outro + sound design + music + color.
5. **Publish:** YouTube long-form. Pull a vertical short from the cold-open clip + Act 1 origin payoff for shorts cross-post.
