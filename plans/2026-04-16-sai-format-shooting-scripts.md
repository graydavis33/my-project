# Sai Content — Shooting Scripts for Every Format & Series

**For:** Sai Karra personal brand (Gray = Creative Director)
**Date:** 2026-04-16
**Source:** built on top of `plans/2026-04-15-sai-debrief-content-strategy.md`

Each entry has the same shape so it's easy to scan on set:

- **Script** — beat-by-beat of what happens on camera
- **Shots** — what we actually point the camera at
- **Setting** — where we film it
- **Editing** — how it gets cut together

## LONG-FORM SERIES

### Primary Weekly Series — *Inside Trendify* / *The Karra Brothers*

- **Script (4-beat weekly structure, 6–10 min):**
  - 0:00–0:15 — Cold open: best moment of the week, mid-scene, no intro
  - 0:15–1:00 — Weekly context: "This week was supposed to be [X]…"
  - 1:00–7:00 — Three mini-chapters (~2 min each): client work / team / personal take
  - 7:00–end — Cliffhanger: one concrete tease of next week
- **Shots:**
  - Sai at desk, morning contemplative
  - Walking NYC streets (handheld, shot from behind, shot from front)
  - Meetings — 2-shot of Sai + Srikar, over-shoulder, reaction cutaways
  - Screen captures of real Trendify dashboards + client ads
  - B-roll: hands typing, whiteboard, team lunch, elevator, Midtown skyline
  - Locked-off talking-head in 2–3 locations for monologue flexibility
- **Setting:** Trendify NYC office (305 W 50th St), NYC streets, coffee shops, cabs — city as character, Liam Ottley style
- **Editing:**
  - Light color grade — NOT teal-and-orange
  - Voiceover over B-roll heavy, never hold talking head >12s without interrupt
  - Opening 60s cut every 4–8s; body holds 10–15s
  - Chill lo-fi electronic; music drops out on emotional beats
  - Text overlays for context/dates — small, unobtrusive sans-serif
  - End card: "Next week…" tease over one sharp image

### Episode 1 — *How I Built My 2nd Agency Before 22*

- **Script (10–12 min, one-time pilot):**
  - 0:00–0:15 — "My brother and I just built our second agency. We're 21 and 23. Here's what happened." Cut to title.
  - 0:15–3:30 — Story arc VO: basketball mixtapes at 11 → TikTok 1M in 2021 → BuiltGen (20M leads) → Trendify pivot
  - 3:30–5:30 — What Trendify is now: show don't tell. YC / Mindvalley ads on monitors. Overlay: "$722K ARR, 2x YoY"
  - 5:30–7:30 — Why Season 1 matters: stakes, team ambitions, what's being built
  - 7:30–9:30 — Tease upcoming client work, hires, challenges
  - 9:30–end — "Subscribe. Next week we're inside [specific thing]."
- **Shots:**
  - Sai + Srikar 2-shot in office (duo thumbnail candidate)
  - BuiltGen archival screenshots, old social posts, early agency footage
  - Real client ads playing on monitors (with Sai watching)
  - NYC establishing: skyline, 305 W 50th St exterior, subway
  - Slow push-ins on Sai delivering key lines (locked-off + insert transition)
- **Setting:** Trendify office main floor, NYC rooftop/skyline, Midtown streets
- **Editing:**
  - Cold open: silence or ambient only, NO music first 10s
  - VO-over-B-roll carries the story arc — talking head only for emphasis
  - Archive photos: quick Ken Burns, 1–2s max per image
  - Overlay numbers as they're spoken ($722K, 20M leads, 50+ clients)
  - End on quiet held frame + subscribe card — no motivational swell

### Series A — *The Monthly Report* (Monthly, 8–12 min)

- **Script:**
  - 0:00–0:20 — Cold open: "Here's what actually happened at Trendify in [Month]."
  - 0:20–2:00 — Wins: new clients, biggest win, directional metric
  - 2:00–5:00 — Losses: what went wrong, lost clients (directional, no names), surprises
  - 5:00–8:00 — Lessons: what we'd change, what we learned
  - 8:00–end — What's changing next month + tease
- **Shots:**
  - Single sit-down Sai, chest-up, slight off-axis
  - Inserts: dashboards, Slack messages (blurred if needed), whiteboard recaps
  - One or two walk-and-talks if the lesson is better delivered in motion
- **Setting:** Office — one location, one session. Monthly = low production overhead
- **Editing:**
  - Chapter markers in YouTube description (Wins / Losses / Lessons / What's Next)
  - Light B-roll cutaways every 15–20s to break up talking head
  - Lower-third text when naming clients, numbers, dates
  - No hype music — ambient bed only
  - Thumbnail: month + one-word sentiment ("BRUTAL" / "WIN")

### Series B — *How to Build an Agency from the Ground Up* (Limited, 10–15 min)

- **Script (per episode):**
  - 0:00–0:15 — Cold open: the ONE counterintuitive truth of this episode
  - 0:15–1:30 — Context: "I've done this twice — BuiltGen and Trendify. Here's the version that actually works."
  - 1:30–8:00 — Walk through the steps with specific examples from both agencies
  - 8:00–12:00 — The mistakes Sai made and what he does now instead
  - 12:00–end — Homework / action step for the viewer + tease next episode
- **Shots:**
  - Whiteboard deep-dives (camera angled slightly from the side, not dead-on)
  - Over-shoulder screen shares of real pitch decks, proposals, contracts (redacted)
  - Walk-and-talk transitions between sections — NYC streets as breather
  - Insert shots: handshake B-roll, notebook writing, phone calls
- **Setting:** Whiteboard room at Trendify + one NYC walk-and-talk location per episode
- **Editing:**
  - Chaptered structure visible in the video (text titles between sections)
  - Speed-ramped transitions between chapters — subtle, not aggressive
  - Callout graphics when specific numbers/frameworks are mentioned
  - End card: "Next episode → [specific topic]" — drives binge
  - Consistent thumbnail template: Ep #, one word, Sai at whiteboard

### Series C — *Office Hours* (Bi-weekly/monthly, 10–25 min)

- **Script:**
  - 0:00–0:20 — "I pulled 5 questions this week. Let's go."
  - 0:20–2:00 — Q1 answered in depth (2–3 min each, not surface-level)
  - 2:00–4:00 — Q2
  - 4:00–6:00 — Q3
  - 6:00–8:00 — Q4
  - 8:00–10:00 — Q5 + "DM me for next round" CTA
- **Shots:**
  - Locked-off Sai single-camera, chest-up
  - On-screen question cards between answers
  - Occasional cutaway B-roll only if the answer references specific work
- **Setting:** One location, one sitting — can be office, can be home — as long as it's consistent across episodes
- **Editing:**
  - Question as big text overlay before each answer
  - Cut filler words tight but keep natural pauses
  - No music under Sai speaking — music only in transitions between Qs
  - Live version: post full stream, then edit to 10–12 min highlight cut for main channel

### Series D — *What I'd Do Differently* (Retrospective, 8–12 min)

- **Script:**
  - 0:00–0:20 — Cold open: "The decision that cost me [X]."
  - 0:20–2:00 — What actually happened (the original story)
  - 2:00–6:00 — What I know now that I didn't know then
  - 6:00–9:00 — What I'd do instead, step by step
  - 9:00–end — The broader lesson + ask the audience for their version
- **Shots:**
  - Sai mostly talking-head, more intimate than the main series
  - Archival footage / screenshots from the actual event when possible
  - Re-enactment B-roll if nothing archival exists (abstract: hands, laptop, street)
- **Setting:** Intimate setup — could be home office, one quiet corner of Trendify office, or a cafe
- **Editing:**
  - Slower pace than other series — let moments breathe
  - Desaturated archival footage vs. full color present-day
  - Music: slightly more melancholy lo-fi than main series
  - No big graphics — this series is about reflection, not production
  - Pin one comment asking viewers: "What would you do differently?"

## SHORT-FORM FORMATS

### Format 1 — Hot Take (Counterintuitive)

- **Script (30–60s):**
  - Hook: "Everyone says [X]. They're wrong."
  - Why: one specific reason
  - Proof: one example from Trendify / BuiltGen
  - Landing: the actual truth, one sentence
- **Shots:** face-cam, chest-up, one angle, no cutaways
- **Setting:** office corner with clean background OR a NYC street for a more "walking authority" feel
- **Editing:**
  - Tight — cut every breath gap
  - Captions full-screen, high-contrast
  - Zero music OR one low ambient bed
  - Text emphasis flashes on the counterintuitive word

### Format 2 — BTS of an Ad

- **Script (45–75s):**
  - Hook: "Here's how we made [client] go viral."
  - Beat 1: the brief / constraint
  - Beat 2: the creative call we made (the one that changed everything)
  - Beat 3: the result (real numbers)
- **Shots:**
  - Team filming the ad (from the side, not participating)
  - Monitor showing the final ad cut
  - Sai reviewing footage at desk
  - Close-ups: camera buttons, lighting being adjusted, slate
- **Setting:** Active set wherever Trendify is filming that week — client's office, studio, or on location
- **Editing:**
  - Split-screen: behind-the-scenes vs. final ad
  - Snappy cuts between "before" and "after" decisions
  - Sai VO carries the narrative — his face only when he's making a point
  - End on the ad's real metric overlay (views / CTR / $ spent)

### Format 3 — "21 and…"

- **Script (20–40s):**
  - Hook: "21 and [specific milestone]."
  - One sentence of context
  - One visual proof beat
  - Landing line
- **Shots:**
  - Sai mid-action — walking into office, reviewing contract, in a meeting
  - One establishing cutaway (office sign, NYC street)
- **Setting:** Wherever Sai is when the milestone happens — captured live, not staged
- **Editing:**
  - Minimal cuts (3–5 total)
  - Age-number appears in big text overlay
  - No music or lo-fi bed
  - Caption is the hook — reinforces on mute

### Format 4 — Day X of Building Trendify

- **Script (45–75s):**
  - Hook: "Day [N] of building Trendify. Today [one thing]."
  - The one thing explained in 2–3 beats
  - What it means for what's next
- **Shots:**
  - Whatever the "one thing" was — capture it in the moment
  - One transition shot (Sai walking, arriving, leaving)
  - Day-number card on screen
- **Setting:** Wherever the day happened — flexible by design
- **Editing:**
  - Day number always same font/position (series consistency)
  - Date stamp in corner (like a vlog)
  - Natural audio over music — hear the environment
  - End card: "Day [N+1] tomorrow"

### Format 5 — Response to Creator

- **Script (45–75s):**
  - Hook: "[Creator] said [X]. Here's what they're missing."
  - Show their clip (10–15s max)
  - Sai's take in 2–3 beats
  - The nuance or counter
- **Shots:**
  - Screen-recorded clip of the original post (credit visible)
  - Sai face-cam reacting
  - One cutaway to Sai in motion if the response is longer
- **Setting:** Office desk with phone/laptop visible OR clean neutral background
- **Editing:**
  - Duet/stitch-style split on TikTok, picture-in-picture on IG/YT
  - Clip's audio ducks when Sai speaks
  - Tag the creator in caption
  - Use "Response" or their name in the hook text overlay

### Format 6 — Client Win / Number Reveal

- **Script (30–45s):**
  - Hook: "We just hit [specific number / milestone]. Here's the story."
  - The quick backstory (1 beat)
  - The turning point (1 beat)
  - The result (specific, with number)
- **Shots:**
  - Screen capture of the win (dashboard, analytics, Slack announcement)
  - Sai reacting in the moment (don't re-stage)
  - One team shot — high-fives, handshake, room energy
- **Setting:** Trendify office, live when it happens
- **Editing:**
  - Number appears giant in center, then shrinks into corner
  - Celebratory nat sound > music
  - Caption repeats the specific number
  - End: "→ next goal: [specific]" — keeps momentum

### Format 7 — The Meeting Clip (Raw Access)

- **Script (20–60s):**
  - No hook — open mid-moment
  - Context overlay appears 2s in: "Trendify pitch meeting, [Client]"
  - Let the moment play
  - Cut on a clean beat (not mid-word)
- **Shots:**
  - Camera in the corner of the meeting room, locked off
  - Secondary phone shot for reaction coverage if possible
- **Setting:** Real Trendify meeting — requires client permission for anything identifiable
- **Editing:**
  - Minimal cuts — let it breathe
  - No music
  - Captions to clarify mumbled lines only
  - Blur/bleep anything under NDA

### Format 8 — Brother Banter

- **Script (30–60s):**
  - Sai and Srikar in one shot
  - One brother starts a claim / question
  - The other contradicts or finishes the thought
  - Natural escalation → landing line
- **Shots:**
  - 2-shot wide (both in frame, equal weight)
  - Alternating tight shots on each brother's reactions
  - One "walking together" variation for variety
- **Setting:** Office, NYC street, cafe — low-stakes environments where chemistry lands
- **Editing:**
  - Keep cuts conversational — don't over-edit
  - Split-screen for disagreements
  - Natural laughter stays in
  - Caption: "Srikar (CEO) vs. Sai (COO)" or similar for viewers who don't know them
  - Tag both in every post

### Format 9 — Lessons From BuiltGen

- **Script (30–60s):**
  - Hook: "At BuiltGen we [made this mistake / figured out this thing]."
  - What happened in 2 beats
  - The lesson applied now at Trendify
- **Shots:**
  - Sai face-cam OR walk-and-talk
  - Archival: old BuiltGen site, old emails, old screenshots (redacted)
- **Setting:** Flexible — office, walking, or at a whiteboard depending on how visual the lesson is
- **Editing:**
  - "BuiltGen, [year]" lower-third when referencing the old agency
  - Split-screen: BuiltGen era (desaturated) vs. Trendify now (full color)
  - Evergreen — can be re-posted across months
  - Caption: the lesson itself, not the hook

### Format 10 — Letter to My 15-Year-Old Self

- **Script (30–45s):**
  - Hook: "If I could go back to 15, here's what I'd tell myself about [topic]."
  - One specific lesson
  - One specific memory from that age
  - Landing: what it means now
- **Shots:**
  - Sai face-cam, slightly more intimate framing (tighter, softer light)
  - One still image of 15-year-old Sai (if available) briefly on screen
- **Setting:** Quieter space — home, cafe corner, not a busy office
- **Editing:**
  - Slower pace than other formats
  - Softer lo-fi music bed
  - Age overlay "15 → 21" visual beat
  - Text caption in handwritten-style font for "letter" feel
  - One episode per topic — first clients, money, partners, failure, scaling, family

### Format 11 — Ask Sai Anything

- **Script (30–60s):**
  - Hook: "You asked: [question]. Here's the truth."
  - The question appears on screen as a comment/DM screenshot
  - Sai answers in 2–3 beats
  - CTA: "Drop your next one in the comments"
- **Shots:**
  - Face-cam single angle
  - Screenshot of the actual comment/DM as the opener
- **Setting:** Same consistent spot every time — builds series recognition
- **Editing:**
  - Open with question on screen for 2s before Sai speaks
  - Pin the original comment if it's a reply
  - Reply directly as a video on TikTok (boosts algo)
  - Number each episode ("AMA #14") for seriality

### Format 12 — Things I Stopped Doing After [X]

- **Script (30–45s):**
  - Hook: "Things I stopped doing after [specific milestone]."
  - List 3 things, one beat each
  - Closing: the one thing that replaced all three
- **Shots:**
  - Face-cam
  - One b-roll per item (typing, phone call, meeting — literal or metaphorical)
- **Setting:** Office or clean neutral background
- **Editing:**
  - Numbered list on screen (1, 2, 3) — big text
  - Sharp cuts between items — no filler
  - Negative framing in caption ("Stop doing this")
  - One milestone per video — reusable template

### Format 13 — How I Built My Client List

- **Script (45–75s):**
  - Hook: "Here's exactly how I got [client] to say yes."
  - The pitch angle or moment
  - The objection
  - The close
- **Shots:**
  - Face-cam
  - Screen recording of the actual email/Slack/DM if shareable (redacted)
  - Cutaway to Sai on the phone or at a desk
- **Setting:** Office, desk-level POV
- **Editing:**
  - Text overlay of the actual pitch line or subject line
  - Tight cuts — no throat clearing, no setup
  - Save-bait: people screenshot the specific line, so make it prominent
  - Caption: "The exact line that closed [client]"

### Format 14 — Srikar Says vs. Sai Says

- **Script (30–60s):**
  - Same question asked to both
  - Srikar's answer first, Sai's second (or vice versa — alternate)
  - One sentence of gentle disagreement or "same answer, different reasons" landing
- **Shots:**
  - Each brother shot in identical framing (same height, same light, same background)
  - Cut between them — never both in frame here (visual contrast is the point)
- **Setting:** Two stations in the office, filmed back-to-back
- **Editing:**
  - Split-screen or back-to-back — lock the template
  - Name + role lower-third: "Srikar / CEO" — "Sai / COO"
  - Cut fast — the whole point is the contrast
  - Caption: the question itself

### Format 15 — The Big Money Mistake (Back Pocket)

- **Script (30–60s):**
  - Hook: "I lost $[X] because of [one sentence]."
  - What happened in 2 beats
  - The mistake named directly
  - What I do now instead
- **Shots:**
  - Face-cam, tight framing
  - One somber-tone cutaway (dark office, rain on window if available)
- **Setting:** Quieter — home or after-hours office
- **Editing:**
  - Slower pace
  - Dollar amount big on screen — shock value
  - Minimal music, almost ambient
  - Use sparingly — 1 per month max or it loses weight
  - Run by Sai before posting — vulnerability-heavy

### Format 16 — Lessons From My Mentors

- **Script (30–45s):**
  - Hook: "The best advice I got was from [mentor name or role]."
  - One specific piece of advice
  - One specific moment it was given
  - How Sai applies it at Trendify now
- **Shots:**
  - Face-cam
  - One still or clip of the mentor if public/shareable, with credit
- **Setting:** Office or walking
- **Editing:**
  - Quote appears on screen as Sai says it — big text
  - Mentor's name as lower-third
  - Tag the mentor in the caption if appropriate
  - Build a playlist: "Lessons from my mentors"

### Format 17 — The Whiteboard

- **Script (45–60s):**
  - Hook: "Let me explain [concept] in 60 seconds."
  - Concept broken into 3 visual steps
  - One concrete Trendify example
  - Closing frame: the full whiteboard
- **Shots:**
  - Camera angled slightly from the side (not dead-on)
  - Insert: tight on marker hitting board
  - One wide at the end showing full whiteboard
- **Setting:** Whiteboard room at Trendify — same one every time (series consistency)
- **Editing:**
  - Speed-ramped drawing beats — fast sketching, slow landing lines
  - Text callouts animate in alongside the drawing
  - Clean sans-serif for digital overlays
  - Batch-shoot 5–10 of these in one afternoon — highest efficiency format
  - End: "Save this one" on screen — save-rate optimized

## Filming Cadence Reminder

- **Daily shorts:** 1 format per day, Mon–Fri. See Week 1 table in the main strategy doc.
- **Weekly long-form:** 1 episode per week.
- **Batching priorities:** Format 17 (Whiteboard), Format 10 (Letter to 15), Format 11 (AMA) — all one-location, one-camera. Shoot 5+ in one session.
- **Client-dependent:** Format 2 (BTS), Format 7 (Meeting Clip), Format 6 (Client Win) — require permission. Start with public clients (YC, Mindvalley, Justin Kan, Checkmate).
- **Srikar-dependent:** Format 8 (Brother Banter), Format 14 (Srikar Says vs. Sai Says), Episode 1 duo thumbnail. Confirm Srikar's availability in the Mon debrief.

## Visual Style Guardrails (applies to all)

- **Color grade:** light, documentary, NOT teal-and-orange
- **Pace:** cut on motivation, not on beat
- **Music:** lo-fi / chill electronic, drops out on emotional beats
- **Overlays:** small, sans-serif, consistent font across the series
- **Anti-pattern:** no whoosh transitions, no YouTube-intro energy, no motivational swells, no suit+crossed-arms thumbnails
